# Reports

Qumis reports transform complex insurance documents into clear, actionable analysis. Every report uses attorney-trained AI with multi-agent workflows — specialized agents read, analyze, validate, and cross-reference your documents to produce output that reads like an experienced coverage attorney's work product.

---

## How reports work

Every report follows the same core workflow:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload documents">
    Add policies, contracts, claims documentation, or other insurance files — either by uploading directly or pulling from your Vault.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add context">
    Provide optional guidance in the Additional Information field to focus the analysis on your priorities. The more context you give, the more relevant the output.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate">
    Qumis analyzes your documents using multi-agent AI workflows. This takes 5–10 minutes for a comprehensive analysis that would traditionally take 5–9 hours.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and explore">
    Read the structured findings — executive summary, detailed analysis, and citations to specific policy language. Use **Qumis Clerk** (the chat within the report) to ask follow-up questions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download and share">
    Export as PDF or Excel for client presentations, internal documentation, or proposals. The output is formatted for professional delivery.
  </Step>
</Steps>

All reports are automatically saved to your dashboard. Documents pulled from Vault remain linked — no re-uploading needed.

## Beyond reports: Populate and Verify

Reports aren't the only output Qumis produces. Two additional capabilities round out the reporting workflow:

### Populate — Turn policies into spreadsheets

Extract structured data from insurance documents into spreadsheet format. Upload a policy and a client template, and Qumis populates it with the relevant data — limits, deductibles, coverage forms, and terms.

→ [Data Extraction overview](/report/populate/data-extraction) · [Client Template Guide](/report/populate/client-template-guide)

### Verify — Check compliance in seconds

Systematically check whether an insurance program meets contractual, regulatory, or internal requirements. Qumis reads both the requirements document and the policy, then produces a gap analysis showing what's met and what's missing.

→ [Contract Compliance](/report/verify/contract-compliance) · [Coverage Verification](/report/verify/coverage-verification)

## Key features across all reports

**Attorney-trained AI.** Qumis interprets policy language the way legal experts do — understanding context, implications, and strategic leverage rather than just extracting text.

**Multi-agent validation.** Specialized AI agents read, validate, and cross-reference documents to catch nuances that a single-pass analysis might miss.

**Interactive chat.** Every report includes Qumis Clerk — an AI assistant that answers follow-up questions, explains findings, and explores coverage scenarios. All conversations are saved.

**Citations and transparency.** Reports reference specific policy sections, clauses, and provisions so you can verify every finding against the original document.

**Professional formatting.** Output is ready for client presentations and proposals without reformatting.

## Best practices

**Choose the right report type.** Each is optimized for a specific workflow — Comparison for competitive quoting, Claims Analysis for coverage questions, Contract Review for compliance. Using the right type gets you better results than a Custom Report for common scenarios.

**Add strategic context.** The Additional Information field is your chance to focus the analysis. Mention the client's industry, key exposures, and what you're trying to accomplish. A report with context is significantly more useful than one without.

**Use Qumis Clerk after every report.** The chat within the report is where you go deeper: "Explain this exclusion in plain language," "Which policy has the strongest additional insured coverage?", "Draft talking points for the client meeting."

**Download for professional delivery.** Export as PDF for client presentations. The formatting is designed to demonstrate analytical rigor — use it to show clients the depth of your review.
