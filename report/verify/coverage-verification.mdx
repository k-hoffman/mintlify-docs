# Coverage Verification

Coverage verification is the counterpart to contract compliance. While contract compliance checks whether insurance meets external requirements, coverage verification asks a different question: does this policy actually cover what the insured thinks it covers?

---

## When to verify coverage

**Client expectations vs. reality.** A client believes they have comprehensive cyber coverage. Does the policy actually cover social engineering fraud, ransomware payments, regulatory fines, and business interruption from a cyber event?

**Coverage adequacy reviews.** Does the coverage program address the insured's actual risk exposures? Are limits adequate? Are there gaps between what the business needs and what the policy provides?

**Post-loss checks.** After a loss occurs, verify coverage before submitting a claim. Does the policy respond to these specific facts?

**Program design validation.** After placing a new program, verify that the coverage as written matches what was intended and proposed.

---

## How coverage verification works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define what you're verifying">
    Specify the coverage questions. These might come from a client conversation, a risk assessment, or a specific scenario you need to check. Examples: "Does this policy cover cyber extortion?" or "Would a claim for faulty workmanship by a subcontractor be covered?"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload the relevant policies">
    Upload the policies, endorsements, and any other documents that make up the coverage program.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the verification">
    Qumis analyzes the policy provisions against your verification questions. For each question, it identifies the relevant coverage grant, applicable exclusions, any conditions, and provides a clear finding with citations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and document">
    Review the verification results. Each finding includes specific policy citations so you can verify the analysis and share documented results with clients or colleagues.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Verification checklist by coverage line

Qumis can verify coverage against standard checklists for common lines of business:

**Commercial General Liability:** Products-completed operations, additional insured grants, contractual liability, professional services exclusion, abuse/molestation exclusion, pollution exclusion scope, damage to your work.

**Professional Liability:** Prior acts coverage, defense cost treatment (inside/outside limits), discovery period provisions, definition of professional services, consent to settle clause.

**Cyber Liability:** First-party vs. third-party coverage, ransomware/extortion, business interruption, regulatory proceedings, social engineering fraud, dependent business interruption.

**Property:** Covered perils, business income and extra expense, ordinance or law, flood and earthquake sublimits, equipment breakdown, contingent business income.

**Directors & Officers:** Side A/B/C coverage allocation, insured vs. insured exclusion, prior acts, regulatory proceedings, entity coverage scope.

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    **Pre-placement verification:** After designing a coverage program, verify that the proposed policies actually deliver the coverage you intend. Catch gaps before binding — not after a claim.

    **Client education:** Use verification results to walk clients through exactly what their policies cover and where gaps exist. This builds trust and demonstrates your value.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    **Annual coverage reviews:** Use the verification checklist to systematically review each policy in a client's program. Document your findings for the coverage review letter.

    **Gap identification:** Run targeted verifications when clients face new risks — entering a new market, signing a new contract, or launching a new product. Identify whether existing coverage extends to the new exposure.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    **Pre-claim verification:** When an insured reports a potential claim, verify coverage before formal submission. This sets realistic expectations and identifies potential coverage issues early.

    **Multi-policy verification:** For claims that might trigger multiple policies, verify coverage across the entire program to identify the most favorable path.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    **Exposure mapping:** Map your organization's top risk exposures against your insurance program. Verify that each significant exposure has corresponding coverage.

    **Program benchmarking:** Verify your coverage against industry standards and best practices for your sector. Identify areas where you may be underinsured relative to peers.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
