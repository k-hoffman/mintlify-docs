# Contract Compliance (Verify)

Verify automates the tedious but critical work of checking whether insurance coverage meets contractual requirements. Upload a contract and the relevant insurance documents, and Qumis systematically checks every insurance requirement against actual coverage — flagging gaps, confirming compliance, and producing a documented audit trail.

---

## How contract compliance verification works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload the contract">
    Upload the contract, lease, loan agreement, or other document that specifies insurance requirements. Qumis identifies every insurance requirement in the document — minimum limits, required coverage types, additional insured provisions, waiver of subrogation, and notice requirements.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload the insurance documents">
    Upload the relevant policies, certificates, or endorsements that are supposed to satisfy the requirements.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the verification">
    Qumis checks each contractual requirement against the actual insurance documents. For each requirement, it determines: met, not met, or partially met — with specific citations explaining the finding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the compliance report">
    The output is a structured compliance report showing each requirement, the verification result, and the specific policy provisions that satisfy (or fail to satisfy) the requirement.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Qumis checks

| Requirement Type | What Qumis Verifies |
|---|---|
| **Minimum limits** | Are per-occurrence and aggregate limits at least as high as required? |
| **Required coverage types** | Does the program include each required line (CGL, auto, umbrella, workers' comp, professional, cyber, etc.)? |
| **Additional insured** | Is the contracting party named as an additional insured on the relevant policies? |
| **Waiver of subrogation** | Does the policy include a waiver of subrogation endorsement in favor of the contracting party? |
| **Primary and non-contributory** | Is the required coverage primary and non-contributory with respect to the contracting party's own insurance? |
| **Notice requirements** | Do the policy's cancellation notice provisions meet the contract's requirements? |
| **Deductible limits** | Are deductibles and SIRs within the maximum allowed by the contract? |

## Common compliance scenarios

**Commercial leases.** Landlords require tenants to carry specific coverages. Verify checks the tenant's insurance program against every requirement in the lease.

**Vendor and subcontractor agreements.** Before onboarding a vendor or subcontractor, verify that their insurance certificates meet your contractual requirements.

**Loan covenants.** Lenders often specify minimum insurance requirements. Verify ensures your coverage satisfies every covenant.

**Construction contracts.** Construction contracts typically have the most detailed insurance requirements. Verify handles the complexity of checking multiple coverage lines, limits stacking, and endorsement requirements.

---

## Batch verification

For organizations managing many contracts — property managers with dozens of leases, general contractors with multiple subcontractors, companies with extensive vendor networks — Verify can process multiple contracts and certificates in sequence, producing a compliance summary across your entire portfolio.

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    **New business support:** When a prospect has contractual insurance requirements, use Verify to show them exactly where their current program falls short — and how your proposed program closes those gaps.

    **Renewal value-add:** At renewal, Verify every client contract against the new policy to confirm continued compliance.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    **Proactive compliance monitoring:** Run Verify at policy inception and at every renewal to catch compliance gaps before they become problems.

    **Client advisory:** When a client signs a new contract, Verify the insurance requirements immediately and flag any gaps that need to be addressed before the contract takes effect.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    **Additional insured verification:** When a claim involves additional insured status, use Verify to confirm whether the proper endorsements are in place.

    **Contractual liability analysis:** Verify can identify whether the policy's contractual liability provisions align with the contract's indemnification requirements.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    **Enterprise compliance:** This is the primary Verify workflow for risk managers. Run systematic compliance checks across every contract in your portfolio. This is the recommended pilot workflow in the Ascend™ implementation framework.

    **Vendor management:** Verify every vendor's insurance certificates against your standard contractual requirements. Flag non-compliant vendors for follow-up before they begin work.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
