## What Client Templates Are

Client templates are custom extraction configurations that map policy data from insurance documents to your existing spreadsheet formats, templates, and deliverable structures. Instead of manually copying data from policies into your standard templates, Qumis learns your template structure and automatically extracts the right information into the right cells.

Templates maintain consistency, reduce manual data entry time, and ensure your entire team produces standardized deliverables.

## Why Use Client Templates

**Maintain Existing Formats**: Keep using your firm's established spreadsheet templates and deliverable formats. Qumis adapts to your systems rather than forcing you to change yours.

**Ensure Team Consistency**: Standardize how information is extracted and presented across your organization. Everyone on your team produces consistent, accurate deliverables.

**Speed Up Repetitive Work**: Automate data entry for routine tasks like renewal summaries, proposal schedules, and coverage matrices. Extract data once; use it multiple times.

**Client-Specific Customization**: Create different templates for different clients, matching their exact requirements and preferences while keeping your process consistent.

## How to Create a Client Template

<Steps>
<Step title="Start from an Existing Spreadsheet or Build from Scratch">
Either upload your current client spreadsheet or create a new template structure. Define the columns, rows, and sections you need.
</Step>

<Step title="Define Columns and Fields to Extract">
Specify which data fields you want Qumis to populate:
- Policy numbers and effective dates
- Coverage types and limits
- Deductibles and premium amounts
- Carrier information
- Endorsements and special provisions
- Certificate requirements
- Any other custom fields your template includes
</Step>

<Step title="Map Fields to Policy Document Sections">
Train Qumis on where to find each piece of information. This typically requires:
- Identifying the field in your template
- Showing Qumis where that information appears in a sample policy document
- Confirming the mapping is accurate
</Step>

<Step title="Save as Reusable Template">
Save your configured template with a clear name and description. Version control your templates as you refine them based on results.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Field Types

Client templates support multiple data field types:

**Text Fields**: Policy numbers, carrier names, agent information, special provisions, and any non-numeric text data.

**Numeric Fields**: Coverage limits, deductibles, premium amounts, and other quantitative data. Qumis extracts and formats numbers consistently.

**Date Fields**: Policy effective dates, expiration dates, renewal dates, and other temporal information. Qumis normalizes dates to your preferred format.

**Coverage Detail Fields**: Structured data from coverage sections, including coverage type, sublimit, condition, and attachment points.

**Limit/Deductible Fields**: Aggregate information about coverage levels, organized by coverage type, with appropriate formatting.

## Template Management

### Saving Templates
Save successful template configurations with descriptive names. Include notes about the template's intended use and any special considerations.

### Sharing with Team
Share templates across your team through the Qumis platform. Control access and ensure everyone uses current versions.

### Versioning
Keep previous template versions for reference and historical consistency. Maintain a change log when templates are updated.

### Organizing by Client or Use Case
Organize your template library by:
- Client name or account
- Use case (renewal summaries, proposals, claims analysis)
- Industry or coverage type
- Report frequency (annual, quarterly, ad hoc)

## Using Templates

1. **Select Template**: Choose from your saved templates that matches your current task
2. **Upload Policies**: Add the policy documents you want to extract data from
3. **Qumis Populates**: The system automatically extracts data and fills your template
4. **Review and Export**: Verify accuracy, make any manual adjustments needed, and export as Excel, PDF, or your preferred format

<Info>
Client templates work with the Populate capability. Upload your existing Excel template and Qumis will learn the structure to auto-populate it from policy documents. This eliminates manual data entry while maintaining your firm's standard formats.
</Info>

## Advanced Features

### Conditional Fields
Create fields that appear or are required based on other data:
- Only populate deductible information if the coverage type is included
- Include additional fields for umbrella/excess policies
- Show specific endorsement details based on policy type

### Calculated Values
Templates can include computed fields that derive values from extracted data:
- Total insured value across multiple policies
- Aggregate limits by coverage type
- Premium calculations or cost allocations

### Multi-Policy Extraction
Extract data from multiple policies into a single template or across multiple rows. Useful for:
- Building comprehensive coverage schedules across an entire program
- Creating portfolio summaries combining multiple insureds
- Comparing coverage across renewal quotes

## Best Practices

**Start Simple**: Begin with core fields that you know Qumis can reliably extract. Build complexity over time as you validate accuracy.

**Validate Against Manual Extraction**: For the first few uses, compare Qumis-extracted data against manually verified information to ensure accuracy.

**Iterate and Refine**: As you use templates, refine field definitions and mappings based on real results. Successful templates improve with use.

**Document Template Purpose**: Include clear documentation in template settings describing:
- What the template is for
- Which clients or use cases it's designed for
- Any special handling or formatting requirements
- Known limitations or edge cases

**Regular Audit**: Periodically review template outputs to catch any drift or changes in document formats that might affect extraction accuracy.

**Leverage Cross-Team Feedback**: Encourage team members using templates to suggest refinements and improvements based on their experience.
