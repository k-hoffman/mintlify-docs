# Choosing the Right Report

Not sure which report to run? Start with your situation, and we'll point you to the right one.

---

## What's your situation?

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="I have competing quotes to evaluate" icon="files" href="/report/types/comparison-report">
    **→ Comparison Report**

    Best for: competitive quoting, carrier selection, quote analysis. Upload two or more quotes, proposals, or specimen policies and get a side-by-side breakdown of coverage differences, QumisScore benchmarking, and strategic leverage points.
  </Card>
  <Card title="I need to review a single policy" icon="file-search" href="/report/types/single-policy-analysis">
    **→ Single Policy Analysis**

    Best for: prospect policy review, coverage audits, gap identification. Upload one policy and get a comprehensive analysis covering coverage grants, exclusions, sublimits, conditions, and improvement opportunities.
  </Card>
  <Card title="I need to determine if a claim is covered" icon="shield-check" href="/report/types/claims-analysis">
    **→ Claims Analysis**

    Best for: coverage determination, claim evaluation, denial support. Upload the policy and claim documentation, describe the loss, and get a structured coverage opinion with legal reasoning and cited policy language.
  </Card>
  <Card title="I need to check contract compliance" icon="badge-check" href="/report/types/contract-review">
    **→ Contract Review**

    Best for: lease compliance, vendor agreements, loan covenants. Upload the contract and the policy, and get a requirement-by-requirement compliance table showing what's met, what's missing, and what needs to change.
  </Card>
  <Card title="I need a clean coverage summary for a proposal" icon="table" href="/report/types/coverage-table">
    **→ Coverage Table**

    Best for: proposal preparation, renewal summaries, board presentations. Produces a professional side-by-side table of limits, deductibles, and key terms across one or more policies — the format CFOs and risk managers expect.
  </Card>
  <Card title="I need something specific or custom" icon="wand-magic-sparkles" href="/report/types/custom-report">
    **→ Custom Report**

    Best for: unique analysis needs, specialized deliverables, non-standard workflows. Full control over scope, format, and focus areas. Use when the standard report types don't fit your exact situation.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Common multi-report workflows

Some situations call for more than one report type. Here are the combinations experienced users rely on:

### Renewal preparation
1. **Comparison Report** — expiring vs. renewal to identify all changes
2. **Coverage Table** — clean summary for the client meeting
3. **Contract Review** — verify the renewed program still meets key contracts

### New business pursuit
1. **Single Policy Analysis** — review the prospect's incumbent coverage
2. **Comparison Report** — your proposed program vs. the incumbent
3. **Coverage Table** — formatted proposal for the prospect

### Claims evaluation
1. **Claims Analysis** — initial coverage determination
2. Use **Legal Search** (in the [Research tab](/research/legal-search)) to support the determination with case law

### Portfolio review
1. **Single Policy Analysis** — run across multiple lines in the program
2. **Coverage Table** — consolidated view of limits and terms
3. **Contract Review** — check the program against the top 3 contracts

## Tips for better reports

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    When running Comparison Reports for competitive quoting, always include context about the client's industry and key exposures. A comparison focused on "manufacturing client with product liability concerns" produces materially different and more useful output than one with no context.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    For renewal prep, run the Comparison Report first (expiring vs. renewal), then ask Qumis Clerk: "Summarize the three most important changes the client needs to know about." Use that summary as your talking points.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    In Claims Analysis, the quality of the coverage opinion depends heavily on how well you describe the claim. Include: the type of loss, the parties involved, the alleged cause, and the specific coverage question. Don't make the AI guess.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    Use Contract Review as a systematic tool: run it against your top 5 contracts every renewal to ensure your updated program still meets requirements. Save the reports as compliance documentation.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
