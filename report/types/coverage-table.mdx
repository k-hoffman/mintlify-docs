A coverage table report transforms your insurance policies into a structured, easy-to-read matrix format. This guide walks you through generating, customizing, and exporting a coverage table for renewal planning, client presentations, program documentation, or claims coordination.

## What the Report Produces

A coverage table report includes:

- **Coverage matrix** - Structured table showing coverage lines and key terms across policies
- **Summary statistics** - Total limits, deductible summary, coverage count
- **Gap analysis section** - Identifies missing coverage lines and potential gaps
- **Endorsement summary** - Lists key endorsements and special conditions
- **Expiration tracking** - Shows policy expiration dates and renewal scheduling
- **Custom fields** - Additional data columns you define (e.g., renewal broker, cost center, risk classification)
- **Notes and flagging** - Annotate important items or flag coverage for attention

The report is formatted for professional presentation and easily exportable to Excel, PDF, or other formats.

## How to Generate a Coverage Table Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Policies">
    Select the insurance policies you want to include in the coverage table. You can:
    - Include all policies in your program
    - Focus on a specific coverage area (e.g., all liability policies)
    - Create separate tables for different business units or locations
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Coverage Lines to Include">
    Select which coverage lines to include in your table:
    - General Liability
    - Products Liability
    - Property
    - Commercial Auto
    - Professional Liability
    - Umbrella/Excess Liability
    - Workers Compensation
    - Employment Practices Liability
    - Directors & Officers Liability
    - Cyber Liability
    - Other specialty coverages
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Detail Level">
    Choose how detailed your table should be:
    - **Summary** - Basic information only (carrier, limits, deductible, expiration)
    - **Standard** - Includes endorsements, special conditions, key terms
    - **Detailed** - Full details including aggregate limits, SIRs, territory restrictions, form numbers
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Data Fields">
    Choose which columns to include:
    - Carrier
    - Policy number
    - Policy period
    - Limits (per occurrence / aggregate)
    - Deductible
    - Self-Insured Retention
    - Coverage territory
    - Key endorsements
    - Special conditions
    - Cost/premium
    - Renewal broker
    - Notes
    - Custom fields
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the Report">
    Click Generate to create your coverage table. Qumis extracts key information from your policies and organizes it in a structured matrix.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and Customize">
    Review the generated table. You can:
    - Add or remove columns
    - Adjust data values (correct values extracted incorrectly)
    - Add notes or flags to specific coverage lines
    - Add rows for gaps (missing coverage you need to address)
    - Group by coverage type, carrier, or business unit
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export and Share">
    Export your table in your preferred format (Excel, PDF, formatted table) and share with stakeholders.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Report Output Format

A coverage table report is organized as a structured matrix:

### Main Coverage Table
Rows: Coverage lines (General Liability, Property, etc.)
Columns: Data fields you've selected (Carrier, Limits, Deductible, etc.)

Example structure:
| Coverage Line | Carrier | Limits | Deductible | Territory | Expiration |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| General Liability | Carrier A | $1M/$2M | $5K | US & Canada | 12/31/25 |
| Property | Carrier B | $5M | $50K | US | 06/30/26 |

### Summary Section
- Total limits across program
- Deductible summary (total out-of-pocket exposure)
- Coverage count and status
- Expiration timeline

### Gap Analysis Section
- Missing coverage lines (identified based on your program needs)
- Limits below recommended levels
- Geographic gaps
- Endorsement gaps

### Endorsement Summary
- Key endorsements by coverage line
- Special conditions or modifications
- Notable exclusions

### Expiration Tracking
- Policies by renewal date
- Aging summary (how old each policy is)
- Renewal coordination needs

## Configuration Options

### Detail Level
- **Summary** - Minimal data for high-level overview
- **Standard** - Balanced detail for most use cases
- **Detailed** - Comprehensive information for technical review

### Data Fields
Include or exclude:
- Coverage line/type
- Carrier name
- Policy number
- Policy period (effective/expiration dates)
- Limits of liability (per occurrence, aggregate)
- Deductibles
- Self-insured retentions
- Coverage territory
- Key endorsements
- Special conditions
- Premium/cost
- Renewal broker
- Custom notes
- Flags or ratings

### Organization
Organize the table by:
- **Coverage type** - Group all liability together, all property together (standard approach)
- **Carrier** - One carrier per column
- **Business unit** - One location/subsidiary per column
- **Expiration date** - Organized by renewal timing
- **Custom grouping** - Based on your organization structure

### Custom Columns
You can add custom columns for:
- Cost/premium tracking
- Renewal broker or contact
- Risk classification
- Business unit or cost center
- Internal rating or status
- Notes or follow-up items
- Compliance status

## Best Practices

<Tip>
Generate coverage tables annually and update them as new policies are issued. This keeps your program documentation current and makes renewal coordination much easier.
</Tip>

- **Keep current** - Update tables as policies are renewed or changed
- **Document gaps** - Flag any gaps and track actions to address them
- **Use for renewal** - Share with your broker as input to renewal planning
- **Track changes** - Compare year-over-year to see program evolution
- **Organize logically** - Group coverage in a way that makes sense for your organization
- **Include context** - Add notes explaining unusual terms or coverage choices
- **Enable quick reference** - Organize to support claims coordination and quick lookups

## Multiple Location/Unit Comparisons

If managing coverage across multiple locations, subsidiaries, or business units:

1. You can create a single master table with each location as a column
2. Or create separate tables for each location/unit
3. Gap analysis becomes more powerful—identifies inconsistencies across the program
4. Helps verify compliance with your program requirements
5. Supports standardization discussions with brokers and carriers

This is useful for:
- Multi-location companies comparing coverage consistency
- Subsidiary/parent company program oversight
- Franchise or distributed operations management
- Consolidating coverage post-acquisition

## Troubleshooting

If your table doesn't match expectations:

- **Missing data?** - Verify policies were uploaded and selected correctly
- **Data incorrect?** - Edit values manually or re-upload corrected policy documents
- **Wrong organization?** - Regroup the table by coverage type, carrier, or location
- **Too much detail?** - Regenerate with summary level or select fewer columns
- **Not enough detail?** - Regenerate with detailed level or add custom columns
- **Formatting issues?** - Export to a different format (Excel vs. PDF)
