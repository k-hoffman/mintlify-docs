A comparison report delivers a polished, professionally formatted document showing exactly how two or more insurance policies differ. This guide walks you through generating, configuring, and customizing a comparison report for client presentations, renewals, compliance verification, or your claims file.

## What the Report Produces

A comparison report includes:

- **Executive summary** - Quick overview of key differences and material changes
- **Change summary table** - One-page summary of what changed across documents
- **Detailed side-by-side comparison** - Section-by-section policy language comparison
- **Change indicators** - Visual markers (Added, Removed, Modified, Unchanged) for each section
- **Coverage gap analysis** - Provisions in one policy but missing in another
- **Premium and rating comparison** - Cost and rate differences if applicable
- **Material changes highlighted** - Focus on changes that impact coverage or cost
- **Recommendations** - Suggested actions based on differences identified

The report is formatted for professional presentation and exportable in multiple formats.

## How to Generate a Comparison Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Documents to Compare">
    Select the documents you want to compare. You can compare:
    - Two or more insurance quotes
    - A renewal proposal and expiring policy
    - Your policy and a third-party requirement (contract, lease, regulatory requirement)
    - Multiple policies across your program
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Comparison Type">
    Specify what you're comparing:
    - **Quote comparison** - Multiple carrier proposals
    - **Renewal comparison** - Renewal vs. expiring policy
    - **Requirement comparison** - Policy vs. contractual or regulatory requirement
    - **Program comparison** - Multiple policies for consistency and gaps
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Comparison Scope">
    Choose your comparison scope:
    - **Full document** - Compare entire policies section-by-section
    - **Specific sections** - Focus on particular coverages or provisions (e.g., General Liability, Exclusions)
    - **Endorsement focus** - Emphasize endorsement differences
    - **Financial terms only** - Limits, deductibles, premiums, and aggregates
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Change Sensitivity">
    Select how sensitive Qumis should be to differences:
    - **Material changes only** - Highlight significant differences that affect coverage or cost
    - **All differences** - Show every change including minor wording differences
    - **Custom threshold** - You define which differences matter (useful for detailed analysis)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the Report">
    Click Generate to create your comparison report. Qumis analyzes the documents and produces a structured side-by-side comparison.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and Customize">
    Review the generated report. You can:
    - Adjust change sensitivity (show more or fewer differences)
    - Add explanatory notes for specific changes
    - Highlight particular differences as critical
    - Add your analysis or recommendations
    - Adjust report sections
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export and Share">
    Export your report (PDF, Word, HTML) and share with clients, carriers, counsel, or include in your file.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Report Sections

The comparison report is organized as follows:

### Executive Summary
A one-page overview including:
- Document identification (policies being compared, dates, carriers)
- Comparison purpose (renewal changes, competitive analysis, compliance check)
- Key differences summary (3-5 most important changes)
- Material changes highlighted
- Recommendations for action

### Change Summary Table
A one-page table showing:
- Coverage section (Liability, Property, etc.)
- Change type (Added, Removed, Modified)
- Brief description of change
- Whether change is material
- Premium impact if applicable

### Detailed Comparison by Section
For each policy section:
- Section title (e.g., "General Liability Coverage")
- Side-by-side policy language from each document
- Change indicators (Added, Removed, Modified, Unchanged)
- Brief annotation explaining the change
- Impact assessment (coverage impact, financial impact, etc.)

### Coverage Gap Analysis
Highlights:
- Provisions that exist in one policy but not the other
- Whether gaps are material or coverage gaps
- Implications (coverage missing in one policy, over-insured in another, etc.)

### Premium and Pricing Comparison
If applicable:
- Premium for each policy
- Rate changes year-over-year
- Premium impact of specific endorsement or coverage changes
- Cost per unit analysis

### Material Changes Section
Focuses on changes that matter most:
- Exclusion changes (new exclusions, removed exclusions, modified language)
- Limit or deductible changes
- Condition modifications that affect claims handling
- Important endorsement changes

## Configuration Options

### Comparison Scope
- **Full document** - Analyze every section of each policy
- **Coverage-focused** - Emphasize coverage parts and insuring agreements, minimize conditions/definitions
- **Specific sections** - Focus on particular coverage parts (e.g., General Liability, Products Liability)
- **Endorsement-focused** - Emphasize endorsement differences
- **Financial terms** - Limits, deductibles, SIRs, aggregates, premiums, rates

### Change Sensitivity
- **Material only** - Show only changes that affect coverage or cost (good for executive summaries)
- **Standard** - Balanced sensitivity, shows meaningful differences (good for most use cases)
- **Detailed** - Shows all differences including minor wording variations (good for legal analysis)

### Output Format
- **PDF** - Professional format for sharing and filing
- **Word** - Editable format for adding commentary
- **HTML** - For web display or email sharing
- **Excel** - For comparative analysis or data review

### Custom Sections
Include or exclude:
- Executive summary
- Change summary table
- Detailed comparison
- Coverage gap analysis
- Premium comparison
- Material changes highlight
- Recommendations
- Appendices (full policy language, change definitions)

## Best Practices

<Tip>
Generate comparison reports early in your renewal cycle to understand changes, identify gaps, and guide your renewal strategy and negotiations with carriers.
</Tip>

- **Use as a negotiation tool** - Share specific differences with carriers to justify requests for coverage improvements or rate adjustments
- **Update regularly** - Regenerate comparisons as new quotes arrive or terms change
- **Customize for audience** - Executive summary for management, detailed comparison for technical review
- **Focus on material changes** - Most clients only care about coverage gaps and premium changes, not minor wording differences
- **Document your analysis** - Add notes explaining why certain differences matter for your situation
- **Coordinate with negotiations** - Use comparison insights to guide your conversations with carriers

## Multiple Document Comparisons

You can compare more than two documents. When comparing multiple policies:

1. Qumis analyzes all documents against a "baseline" (usually the first policy listed)
2. Results show differences across all documents simultaneously
3. Gap analysis becomes more valuable (identifies provisions that vary across program)
4. Change summary becomes broader (all differences across all documents)

This is particularly useful for:
- Multi-carrier competitive analysis (3+ quotes)
- Program consistency review (multiple locations, subsidiaries)
- Historical trend analysis (policies over multiple years)

## Common Customization Scenarios

### Client-Ready Renewal Presentation
Generate a standard comparison report, then:
1. Add executive summary with your recommendations
2. Highlight coverage improvements negotiated
3. Add notes explaining why changes matter to the client's business
4. Include suggestions for further improvements at next renewal

### Competitive Quote Comparison for Pitch
Generate with financial terms focus, then:
1. Add summary of your firm's value-adds (relationships, service, coverage expertise)
2. Highlight areas where you've negotiated better terms
3. Include recommendations for optimal coverage selection
4. Note any special programs or endorsements available

### Program Consistency Review
Generate with full document scope comparing multiple carrier policies, then:
1. Flag any inconsistencies in terms across carriers
2. Identify coverage gaps relative to program requirements
3. Recommend standardization of key terms
4. Prioritize gaps for renewal negotiations

## Troubleshooting

If your report doesn't match expectations:

- **Too brief?** - Regenerate with change sensitivity set to "Detailed"
- **Too much detail?** - Regenerate with change sensitivity set to "Material Only"
- **Missing comparison?** - Verify all documents were selected and uploaded correctly
- **Wrong focus?** - Reconfigure the scope to match your comparison purpose
- **Formatting issues?** - Try exporting to a different format (PDF vs. Word)
