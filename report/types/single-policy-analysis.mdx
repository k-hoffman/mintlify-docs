A single policy analysis report transforms your understanding of an insurance policy into a polished, formatted document suitable for client presentations, team reference, claims files, or professional documentation. This guide walks you through generating, customizing, and exporting a policy analysis report.

## What the Report Produces

A single policy analysis report includes:

- **Executive summary** - Quick overview of coverage, key terms, and notable features
- **Policy summary** - Named insured, period, coverage territory, classification
- **Coverage analysis** - Each coverage part explained with limits, deductibles, and key terms
- **Limits and deductibles** - Easy-to-reference financial terms and exclusions
- **Exclusion analysis** - Key exclusions explained, with guidance on what's NOT covered
- **Endorsement review** - Every endorsement listed and explained
- **Conditions and definitions** - Important policy conditions and how key terms are defined
- **Notable provisions** - Unusual or significant terms unique to this policy
- **Risk assessment** - Observations on coverage adequacy, gaps, or exposure areas
- **Recommendations** - Suggested actions or follow-up items

The report is formatted for professional presentation and exportable in multiple formats.

## How to Generate a Single Policy Analysis Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the Policy">
    Select the insurance policy you want to analyze. Upload or select from your library:
    - The base policy document
    - All endorsements and amendments
    - Any related coverage documents
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Analysis Depth">
    Select your desired report depth:
    - **Executive summary** - Key information only, 1-2 pages
    - **Standard** - Balanced detail covering all major sections, 3-5 pages
    - **Detailed** - Comprehensive analysis of every provision, 5+ pages
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Sections to Include">
    Choose which sections to include in your report:
    - Executive summary
    - Policy summary and basics
    - Coverage analysis (detailed by coverage part)
    - Limits and deductibles
    - Exclusion analysis
    - Endorsement review
    - Conditions and definitions
    - Notable provisions
    - Risk assessment
    - Recommendations
    - Appendices (full policy language, form numbers, carrier contact)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Custom Focus Areas">
    Optionally, indicate areas you want emphasized:
    - Specific coverage parts (e.g., General Liability, Property)
    - Coverage questions or concerns
    - Compliance or contractual requirements to evaluate
    - Comparison with prior policy (if renewing)
    - Specific exclusions or conditions of concern
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the Report">
    Click Generate to create your formatted policy analysis report. Qumis analyzes the entire policy and produces a structured, professional document.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and Customize">
    Review the generated report. You can:
    - Adjust analysis depth (add more or less detail)
    - Edit specific sections
    - Add your own notes or insights
    - Emphasize particular areas of concern
    - Add recommendations for coverage improvements
    - Include additional analysis or context
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export and Share">
    Export your report in your preferred format (PDF, Word, HTML) and share with clients, team members, counsel, or include in your file.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Report Sections

A single policy analysis report typically includes:

### Executive Summary
A one-page quick reference including:
- Policy identification (carrier, policy number, period)
- Coverage overview (what's covered, key limits)
- Key findings (strengths, gaps, notable features)
- Risk assessment (adequacy, gaps, concerns)
- Recommended actions (if any)

### Policy Summary and Basics
Details including:
- Named insured, policy number, form numbers
- Policy period (effective through expiration)
- Policy territory and occupancy/classification
- Carrier underwriter and producer information
- Premium or rate information

### Coverage Analysis
For each coverage part (General Liability, Property, etc.):
- Coverage grant (what's covered, simplified)
- Limits of liability
- Deductibles or retentions
- Territory and occupancy limitations
- Sub-limits (specific coverages with lower limits)
- Key endorsements affecting this coverage
- Notable exclusions or conditions
- Adequacy assessment (does this limit seem appropriate?)

### Limits and Deductibles
Easy-to-reference table including:
- Coverage part
- Limits (per occurrence, aggregate)
- Deductibles
- Self-insured retentions
- Premium or rate per coverage
- Out-of-pocket exposure summary

### Exclusion Analysis
Details on exclusions, including:
- Major exclusions (what's NOT covered)
- Conditions that create exclusions (failure to meet conditions = no coverage)
- Any unusual or notably restrictive exclusions
- Explanations of how exclusions might apply to your operations

### Endorsement Review
Lists every endorsement with:
- Endorsement number and name
- Whether it expands, restricts, or clarifies coverage
- Key effect on coverage
- Special conditions or limitations created

### Conditions and Definitions
Important policy conditions including:
- Notice and reporting requirements and deadlines
- Cooperation requirements
- Proof of loss requirements
- Subrogation terms
- Cancellation and non-renewal conditions
- How critical terms are defined and whether definitions differ from your expectations

### Notable Provisions
Highlights:
- Unusual or non-standard terms
- Provisions that differ from typical policies
- Anything surprising or requiring attention
- Special arrangements or accommodations

### Risk Assessment
Analysis including:
- Coverage adequacy for your operations
- Identified gaps or concerns
- Exposure areas not covered
- Deductible and retention appropriateness
- Endorsement adequacy for your contracts
- Comparison to contractual or regulatory requirements (if you specified)

### Recommendations
Suggested actions such as:
- Coverage improvements at renewal
- Endorsements to add
- Limits to increase
- Deductibles to adjust
- Documentation needed for claims coordination
- Gaps to address in your program

## Best Practices

<Tip>
Generate a policy analysis report when you first receive a new policy, before you need it for claims or coverage questions. This helps you understand coverage early and identify gaps proactively.
</Tip>

- **Generate early** - Analyze policies when first received, before claims emerge
- **Use for onboarding** - Provide clients with clear policy summaries during new business
- **Customize for audience** - Technical detail for counsel, plain language for clients and claims team
- **Document assumptions** - Note what you're assuming about the business operations
- **Compare with renewal** - When renewal time comes, compare new proposal with existing policy analysis
- **Identify gaps** - Use analysis to proactively identify coverage gaps and develop improvement recommendations
- **Update as needed** - Regenerate if policies are amended or if your understanding changes

4. Include recommendations for improvements
5. Prepare for renewal discussion with broker

### Coverage Adequacy Assessment
Generate a detailed report, then:
1. Compare coverage against contractual requirements
2. Assess limits against operations and risk profile
3. Identify any gaps or inadequacies
4. Make specific recommendations for improvements
5. Document your analysis for risk committee review

## Troubleshooting

If your report doesn't match expectations:

- **Too brief?** - Regenerate with higher depth level (Executive → Standard → Detailed)
- **Too much detail?** - Regenerate with lower depth level or select fewer sections
- **Missing analysis?** - Update your custom focus areas and regenerate
- **Wrong focus?** - Select specific sections you want included
- **Need comparison?** - Manually add a column with prior year or renewal proposal details
- **Formatting issues?** - Try exporting to a different format (PDF vs. Word)
