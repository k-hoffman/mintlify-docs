A claims analysis report transforms your coverage evaluation into a polished, formatted document suitable for filing, sharing with outside counsel, presenting to stakeholders, or including in your claims file. This guide walks you through generating, customizing, and exporting a claims analysis report.

## What the Report Produces

A claims analysis report includes:

- **Executive summary** - Quick overview of the coverage position and key findings
- **Coverage analysis by insuring agreement** - Section-by-section evaluation of relevant coverage grants
- **Exclusion analysis** - Detailed review of exclusions and how they apply to your claim
- **Conditions analysis** - Assessment of policy conditions (notice, cooperation, proof of loss, etc.)
- **Coverage position statement** - Clear determination with confidence level and supporting reasoning
- **Cited policy language** - Direct quotes from the policy with page references
- **Recommendations** - Suggested next steps (counsel engagement, additional documentation, etc.)

The report is formatted for professional presentation and can be customized by section.

## How to Generate a Claims Analysis Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Policy Documents">
    Upload or select the insurance policy documents you want to analyze. Include the base policy, all endorsements, and any related coverage documents.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe the Claim Scenario">
    Provide the claim facts: What happened? When? Where? Who was involved? What is the nature of the loss or damage? Be as specific as possible—Qumis uses these details to evaluate coverage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Analysis Scope">
    Choose your analysis scope:
    - **Full policy analysis** - Evaluate the entire policy for coverage
    - **Specific coverage parts** - Focus on particular sections (e.g., General Liability, Property)
    - **Endorsement focus** - Emphasize specific endorsements or modifications

    You can also indicate priority areas or coverage questions you want emphasized.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Report Depth">
    Select your desired report depth:
    - **Executive summary** - Key findings and coverage position only (1-2 pages)
    - **Standard** - Detailed analysis with section-by-section evaluation (3-5 pages)
    - **Comprehensive** - Full analysis including all provisions, case law references, and detailed reasoning (5+ pages)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the Report">
    Click Generate to create your formatted claims analysis report. Qumis analyzes the policy documents against claim facts and produces a structured report.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and Customize">
    Review the generated report. You can:
    - Add or remove sections
    - Edit analysis for specific provisions
    - Add your own notes or commentary
    - Adjust the coverage position or confidence level
    - Add additional policy documents or claim details
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export and Share">
    Export your report in your preferred format (PDF, Word, etc.) and share with counsel, adjusters, clients, or include in your claims file.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Report Sections

The claims analysis report is organized as follows:

### Executive Summary
A one-page overview including:
- Policy identification (carrier, policy number, period)
- Claim summary (loss date, description, amount)
- Coverage position (coverage likely/uncertain/unlikely)
- Confidence level (high/medium/low)
- Key findings (most important supporting or limiting factors)

### Coverage Analysis by Insuring Agreement
For each applicable coverage part, includes:
- Coverage grant language (quoted directly from policy)
- How the claim fits within (or outside) the coverage grant
- Supporting factors from claim facts
- Limiting factors or potential challenges
- Confidence assessment

### Exclusion Analysis
Reviews exclusions that could apply:
- Exclusion language (direct quote)
- Whether the exclusion applies to this claim
- Supporting or opposing arguments
- Case law or regulatory considerations

### Conditions Analysis
Evaluates policy conditions:
- Notice requirements (met? timeline issues?)
- Cooperation requirements
- Proof of loss requirements
- Other conditions that could affect coverage

### Coverage Position Statement
A clear determination including:
- Primary coverage position
- Confidence level with explanation
- Most important supporting arguments (with citations)
- Most important limiting arguments (with citations)
- Assumptions underlying the analysis
- Recommended next steps

### Cited Policy Language
All direct quotes from the policy with:
- Exact policy language (in quotation marks)
- Page numbers or section references
- Context and application to the claim

## Configuration Options

### Analysis Scope
- **Full policy** - Evaluate all coverage parts and endorsements
- **Specific sections** - Focus on particular coverage parts (e.g., General Liability, Products/Completed Operations)
- **Endorsement-focused** - Emphasize endorsements and modifications

### Depth Level
- **Summary** - Key findings only, minimal detailed analysis
- **Standard** - Balanced detail for most use cases
- **Detailed** - Comprehensive analysis of every relevant provision

### Output Format
- **PDF** - For sharing and filing
- **Word** - For editing and additional commentary
- **HTML** - For web display or email sharing

### Custom Sections
You can include or exclude:
- Executive summary
- Coverage analysis by insuring agreement
- Exclusion analysis
- Conditions analysis
- Endorsement analysis
- Definitions section
- Coverage limitations and aggregates
- Case law references
- Recommendations
- Appendices (policy excerpts, claim documents)

## Best Practices

<Tip>
Generate your claims analysis report early in the claims process—even preliminary analysis helps organize information and identifies gaps in your claim file (missing documents, needed investigation, etc.).
</Tip>

- **Generate early** - Analyze coverage while facts are fresh and before positions harden
- **Customize for audience** - Technical detail for counsel, summary for claims team and management
- **Document assumptions** - Clearly state what you're assuming about the facts or policy interpretation
- **Use as a workflow tool** - Reports organize your analysis and support decision-making, even if not shared externally
- **Update as facts evolve** - Regenerate reports as claim details emerge or facts change
- **Coordinate with counsel** - Share preliminary Qumis analysis with counsel to guide their deeper review

## Common Customization Scenarios

### Client-Ready Claims Summary
Generate a standard report, then:
1. Remove technical legal references and heavy case law citations
2. Focus on the "coverage position" and key supporting points
3. Add a client-friendly explanation of next steps
4. Include recommendations for coverage improvements at renewal

### Outside Counsel Brief
Generate a comprehensive report, then:
1. Expand the legal analysis and case law section
2. Include detailed exclusion and condition analysis
3. Add appendices with full policy language and claim documents
4. Include detailed assumptions and coverage questions for counsel guidance

### Claims File Documentation
Generate the report with:
1. Executive summary for file accessibility
2. Full analysis with all policy language cited
3. Confidence levels clearly stated
4. Assumptions documented
5. Internal notes on investigation needs or follow-ups

## Troubleshooting

If your report doesn't match your expectations:

- **Too brief?** - Regenerate with a higher depth level (Summary → Standard → Detailed)
- **Missing analysis?** - Update your claim description with additional facts or specifics
- **Wrong focus?** - Reconfigure the scope to include specific endorsements or coverage parts
- **Need more citations?** - Use the Detailed depth level for comprehensive policy language citations
