## The strategy

Contract reviews and certificates of insurance are the highest-volume servicing activity in most brokerages. A single mid-market account might generate 20–50 certificate requests per year. A large account with active construction, real estate, or vendor operations can generate hundreds.

The traditional workflow is slow and error-prone. A client sends a contract with insurance requirements. An account manager reads through the contract, extracts the insurance provisions, compares them against the current program, identifies any gaps, flags them with the producer, waits for a response, and then issues the certificate. When the requirements don't match the current program, the back-and-forth between the client, the brokerage, and the carrier can stretch the process from days to weeks.

Qumis compresses this workflow with the [Contract Analysis](/platform/contract-review) report. Upload the contract alongside the client's active policies, and the report identifies every insurance requirement, determines whether each is compliant, non-compliant, or ambiguous, and cites the specific policy language supporting each finding. The embedded [Chat](/platform/chats) then lets you draft professional client communications and resolve gaps without leaving the report. The account manager goes from "let me review this and get back to you" to "here's exactly where you stand, here's what's covered, and here's what we need to address" in a single conversation.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Determine the requirements and compliance status">
    When a client sends a contract for insurance review, upload it to the [Document Vault](/platform/document-vault) alongside the client's active policies. Then run a [Contract Analysis](/platform/contract-review) report with the contract and the policies loaded.

    The Contract Analysis report extracts every insurance-related requirement from the contract and checks each one against the active program. For each requirement, it determines whether the current coverage is:

    - **Compliant** — the policy satisfies the requirement, with citations to the specific endorsement or language
    - **Non-Compliant** — the program doesn't address this requirement, with an explanation of what's missing
    - **Ambiguous** — the policy language could be interpreted either way, flagged for further review

    The report covers limits, deductibles, additional insured status, waiver of subrogation, primary and noncontributory provisions, notice of cancellation, carrier rating requirements, and any other insurance-related conditions in the contract.

    <Note>
      When a contract requires primary and noncontributory coverage across all layers, pay extra attention to the umbrella. Most umbrella forms contain "other insurance" clauses stating they are excess of any other liability policy — which can conflict with a contractual promise of primary and noncontributory coverage when additional insured status is granted on the underlying GL. Use the embedded [Chat](/platform/chats) to validate whether the umbrella's language creates a conflict.
    </Note>

    If the report flags a primary and noncontributory requirement, use the following prompt in the Chat to check whether the umbrella responds correctly:

    ```
    The contract requires primary and noncontributory coverage across
    all layers. Review the umbrella/excess policy's "other insurance"
    clause and determine whether it would respond as primary when the
    underlying GL has granted additional insured status to the
    certificate holder. Cite the specific "other insurance" language
    in the umbrella and explain whether it conflicts with the
    contractual primary and noncontributory requirement. If there is
    a conflict, recommend how to address it — whether through an
    endorsement to the umbrella, clarification from the carrier, or
    a coverage opinion.
    ```

    <Warning>
      The Contract Analysis report is an analysis tool, not legal advice. Always note that the client should have their legal counsel review any contractual insurance provisions alongside your analysis. Qumis identifies whether the insurance program can satisfy the requirements — it does not interpret the broader contractual obligations.
    </Warning>

    → [Contract Analysis](/platform/contract-review)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Communicate and address gaps">
    Use the embedded [Chat](/platform/chats) in the Contract Analysis report to draft professional client communications based on the findings. The Chat has full context of the analysis, so it can generate accurate, citation-backed communications without you needing to summarize the results manually.

    **Compliant — ready to issue:**
    ```
    Draft a client email confirming that their current program meets
    all insurance requirements in this contract. Include a brief
    summary of each requirement and the policy or endorsement that
    satisfies it. Note that we can issue the certificate immediately
    and ask for the certificate holder's contact information if we
    don't have it on file.
    ```

    **Gaps identified — action needed:**
    ```
    Draft a client email explaining the contract review results.
    Summarize which requirements are met by the current program
    and which have gaps. For each gap, describe what's missing
    and recommend a specific solution (endorsement modification,
    limit increase, or new coverage). Include estimated timelines
    for resolving each gap. Ask whether the client wants us to
    issue a certificate showing current coverage while modifications
    are in process, or wait until all gaps are resolved.
    ```

    **Ambiguous requirements — needs clarification:**
    ```
    Draft a client email explaining that certain contract requirements
    are ambiguous based on the current policy language. For each
    ambiguous item, explain both possible interpretations, cite the
    relevant policy language, and recommend next steps — whether
    that's requesting clarification from the counterparty, obtaining
    a coverage opinion from the carrier, or having legal counsel
    review the specific provision.
    ```

    <Tip>
      Use the Chat to present solutions alongside problems. When the report identifies a gap, ask the Chat to draft language that explains what's missing and recommends a specific fix. For example, if the contract requires a higher GL limit than the current program carries, ask the Chat to draft a client email explaining the gap and recommending an endorsement to increase the limit or an adjustment to the umbrella, with a timeline for pricing. The Chat already has the full analysis context, so it can cite the exact requirement and the exact policy language in the communication it drafts.
    </Tip>

    → [Chat](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Issue the certificate or evidence of insurance">
    Once all requirements are confirmed compliant — or the client has acknowledged and accepted any gaps — issue the certificate of insurance or evidence of insurance. If your agency management system or certificate of insurance creation tool is browser-based, you can use the [Browser Extension](/platform/coming-soon/chrome-extension) to instruct Qumis to create the certificate directly from the browser, keeping the workflow within Qumis and reducing the manual data entry that leads to certificate errors.

    Save the Contract Analysis report in the [Document Vault](/platform/document-vault) as a record of the analysis that supported the certificate. This creates an audit trail showing that every item on the certificate was verified against the actual policy, which is valuable for E&O protection, renewal reference, and stewardship reviews.

    → [Browser Extension](/platform/coming-soon/chrome-extension)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Validate the certificate against the policies">
    As a final quality check, upload the issued certificate back into the [Document Vault](/platform/document-vault) and run a [Contract Analysis](/platform/contract-review) report comparing the certificate against the client's active policies. This catches the errors that are most common in certificate issuance: listing endorsements that don't exist on the policy, overstating limits, naming additional insureds without a supporting endorsement, or referencing waiver of subrogation provisions that don't extend to the certificate holder.

    This validation step is especially valuable when certificates are issued by support staff or when a high volume of certificates is going out on the same account. It takes minutes to run and catches discrepancies before they reach the certificate holder.

    <Tip>
      Build this validation step into your standard certificate workflow. The few minutes it takes to run the check is trivial compared to the cost of issuing an inaccurate certificate — whether that's the E&O exposure, the client embarrassment, or the time spent correcting and reissuing.
    </Tip>

    → [Contract Analysis](/platform/contract-review)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common contract review scenarios

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Lease agreements">
    Commercial leases typically require the tenant to carry GL, property (for tenant improvements), umbrella, and sometimes professional liability. Key issues to check: whether the landlord is properly named as additional insured, whether the property coverage extends to tenant improvements and betterments, and whether the waiver of subrogation runs in both directions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Construction subcontracts">
    Subcontracts often have the most demanding insurance requirements: high limits, specific additional insured endorsement versions, primary and noncontributory language, completed operations coverage, and sometimes wrap-up program compliance. Pay particular attention to whether the umbrella responds as primary when additional insured status is granted on the underlying GL — the umbrella's "other insurance" clause may conflict with the contractual primary and noncontributory requirement.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Vendor and service agreements">
    Vendor agreements vary widely but commonly require GL, professional liability or E&O, cyber liability, and workers' comp. The key issue is often whether the vendor's professional liability or cyber policy is broad enough to cover the specific services described in the contract.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Loan and financing agreements">
    Lenders require property insurance covering the collateral, often with specific requirements around loss payee status, replacement cost valuation, and agreed amount endorsements. Failure to comply can trigger loan covenant violations.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Measuring success

| Metric | What to track | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Contract review turnaround | Time from client request to compliance analysis delivered | Under 4 hours for standard contracts |
| First-time accuracy | Percentage of certificates issued correctly without revision | 95%+ |
| Compliance gaps identified proactively | Number of gaps found in contract reviews that the client didn't know about | Track per review |
| Documentation completeness | Percentage of issued certificates with a Contract Analysis report in the Vault | 100% |
