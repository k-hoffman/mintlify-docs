## The strategy

Coverage questions are the most frequent and most time-sensitive servicing interaction. A client calls and asks: "Am I covered if...?" "Does my policy include...?" "What happens when...?" The quality and speed of the answer determines how the client perceives their broker's expertise.

The traditional workflow looks like this: the client asks a question, the account manager says "let me check and get back to you," they pull out the 80-page policy PDF, search through it manually, maybe consult a colleague, draft a response, have it reviewed, and send it back. Even then, the response is often a summary without citations, which means the client has to trust the broker's interpretation without seeing the underlying language.

Most experienced brokers and account managers know their products well — they've been selling and servicing them for years and understand the differences between forms, endorsements, and carrier appetites. But every program has areas where the answer isn't immediately obvious: an industry or product line you don't work with often, a recently updated form, or a policy built on uncommon proprietary manuscripted language. And for newer team members who are still building that product knowledge, every coverage question can feel like a research project.

Qumis eliminates the delay regardless of familiarity. Upload the client's policy (or it's already in the Vault), ask the coverage question in [Chat](/platform/chats), and get a citation-backed answer in seconds. Whether you're a 20-year veteran checking a niche exclusion or a new account manager fielding your first GL question, the answer comes back the same way: grounded in the actual policy language, with citations to the specific provisions that apply.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ensure the client's program is in Document Vault">
    The fastest way to answer a coverage question is to have the client's policies already loaded in the [Document Vault](/platform/document-vault). If you've been following the broking and stewardship playbooks, they're already there.

    If not, upload the relevant policy before starting the analysis. For most coverage questions, you only need the specific policy related to the question (the GL policy for a liability question, the property policy for a building damage question), but having the full program available lets Qumis check whether other policies might also respond.

    <Tip>
      Make it a standard practice to upload every client's full program to the Vault at binding. The upfront investment saves hours across the policy period because every coverage question, contract review, and stewardship meeting starts instantly instead of with a document hunt.
    </Tip>

    → [Document Vault](/platform/document-vault)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ask the coverage question in Chat">
    Open a [Chat](/platform/chats) session with the relevant policy or policies loaded and ask the question the same way the client asked you — in plain English, the way you'd ask a colleague sitting next to you. Qumis understands both plain language and technical terminology, so there's no need to translate the question into a specific format.

    For example, if a client asks "Am I covered if one of my subcontractors causes property damage on a job site?", just type that. Qumis will identify the relevant insuring agreements, check for applicable exclusions and exceptions, cite the specific policy language, and flag if other policies in the program might also respond.

    <Tip>
      If the answer comes back and the language isn't clear-cut, tell Qumis. Ask it to explain where the ambiguity is, what the possible interpretations are, and what the insured should know about the uncertainty. Also ask whether any other policies in the program might respond to the same scenario — umbrella drop-downs, cyber overlaps with property, or inland marine coverage that supplements property.
    </Tip>

    <Warning>
      Qumis provides coverage analysis based on the policy language — not coverage opinions or legal advice. Treat the output as an analytical starting point, not a definitive coverage determination.
    </Warning>

    → [Chat](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Supplement with Legal Search when relevant">
    For coverage questions that involve ambiguous language or potential disputes, use Legal Search in the [Chat](/platform/chats) to find case law that addresses how similar language has been interpreted by courts.

    **When to use Legal Search:**

    - The policy language is ambiguous and could be read two ways
    - The client is asking about an unusual or emerging risk scenario
    - The question involves an exclusion whose scope is debatable
    - The answer could have significant financial implications and the client needs more than just a policy read

    **Example Legal Search prompt:**
    ```
    Find cases interpreting the "expected or intended" exclusion
    in CGL policies as applied to claims arising from construction
    defects where the insured did not intend to cause property
    damage but the work itself was intentional
    ```

    → [Chat](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Draft and send the response">
    Use the [Chat](/platform/chats) to draft a client-ready email based on the analysis. The Chat already has the full context of the coverage question and the policy language, so it can generate a response that's accurate, cited, and written in a way the insured can understand.

    ```
    Draft an email response to the client answering their coverage
    question in an insured-friendly way. Summarize the answer clearly,
    reference the key policy provisions that support it, and note any
    conditions or limitations they should be aware of. If the answer
    is ambiguous, explain the uncertainty and recommend next steps.
    ```

    For questions with claim implications, add a note in the email that the analysis is based on the policy language and recommend the client share it with their legal counsel or claims team.

    → [Chat](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Reference past conversations">
    Chat sessions are automatically saved in Qumis, creating a searchable history of coverage questions and answers for each account. This history is valuable in three ways:

    - **Stewardship input** — recurring questions about the same coverage area signal that the client needs better understanding of their program or that the coverage itself should be improved.
    - **Renewal intelligence** — a pattern of coverage questions can inform what to negotiate at renewal.
    - **Institutional knowledge** — if the account manager changes, the new team member can review past coverage conversations to get up to speed on what's come up during the policy period.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Coverage question prompts by scenario

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="'Am I covered for...?' questions">
    The most common type. The client describes a scenario and wants to know if their policy responds.

    ```
    The client is asking whether their policy covers the following
    scenario: [describe the scenario].

    Analyze the uploaded policy to determine whether the insuring
    agreement provides coverage for this scenario, whether any
    exclusions apply that would eliminate or limit coverage, whether
    any exceptions to exclusions restore coverage, what deductible
    or retention applies, and what the applicable limits would be.
    If other policies in the program might also respond, identify
    those as well.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="'What are my limits for...?' questions">
    Clients often need to know specific limits for contracts, financing requirements, or risk management decisions.

    ```
    The client needs to know their exact coverage limits for
    [specific coverage type or scenario]. Review the uploaded
    policy and identify the per-occurrence or per-claim limit,
    the aggregate limit and whether it's shared with other
    coverages, any sublimits that apply to this specific exposure,
    whether defense costs erode the limit, and if the umbrella
    or excess provides additional limits for this exposure and
    whether it follows form. Present the full tower of available
    limits.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="'Does this exclusion apply to...?' questions">
    Exclusion questions often arise when a client is evaluating a new business opportunity or contract and wants to understand their risk.

    ```
    The client is asking whether the [specific exclusion, e.g.,
    "pollution exclusion" or "professional services exclusion"]
    in their policy would apply to the following situation:
    [describe the situation].

    Analyze the exact scope of the exclusion as written, whether
    any exceptions to the exclusion might apply to this situation,
    whether the situation as described falls within or outside
    the exclusion's language, and if the language is ambiguous,
    explain both possible interpretations. Also check whether any
    other policy or endorsement might provide coverage that the
    exclusion removes from the primary.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="'What happens if...?' process questions">
    Clients often ask about the mechanics of how their coverage works in practice: notice requirements, claim procedures, cancellation provisions, and other operational questions.

    ```
    The client is asking about the process or mechanics of their
    coverage: [describe the question, e.g., "What do I need to
    do if I want to add a new location mid-term?" or "How much
    notice do I need to give before cancelling?" or "What happens
    if I discover a claim after the policy expires?"].

    Review the policy conditions, definitions, and any relevant
    endorsements to explain the exact process or requirement as
    stated in the policy, any deadlines or timeframes the insured
    must meet, any consequences of failing to follow the process,
    and a recommendation for how the client should proceed.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Multi-policy questions">
    Some questions involve potential coverage from multiple policies. These are the most complex and the most valuable to answer well.

    ```
    The client is asking about coverage for a scenario that may
    involve multiple policies: [describe the scenario].

    Review ALL uploaded policies to determine which policies
    potentially respond to this scenario, the order in which
    policies apply (primary, excess, other insurance provisions),
    whether any policy exclusions eliminate coverage that another
    policy might provide, the total available limits across all
    responding policies, and any coordination issues between
    policies (gaps, overlaps, or conflicting provisions). Present
    the full coverage picture across the program.
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Real-time response framework

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="While the client is on the phone">
    If a client calls with a coverage question and their policies are already in the Vault, you can answer in real time.

    1. Open a Chat session with their policies loaded
    2. Type the question while the client is still explaining the scenario
    3. Review the Qumis output
    4. Summarize the answer verbally: "Based on what I'm seeing in your policy, [answer]. The relevant language is in [section/endorsement]. I'll send you a written summary with the citations after we hang up."
    5. Use the Chat to draft an email to the insured summarizing the analysis and the key citations, then send it as the follow-up
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Responding to email questions">
    For email questions, the workflow is the same: load the policies, ask the question, and use the Chat to draft the response.

    1. Read the client's question
    2. Open a Chat session with the relevant policies
    3. Ask the question in plain English
    4. Use the Chat to draft an email response to the insured in an insured-friendly way
    5. Review, adjust, and send
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Handling ambiguous questions">
    When Qumis identifies genuinely ambiguous policy language where the answer could go either way, escalate appropriately.

    1. Share both interpretations with the client
    2. Cite the specific language that creates the ambiguity
    3. If Legal Search provides relevant case law, include it
    4. Recommend that the client consult coverage counsel if the stakes are high
    5. Offer to request a coverage clarification from the carrier if appropriate
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Measuring success

| Metric | What to track | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Citation inclusion rate | Percentage of coverage answers that include specific policy language citations | 100% |
| Client satisfaction on responsiveness | Client feedback on speed and quality of coverage question responses | Track via NPS or direct feedback |
| Real-time answer rate | Percentage of phone calls where you answered with citations while the client was on the line | Track for accounts with policies in the Vault |

<Tip>
  The more policies you have loaded in the Document Vault, the faster every coverage question gets answered. Prioritize getting your highest-activity accounts fully loaded first, then expand from there.
</Tip>
