A portfolio review assesses an entire insurance program — multiple policies across multiple lines — to evaluate whether the program provides adequate, coordinated protection. This workflow is especially valuable for risk managers reviewing their own programs and for brokers conducting annual stewardship reviews.

---

## The portfolio review workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Inventory the program">
    Upload all policies in the insurance program. Use Populate to extract key data from each policy into a structured summary:

    _"From each policy, extract: carrier, policy number, effective dates, coverage type, per-occurrence limit, aggregate limit, deductible, and annual premium."_

    This produces the program inventory — the foundation for everything that follows.

    → [Coverage Table](/platform/populate)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Analyze each policy">
    Run a Policy Analysis on each major line to understand the key provisions, exclusions, and limitations:

    - CGL: Focus on products-completed operations, additional insured, and contractual liability
    - Professional liability: Focus on prior acts, defense costs, and consent to settle
    - Property: Focus on business income, ordinance or law, and catastrophe sublimits
    - Cyber: Focus on first-party vs. third-party, ransomware, and regulatory coverage
    - Umbrella/excess: Focus on follow-form provisions, retained limits, and coverage gaps

    → [Policy Analysis](/platform/summarize)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check coverage coordination">
    Use Compare to evaluate how policies work together:

    _"Compare the CGL policy and the umbrella policy. Does the umbrella follow form? Are there any coverage gaps where the umbrella doesn't extend CGL coverage?"_

    Key coordination questions:

    - Does the umbrella/excess follow form to all underlying policies?
    - Are there gaps between underlying limits and umbrella attachment?
    - Are definitions consistent across the program?
    - Do "other insurance" clauses create conflicts?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify against requirements">
    Run Contract Compliance to check the program against contractual requirements:

    _"Check this insurance program against the insurance requirements in our [top 3 contracts / standard lease / vendor agreement]."_

    → [Contract Review](/platform/contract-review)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Identify gaps and overlaps">
    Use Contract Review to check for specific exposures:

    _"Given our company's risk profile as a [industry/description], identify coverage gaps across the entire program. Consider: [key risk exposures specific to the business]."_

    Also look for overlaps — areas where multiple policies cover the same exposure, potentially creating coordination issues.

    → [Contract Review](/platform/contract-review)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the Coverage Table">
    Produce a visual summary of the entire program using a Coverage Table. This becomes the executive-level view of the portfolio.

    → [Coverage Table](/platform/populate)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compile the review document">
    Ask Qumis to synthesize findings across all policies:

    _"Based on our analysis of all policies in this program, draft a portfolio review summary that includes: (1) program strengths, (2) coverage gaps ranked by severity, (3) coordination issues between policies, (4) compliance gaps against contractual requirements, and (5) recommendations."_
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Portfolio review frequency

| Trigger                 | What to Review                                                    |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Annual stewardship**  | Full portfolio — all policies, all requirements, all coordination |
| **Mid-term change**     | Affected policies + coordination check against umbrella/excess    |
| **New contract signed** | Contract requirements + relevant policies for compliance          |
| **Claim occurrence**    | Responding policies + coordination + limits adequacy              |
| **Business change**     | Risk profile impact + coverage gap assessment                     |

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    **Stewardship value.** A comprehensive portfolio review is one of the highest-value deliverables you can provide. It demonstrates that you're actively managing the account, not just renewing policies. Schedule annual portfolio reviews for your top accounts.

    **New business tool.** Offer a free portfolio review to prospects. The gaps and coordination issues you identify become the foundation of your proposal.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    **Proactive service.** Don't wait for the annual review. Run targeted portfolio checks when clients announce business changes — new locations, new contracts, new product lines. Show them how their coverage needs to adapt.

    **Cross-selling.** Portfolio reviews naturally surface gaps that represent cross-selling opportunities. Present these as risk management recommendations, not sales pitches.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    **Multi-policy claims.** When a loss potentially triggers multiple policies, use the portfolio review workflow to map which policies respond, how they coordinate, and where coverage gaps exist.

    **Lessons learned.** After significant claims, recommend a targeted portfolio review to address any coverage gaps the claim revealed.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    **This is your primary workflow.** An annual portfolio review is the cornerstone of your insurance risk management program. Use Qumis to produce the analytical foundation, then layer in your business context and strategic priorities.

    **Board reporting.** Use the Coverage Table and portfolio summary to produce executive-level reporting on your insurance program's adequacy and cost-effectiveness.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
