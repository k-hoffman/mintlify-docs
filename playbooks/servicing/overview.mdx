Servicing is where retention is won or lost. The placement is done, the policy is bound, and now the client needs to see ongoing value from their broker. The brokerages that retain and expand accounts are the ones that show up between renewals with insight, responsiveness, and a level of coverage knowledge that makes switching unthinkable.

Qumis gives your servicing team the ability to respond to client requests faster, deliver more thorough stewardship, and answer coverage questions with citation-backed precision instead of "let me get back to you."

## Who these playbooks are for

These playbooks are designed for **account managers, client service teams, and CSMs** who handle day-to-day client relationships after the policy is bound. They're also valuable for producers who stay involved in servicing their accounts. They assume you have access to Qumis and are comfortable with the core features.

<Note>
  If you're new to Qumis, start with the [Get Started](/get-started/welcome) guides first. These servicing playbooks build on foundational skills like uploading documents to the Vault and using the Chat and Report features.
</Note>

## How to use these playbooks

Each playbook follows the same structure:

- **The strategy** explains why this servicing activity matters for retention and how Qumis changes the workflow.
- **The Qumis workflow** walks you through the exact steps, features, and prompts you'll use.
- **Templates and prompts** give you ready-to-use language for client communication, analysis, and documentation.
- **Measuring success** defines what good looks like so your team can track service quality improvement.

These three playbooks cover the servicing activities that happen most frequently between renewals. They can be used independently, but the biggest retention impact comes when all three are Qumis-powered so the client experiences consistent analytical depth at every touchpoint.

## How servicing connects to broking and prospecting

If you've used the [Prospecting Playbooks](/playbooks/prospecting/overview) and [Broking Playbooks](/playbooks/broking/overview), the servicing playbooks complete the lifecycle. The coverage analysis you built during placement becomes the foundation for your stewardship review. The binding review you conducted informs your certificate issuance. The vertical expertise you demonstrated during prospecting is what the client expects to see every time they call with a coverage question.

<Tip>
  The fastest path to a lost account is a gap between the analytical rigor you showed during placement and the service quality you deliver afterward. These playbooks ensure the client experience stays consistent from day one through renewal.
</Tip>
