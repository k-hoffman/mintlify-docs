Broking is where the promises made during prospecting get tested. The quality of your market submissions, the depth of your quote analysis, and the accuracy of your binding review are what determine whether a client stays, expands, or leaves.

Qumis gives brokers a structural advantage at every stage of the placement process. Instead of relying on spreadsheets, memory, and manual policy reads, you can run attorney-grade analysis on submissions, quotes, and binders in a fraction of the time, with citations back to the actual policy language.

## Who these playbooks are for

These playbooks are designed for **producers, brokers, and anyone who handles the day-to-day work** of marketing accounts, analyzing quotes, and binding coverage. They assume you have access to Qumis and are comfortable uploading documents, running chats, and generating reports.

<Note>
  If you're new to Qumis, start with the [Get Started](/get-started/welcome) guides first. These broking playbooks build on foundational skills like uploading documents to the Vault and using the Chat and Report features.
</Note>

---

## How to use these playbooks

Each playbook follows the same structure:

- **The strategy** explains why Qumis changes the workflow and what advantage it creates over traditional methods.
- **The Qumis workflow** walks you through the exact steps, features, and prompts you'll use at each stage.
- **Templates and prompts** give you ready-to-use prompt language and output structures for common placement scenarios.
- **Measuring success** defines what good looks like so you and your team can track improvement.

These three playbooks follow the natural sequence of a placement. You can use them independently, but the biggest impact comes from running all three on the same account so that every stage of the placement is Qumis-powered.

## How broking playbooks connect to prospecting

If you've already used the [Prospecting Playbooks](/playbooks/prospecting/overview), the broking playbooks pick up right where those leave off. The coverage intelligence brief you built during prospecting becomes the foundation for how you market the account. The analysis you used to win the meeting now informs how you structure the submission and evaluate the quotes that come back.

<Tip>
  The strongest client impression happens when the same analytical rigor they saw during prospecting carries through into placement. If you showed them Qumis-powered coverage analysis to win the account, use Qumis-powered quote comparison to prove it wasn't a one-time show.
</Tip>
