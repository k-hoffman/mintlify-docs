## The strategy

Binding is where a placement becomes a promise. You've marketed the account, presented the quotes, and secured the client's decision. Now the bind order needs to go out accurately, the binder needs to come back matching what was quoted, the issued policy needs to match the binder, and the client needs to receive a clean, verified policy package. Every handoff in this chain is an opportunity for something to go wrong.

The most common E&O exposures in broking don't originate from bad coverage advice — they originate from execution errors during binding. A sublimit that changed between quote and binder. An endorsement edition that defaulted to the carrier's library instead of the version the underwriter quoted. A named insured that dropped the subsidiary language. These are the kinds of discrepancies that surface months later when a claim is filed and the policy doesn't respond the way everyone expected.

Most brokers handle this phase with a manual side-by-side read: the binder in one window, the quote in another, checking line by line. On a simple monoline policy this is manageable. On a complex placement with manuscript endorsements, layered programs, and negotiated terms across multiple submissions, things get missed. And the issued policy review — weeks later, with the broker already deep into the next account — often gets less attention than it deserves.

Qumis changes this by turning every verification into a structured comparison with citations to the exact language in both documents. [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) identifies every difference between what was quoted and what was bound, categorized by severity. [Chats](/platform/chats) drafts the bind order and carrier correction requests grounded in the specific language that needs to change. The result is a binding process where discrepancies surface immediately, corrections happen before delivery, and every review is documented for the file.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Send the bind order to the carrier">
    Once the client has made their decision, use [Chats](/platform/chats) to draft a bind order that leaves no room for ambiguity. Load the accepted quote into the Chat session and have Qumis generate a bind order that references every material term — named insured, policy period, limits, deductibles, premium, and each endorsement by form number and edition date. This level of specificity makes it harder for the carrier to issue a binder that deviates from what was agreed.

    **Bind order prompt:**
    ```
    Based on the accepted quote, draft a formal bind order email to the
    underwriter. Include:

    1. Named insured (exact entity name and any subsidiaries or
       additional named insureds)
    2. Policy period (effective and expiration dates)
    3. Limits and sublimits (every limit listed in the quote)
    4. Deductibles and SIRs
    5. Premium (including any installment terms)
    6. Every endorsement by form number and edition date
    7. Any manuscript or negotiated modifications agreed during placement

    Reference the quote date and any correspondence where terms were
    confirmed. Format as a professional email ready to send.
    ```

    <Tip>
      The more specific your bind order, the fewer discrepancies you'll find in the binder. Carriers issue binders based on what the broker requests — if your bind order is vague, the carrier fills in the gaps with their defaults. Referencing every endorsement by form number and edition date forces the issuance team to match exactly what was quoted.
    </Tip>

    → [Chats](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check the binder against the proposal and application">
    When the binder arrives, upload it to the [Document Vault](/platform/document-vault) alongside the accepted quote and your bind order. Run [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) with the binder and the accepted quote to surface every difference between what was quoted and what was bound. Compare Documents automatically generates a Coverage Table and a QumisScore for each document, giving you a structured side-by-side view of limits, deductibles, and key terms alongside the detailed analysis.

    For any discrepancies Compare Documents flags, open a [Chat](/platform/chats) session to draft a correction request that cites the specific binder language and states exactly what needs to change. Attach the relevant section of the comparison report so the carrier can see the finding directly.

    **Binder verification prompt:**
    ```
    Compare this binder against the accepted carrier quote. For every
    difference between the two documents, cite the exact language from
    both and categorize the discrepancy as:
    - CRITICAL (affects coverage scope, limits, or could result in
      claim denial)
    - MODERATE (affects terms or conditions but unlikely to affect
      claim outcomes)
    - MINOR (administrative, formatting, or non-substantive)

    Check: named insured, policy period, every limit and sublimit,
    deductibles, premium, endorsements (form numbers and edition dates),
    coverage terms, definitions, and any negotiated manuscript changes.
    ```

    <Warning>
      Don't wait for the full issued policy to start your review. Run the verification on the binder as soon as it arrives. Corrections are far easier at the binder stage than after the policy has been issued. Target completing the review and sending any correction requests within 48 hours of receiving the binder.
    </Warning>

    → [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) · [Chats](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check the policy against the proposal and application">
    When the full issued policy arrives — typically four to eight weeks after binding — upload it to the [Document Vault](/platform/document-vault) and add it to the Compare Documents report you ran at the binder stage. Open the History tab in Qumis, find the binder comparison report, and click **Add Documents** to include the issued policy. Qumis re-runs the comparison across all the documents — the accepted quote, the binder, and now the issued policy — so you can see exactly what changed between binder and issuance without starting from scratch.

    This is where you confirm that any corrections you requested at the binder stage were actually implemented. Carriers sometimes introduce changes between binder and issuance, especially on endorsement language, and this second pass catches anything that shifted. Pay special attention to endorsement edition dates — carriers sometimes issue a policy with a newer edition than what was quoted, and a different edition date can mean different coverage terms even if the form number is the same.

    For any new discrepancies, use [Chats](/platform/chats) to draft correction requests the same way you did at the binder stage. The carrier needs to issue an endorsement or corrected policy before you deliver to the client.

    <Tip>
      Building on the existing report instead of starting a new comparison has two advantages: you preserve the full audit trail from binder through issuance in a single report, and Qumis already has context on the original quote terms and any discrepancies you flagged earlier.
    </Tip>

    → [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) · [Chats](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Deliver the policy to the insured">
    Before sending the policy to the client, use [Chats](/platform/chats) to generate a delivery summary that confirms the coverage matches what was presented and highlights anything the client should know. If you used the [Proposal Generator](/platform/coming-soon/proposal-generator) during the [Presenting Quotes](/playbooks/broking/quote-comparison) phase, reference the proposal to confirm the bound terms align with what the client approved.

    If any discrepancies were found and corrected during the binder or policy review, note them in the delivery communication so the client knows the review was thorough. If corrections are still outstanding with the carrier, flag that clearly and provide a timeline for resolution.

    **Policy delivery prompt:**
    ```
    Draft a policy delivery email to the client confirming the bound
    coverage. Include:

    1. Carrier name, policy type, and policy period
    2. Confirmation that all coverage terms, limits, and endorsements
       match what was presented in the proposal
    3. A summary of any discrepancies found and corrected during our
       binding review
    4. Any outstanding items still being resolved with the carrier
    5. Certificate of insurance readiness — confirm whether certs can
       be issued immediately
    6. Next steps (when to expect the full policy if only a binder
       has been issued, upcoming renewal timeline)

    Write in a professional but accessible tone. The audience is the
    client's risk manager or CFO.
    ```

    <Tip>
      The comparison reports and correction correspondence from Steps 2 and 3 serve as your placement documentation. They record what was verified, what was corrected, and what was delivered — which is valuable for E&O defense, renewal benchmarking, and stewardship reviews.
    </Tip>

    → [Chats](/platform/chats)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Measuring success

| Metric | What to track | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Binding reviews completed | Percentage of placements with a Qumis-powered binder and policy review | 100% of placements |
| Discrepancies caught at binder stage | Number of discrepancies identified before policy issuance | Track per placement (expect 1–3 on average) |
| Critical discrepancies caught | Coverage-affecting discrepancies found before policy delivery | Zero missed |
| Time from binder receipt to review | Hours from binder arrival to completed verification | Under 2 hours for standard placements |
| Correction turnaround | Days from discrepancy identification to carrier correction | Under 5 business days |
| Policy delivery time | Business days from issued policy receipt to client delivery | Under 3 business days |

<Note>
  The binding review is the highest-ROI activity in the placement lifecycle when measured against the cost of a single E&O claim. The 1–2 hours invested in a structured verification protects the client, protects the brokerage, and creates a documented audit trail for every placement.
</Note>
