## The strategy

Presenting quotes is where your analytical work meets the client relationship. You've marketed the account, received quotes, and run your comparisons. Now you need to translate all of that into a clear recommendation the client can act on — and defend that recommendation through stakeholder questions, objections, and negotiations before arriving at a binding decision.

Most brokers handle this phase with a spreadsheet comparison and a verbal walkthrough. The spreadsheet shows limits and premiums but doesn't explain *why* one option is stronger. The verbal presentation depends entirely on the broker's memory of the analysis they did days or weeks earlier. When the CFO asks a pointed question about an exclusion, the broker either has the answer or says "let me get back to you" — and momentum stalls.

Qumis changes this by connecting the presentation phase directly to the analysis that preceded it. The [Proposal Generator](/platform/coming-soon/proposal-generator) produces a branded proposal you can fine-tune in [Qumis Studio](/platform/coming-soon/qumis-studio). [Chats](/platform/chats) turns that proposal into an interactive microsite you can share or screen-share. And when client questions arise, [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) gives you instant access to the citation-backed findings that answer them.

The result is a presentation phase where you never lose credibility, never lose momentum, and never have to circle back.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Build the proposal">
    Use the [Proposal Generator](/platform/coming-soon/proposal-generator) to produce the formal proposal document. It applies your organization's branded templates and structures the output with an executive summary, coverage and premium comparison, key terms breakdown, and your recommendation with rationale. Configure your templates and branding in [Branding and Themes](/platform/branding-and-themes) so every proposal reflects your firm's identity.

    Once the Proposal Generator produces the initial draft, open it in [Qumis Studio](/platform/coming-soon/qumis-studio) to fine-tune the content. Studio is a full document editor inside Qumis where you can adjust language, add charts, graphs, tables, and other graphics, and tailor the presentation to the specific audience for this account. Export the finished proposal as Word or PDF for client delivery, or keep it in Qumis to feed the next step.

    <Tip>
      Build a Proposal Generator template for each major account type your team handles (middle market, large commercial, specialty). The template defines the structure and branding. This makes proposal quality consistent across your team regardless of who's handling the account.
    </Tip>

    → [Proposal Generator](/platform/coming-soon/proposal-generator) · [Qumis Studio](/platform/coming-soon/qumis-studio) · [Branding and Themes](/platform/branding-and-themes)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Deliver the presentation">
    You have two options for presenting to the client. Download a branded, polished PDF of the proposal directly from Qumis for a traditional format you can email or screen-share. Or use [Chats](/platform/chats) to generate a self-contained HTML microsite from your proposal output — a branded, interactive presentation page the client can navigate through in their browser without any special software.

    **Microsite generation prompt:**
    ```
    Using the attached proposal, create a self-contained HTML page that
    presents this quote comparison as an interactive microsite. Include:

    1. A fixed navigation bar with anchor links to each section
    2. An executive summary with our recommendation and top three reasons
    3. A side-by-side coverage comparison table organized by dimension
       (limits, deductibles, coverage grants, exclusions, conditions)
    4. A premium breakdown showing each carrier's total and the change
       from expiring
    5. A recommendation section with the rationale and next steps

    Use a clean, professional design. All content should come from the
    proposal — do not add or fabricate any coverage data.
    ```

    <Tip>
      Ask the Chat to structure the microsite with navigation anchors for each section so you can jump directly to the relevant section when a stakeholder asks a question during the presentation.
    </Tip>

    → [Chats](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Handle client questions and objections">
    Run [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) ahead of the meeting to get fully educated on the options you're presenting. The structured coverage analysis identifies every material difference across quotes, so you walk in already knowing the answers to most questions. The QumisScore benchmarking across six dimensions gives you a framework for explaining why one option is stronger than another.

    When the client raises a question you didn't anticipate, open a [Chat](/platform/chats) session to query the underlying quote documents in real-time. For broader questions about carrier financial strength or market pricing, use [Web Search](/platform/research/web-search) to pull in carrier ratings, regulatory context, or market benchmarks.

    <Tip>
      Compare Documents is the strongest tool for this step because client questions are almost always comparative in nature. [Policy Analysis](/platform/summarize) can also analyze individual quotes in depth, but Compare Documents is the better starting point since it directly addresses side-by-side tradeoffs.
    </Tip>

    → [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) · [Chats](/platform/chats) · [Web Search](/platform/research/web-search)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Secure the client's decision">
    If carriers come back with revised quotes during negotiations, upload the new versions to the [Document Vault](/platform/document-vault) and re-run [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) to see exactly what changed. Use [Chats](/platform/chats) to draft counter-requests that cite the specific carrier language and state exactly what needs to change, the same way you did during the negotiation step in the [Marketing the Account](/playbooks/broking/marketing-account) playbook.

    <Note>
      The QumisScore is especially useful during late-stage negotiations. When a carrier revises terms to secure the business, re-running the comparison lets you instantly see whether the revision actually closed the gap or just moved numbers around without addressing the underlying coverage issue.
    </Note>

    → [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) · [Chats](/platform/chats)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Measuring success

| Metric | What to track | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Proposals generated with Qumis | Percentage of placements where the proposal was built with Proposal Generator | 80%+ of placements |
| Client questions answered on the spot | Percentage of client questions resolved during the presentation vs. requiring follow-up | 90%+ |
| Time from last quote to client presentation | Business days from final quote received to proposal delivered | 50% reduction vs. baseline |
| Rounds of revision before binding | Average number of back-and-forth rounds before the client signs off | Decrease by 1–2 rounds |
| E&O documentation | Percentage of placements with a documented recommendation and rationale on file | 100% |

<Note>
  The Compare Documents report and Proposal Generator output together serve as your placement documentation. They record what was analyzed, what was recommended, and why — which is valuable for E&O defense, renewal benchmarking, and stewardship reviews.
</Note>
