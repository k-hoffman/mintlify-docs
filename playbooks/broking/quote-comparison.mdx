## The strategy

Once quotes are in hand and you've analyzed the options, the quote presentation phase is where you turn that analysis into a client decision. This playbook walks through the five steps of presenting quotes to an insured — from building the proposal through issuing binding instructions — and maps the Qumis features that support each step.

<Note>
  The analytical work of organizing, comparing, and evaluating competing quotes is covered in the [Marketing the Account](/playbooks/broking/marketing-account) playbook under the "Negotiate terms and quotes" step. This playbook picks up where that one leaves off.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Build the proposal">
    The broker assembles a formal proposal document that presents the options in a clear, digestible format. For middle market and larger accounts, this typically includes an executive summary, a side-by-side coverage and premium comparison, a breakdown of key terms and conditions by line, a summary of what changed from the expiring program (if renewal), and the broker's recommendation with rationale. The proposal is designed to help the client make an informed decision without needing to read the underlying quote documents.

    **Qumis features:**

    - [Proposal Generator](/platform/coming-soon/proposal-generator) — Combines policy data, market research, and the organization's branded templates to produce professional, client-ready proposals. It pulls in coverage comparisons, market insights, and competitive positioning directly from your Qumis workspace, so the proposal is grounded in the actual analysis work completed during the marketing phase rather than built from scratch. Output can be exported as Word or PDF, ready for client delivery.

    → [Proposal Generator](/platform/coming-soon/proposal-generator)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Deliver the presentation">
    The broker presents the proposal to the client, usually in a scheduled meeting (in-person or virtual). They walk through the options, explain the tradeoffs, highlight where the recommended option is stronger, and address coverage nuances the client needs to understand. For larger accounts, this may involve multiple stakeholders — the CFO, risk manager, legal, operations — each with different concerns. The broker acts as the advisor, not just a messenger.

    **Qumis features:**

    - [Chats](/platform/chats) — Use a Chat session to generate a self-contained HTML microsite from your Proposal Generator output. The Chat reads the proposal data and produces a branded, interactive presentation page — complete with an executive summary, side-by-side coverage comparisons, and your recommendation — that you can share as a link or screen-share during the client meeting. This gives the client a polished digital experience rather than a static PDF, and the self-contained format means it works without any hosting or special software.

    **Microsite generation prompt:**
    ```
    Using the attached proposal, create a self-contained HTML page that
    presents this quote comparison as an interactive microsite. Include:

    1. A fixed navigation bar with anchor links to each section
    2. An executive summary with our recommendation and top three reasons
    3. A side-by-side coverage comparison table organized by dimension
       (limits, deductibles, coverage grants, exclusions, conditions)
    4. A premium breakdown showing each carrier's total and the change
       from expiring
    5. A recommendation section with the rationale and next steps

    Use a clean, professional design. The audience is a risk manager and
    CFO. All content should come from the proposal — do not add or
    fabricate any coverage data.
    ```

    <Tip>
      Ask the Chat to structure the microsite with navigation anchors for each section so you can jump directly to the relevant section when a stakeholder asks a question during the presentation.
    </Tip>

    → [Chats](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Handle client questions and objections">
    After the presentation, the client typically has follow-up questions: "Why is Carrier A more expensive?", "Can we get the sublimit on X increased?", "What happens if we raise the retention?", "How does this compare to what we had last year?" The broker needs to address these quickly and confidently.

    **Qumis features:**

    - [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) — Should be used ahead of the meeting to get fully educated on the options being presented. The structured coverage analysis identifies every material difference in coverage, limits, exclusions, and terms, so the broker walks into the presentation already knowing the answers to most comparison questions the client will raise. The QumisScore benchmarking across six dimensions also gives the broker a framework for explaining why one option is stronger than another.
    - [Chats](/platform/chats) — When the client raises a question the broker didn't anticipate, they can quickly query the underlying quote documents in real-time to get a precise, cited answer.
    - [Web Search](/platform/research/web-search) — Supplements by pulling in carrier ratings, regulatory context, or market benchmarks when the client asks broader questions about a carrier's financial strength, claims reputation, or market pricing.

    <Tip>
      Compare Documents is the strongest tool for this step because client questions are almost always comparative in nature. [Policy Analysis](/platform/summarize) can also analyze individual quotes in depth, but Compare Documents is the better starting point since it directly addresses side-by-side tradeoffs.
    </Tip>

    → [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) · [Chats](/platform/chats) · [Web Search](/platform/research/web-search)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Secure the client's decision">
    The broker works through any remaining concerns, negotiates final adjustments with carriers if needed, and gets the client to a binding decision. This may involve multiple rounds of back-and-forth — revised quotes, adjusted terms, updated proposals — before the client signs off. For larger accounts with multiple lines, the client may approve some lines immediately and ask for further negotiation on others.

    Qumis doesn't directly address this step. This is a negotiation and relationship management exercise. The tools used in the marketing phase ([Compare Documents](/platform/compare), [Coverage Table](/platform/populate), [Chats](/platform/chats)) remain useful if the broker needs to re-evaluate revised quotes that come back from carriers during this back-and-forth.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Issue binding instructions">
    Once the client has made their selection, the broker sends formal binding instructions to the carrier(s), confirming effective dates, coverage terms, named insureds, and any outstanding subjectivities. This is the handoff from the sales/advisory phase to the operational/servicing phase.

    **Qumis features:**

    - [Chrome Extension](/platform/coming-soon/chrome-extension) — Lets the broker access Qumis capabilities directly from the browser without leaving their current workflow. The broker can use the extension to streamline getting the insured's bind order over to the carrier — triggering actions directly from the browser when working in a DMS or email — rather than toggling between systems.

    → [Chrome Extension](/platform/coming-soon/chrome-extension)
  </Step>
</Steps>
