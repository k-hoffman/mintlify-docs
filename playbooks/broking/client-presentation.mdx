This workflow shows how to combine multiple Qumis capabilities to produce a polished, client-ready presentation or coverage review document. Whether you're presenting at a renewal meeting, a coverage review, or a stewardship meeting, this workflow gets you from raw policy documents to presentation-ready analysis in a fraction of the time.

---

## The workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate the analysis">
    Start by running the reports that provide the raw analysis for your presentation. Which reports you generate depends on the meeting type:

    | Meeting Type | Reports to Generate |
    |---|---|
    | **Renewal review** | Comparison Report (expiring vs. renewal) + Coverage Table |
    | **Coverage review** | Single Policy Analysis for each major line + Coverage Table |
    | **New business proposal** | Comparison Report (competing quotes) + Coverage Table |
    | **Stewardship meeting** | Portfolio summary using Populate + Coverage Table |

    → [Choosing the Right Report](/platform/what-can-you-do)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize for your audience">
    Raw Qumis output is thorough — sometimes more thorough than your audience needs. Ask Qumis to tailor the analysis:

    - *For a CFO:* "Summarize the key coverage differences in three bullet points, focusing on financial impact."
    - *For a risk manager:* "Provide a detailed comparison of limits, exclusions, and gaps, organized by coverage line."
    - *For a board presentation:* "Create a two-paragraph executive summary of our insurance program's strengths and the three most significant gaps."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build the narrative">
    A great presentation tells a story. Use Qumis to draft the narrative elements:

    - *Opening:* "Draft a one-paragraph summary of why this renewal matters, given the current market conditions and the changes we're seeing."
    - *Key findings:* "What are the three most important things the client needs to understand about their coverage?"
    - *Recommendations:* "Based on the coverage gaps identified, what actions should we recommend?"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Extract supporting data">
    Use Populate to extract the specific data points you need for tables, charts, or comparisons in your presentation:

    - Premium by coverage line
    - Limits and deductible summaries
    - Year-over-year changes
    - Coverage gap inventory

    → [Coverage Table](/platform/populate)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Final polish">
    Review the Qumis output, apply your professional judgment, and assemble the final deliverable. Qumis provides the analytical foundation — you add the client context, relationship insight, and strategic recommendations that only you can provide.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Presentation templates by meeting type

### Renewal meeting

1. **Market context** — Brief overview of relevant market conditions (you provide this context)
2. **Coverage comparison** — Qumis Comparison Report highlights: key changes from expiring
3. **Coverage gaps** — Issues identified through policy analysis or coverage verification
4. **Recommendations** — Your professional advice on how to address gaps
5. **Next steps** — Timeline and action items

### Coverage review / stewardship

1. **Program summary** — Coverage Table showing the full program at a glance
2. **By-line analysis** — Key findings from Single Policy Analysis for each major line
3. **Gap analysis** — Coverage gaps identified through verification
4. **Claims performance** — (You provide claims data)
5. **Improvement recommendations** — Based on gap analysis and market opportunities

### New business proposal

1. **Current program assessment** — Analysis showing gaps in the prospect's existing coverage
2. **Proposed solution** — Comparison Report showing how your proposal addresses those gaps
3. **Coverage comparison** — Side-by-side analysis of current vs. proposed
4. **Value proposition** — Summary of coverage improvements

## Time savings

The traditional approach to preparing a client presentation — manually reviewing policies, building comparison spreadsheets, drafting summaries — typically takes 4-8 hours for a comprehensive renewal review.

With Qumis, the analytical work takes 30-60 minutes. You invest your time in the high-value activities that clients pay for: interpreting the analysis, developing recommendations, and presenting with confidence.

## Tips for Your Role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    Focus your presentations on competitive differentiation. Use Comparison Reports to show clients exactly where your recommended program outperforms alternatives. Lead with the coverage advantages, then address pricing — the analysis gives you the ammunition to justify premium differences with specific coverage improvements.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    Build stewardship presentations that demonstrate ongoing value. Use year-over-year Comparison Reports to show how you've improved the program over time. Coverage Tables give clients a clear snapshot of their full program, and gap analysis shows you're proactively identifying risks before they become claims.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    When presenting coverage positions to insureds or counsel, use Claims Analysis Reports as your foundation. The structured format — insuring agreement analysis, exclusion review, conditions check — provides a defensible framework that shows your analysis was thorough and methodical.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    Present portfolio reviews to leadership using Coverage Tables for the program overview and Contract Compliance reports to demonstrate your insurance program meets all contractual obligations. This data-driven approach gives executives confidence in the risk transfer strategy.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
