## The strategy

Marketing an account is where broking expertise meets execution. The process spans everything from assembling the submission package to negotiating the final bound terms, and each step creates an opportunity to differentiate yourself through better analysis, sharper submissions, and more informed carrier conversations.

Most brokers run this process on a combination of institutional memory, manual document reads, and spreadsheet comparisons. The quality varies depending on who's handling the account and how much time they have. The result is inconsistent submissions, reactive negotiations, and coverage decisions that rely more on pricing than on actual policy language.

Qumis changes this by embedding structured analysis into every stage of the marketing lifecycle. You gather submission data faster using automated extraction. You assess risk with citation-backed policy analysis. You build submissions grounded in specific coverage findings. You select markets using structured data rather than relationships alone. And when quotes come back, you negotiate from a position of analytical authority — comparing terms across carriers with precision and supporting every ask with cited policy language.

The result is a placement process that's faster, more rigorous, and produces better outcomes for your client.

## The Qumis workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Gather submission information">
    Start by building a complete picture of the client's existing program. Upload the expiring policies, endorsements, dec pages, and schedules into the [Document Vault](/platform/document-vault), then use the [Coverage Table](/platform/populate) to extract structured data from every document at once.

    Create a folder for the account and renewal cycle (e.g., `Acme Corp - 2026 Renewal / Expiring Program`). Having the complete expiring in one place means Qumis can reference every document when you ask questions downstream.

    **Coverage Table extraction prompt:**
    ```
    From each policy in this program, extract: carrier, policy number,
    effective dates, coverage type, per-occurrence limit, aggregate limit,
    deductible/retention, key sublimits, and annual premium. Include all
    endorsements that modify limits or coverage scope.
    ```

    The Coverage Table gives you a structured inventory of the entire program — limits, deductibles, sublimits, and key terms — in a format you can review at a glance and share with your team. This becomes the foundation for every step that follows.

    <Tip>
      Upload endorsements as separate documents rather than as part of a single combined PDF. Qumis can reference them more precisely when they're individually uploaded, and your citations will point to the specific endorsement rather than a page number in a merged file.
    </Tip>

    → [Coverage Table](/platform/populate)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assess risk and develop strategy">
    With the program data in hand, use [Policy Analysis](/platform/summarize) to run a deep review of each major line of coverage. Then open a [Chat](/platform/chats) session with the program documents to ask follow-up questions and develop your marketing strategy. Supplement your analysis with [Web Search](/platform/research/web-search) to research market conditions, industry exposures, and emerging risks relevant to the account.

    **Policy Analysis focus areas:**

    - Coverage gaps and exclusions that are unusually broad or restrictive
    - Endorsements that narrow the base form versus those that broaden it
    - Sublimits that appear inadequate relative to the risk profile
    - Coordination issues between underlying and excess layers
    - Definitions or conditions that could create problems at the time of a claim

    **Risk assessment Chat prompt:**
    ```
    Based on the Policy Analysis findings for this program, identify the
    top five coverage issues that should drive our marketing strategy.
    For each issue: (1) cite the specific policy language, (2) explain
    why it matters for this client's risk profile, and (3) recommend
    what we should request from carriers to address it. Prioritize by
    impact — which gaps create the most exposure if left unaddressed?
    ```

    **Web Search for market context:**
    ```
    Current market conditions for [line of business] in [industry sector].
    Rate trends, capacity changes, and any emerging coverage issues
    affecting this class of business.
    ```

    This step produces your marketing roadmap: you now know exactly what to ask carriers for, which coverage areas to emphasize, and where the expiring program falls short.

    <Note>
      The combination of Policy Analysis and follow-up Chat questions is powerful. Policy Analysis gives you the structured findings. Chat lets you explore the implications, ask "what if" questions, and develop the narrative that connects the findings to your client's specific situation.
    </Note>

    → [Policy Analysis](/platform/summarize) · [Chats](/platform/chats) · [Web Search](/platform/research/web-search)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Build the submission">
    Use [Chats](/platform/chats) to draft a submission narrative that goes beyond the standard ACORD application. Most submissions arrive at an underwriter's desk as a stack of forms with no context. A narrative that explains the risk, highlights the coverage needs you've identified, and positions the account strategically will get better attention and better terms.

    **Submission narrative prompt:**
    ```
    Based on the coverage analysis findings, draft a submission narrative
    for this account that I can include with the ACORD application.
    The narrative should:

    1. Describe the insured's operations and risk profile in 3–4
       sentences.
    2. Summarize the expiring program structure (carrier, limits,
       key terms).
    3. Identify the specific coverage enhancements we are requesting,
       with justification for each based on the policy analysis findings.
    4. Note any positive risk characteristics (loss history, risk
       management practices, contractual requirements) that support
       favorable terms.

    Write it as a professional submission cover letter from a broker
    to an underwriter. Keep it to one page.
    ```

    <Tip>
      Save your best submission narrative prompts to the [Prompt Library](/platform/prompt-library), organized by line of business. A strong GL submission narrative reads differently than a professional liability narrative — build a library of templates your team can reuse.
    </Tip>

    → [Chats](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select markets">
    Use [Batch Extraction](/platform/batch-extraction) to pull structured program data that feeds your market selection process and any upstream BI tools your team uses for carrier appetite matching. Supplement with [Web Search](/platform/research/web-search) to research carrier appetite, recent market entries or exits, and specialty markets that align with the coverage needs your analysis identified.

    **Batch Extraction prompt:**
    ```
    From each policy and quote document in this account folder, extract:
    carrier name, line of business, policy form (ISO or manuscript),
    limits structure, deductible/retention, key endorsements, premium,
    and any unique coverage features or restrictions. Output as a
    structured table.
    ```

    Use the extraction output to match markets to needs. Instead of submitting to every carrier you have a relationship with, target the carriers whose forms and appetite align with the specific coverage enhancements you've identified.

    For example, if your analysis revealed that the expiring program has an overly broad professional services exclusion, prioritize carriers known for offering a more favorable version of that endorsement. If the analysis flagged inadequate cyber sublimits, include a standalone cyber market in your submission strategy.

    <Note>
      The structured data from Batch Extraction also feeds downstream workflows — renewal tracking, benchmarking, and portfolio analytics. Extracting it here means you're building institutional data while doing placement work.
    </Note>

    → [Batch Extraction](/platform/batch-extraction) · [Web Search](/platform/research/web-search)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit to carriers">
    Assemble the full submission package and send it to your selected markets. This step happens outside of Qumis — through your agency management system, email, or carrier portals — but the quality of what you're sending is dramatically higher because of the work in the preceding steps.

    **Your Qumis-powered submission package includes:**

    - ACORD applications (completed and signed)
    - Current dec pages and policy forms
    - Five-year loss history with loss runs valued within 60 days
    - Supplemental applications (by line of business)
    - Exposure schedules (SOVs, vehicle schedules, headcount, revenue)
    - **Submission cover narrative** generated from your coverage analysis, positioning the account and highlighting specific coverage needs
    - **Coverage enhancement request list** with citations to the expiring policy language that needs improvement
    - **Program structure summary** from the Coverage Table, mapping the full program so carriers can see where they fit

    <Tip>
      Underwriters notice when a broker has analyzed the expiring before submitting. It signals that you understand the risk, have a point of view on what needs to change, and will be a productive negotiating partner. This often results in better initial quotes and fewer rounds of back-and-forth.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Negotiate terms and quotes">
    As quotes come back from carriers, upload each one to the Document Vault and use Qumis to analyze, compare, and negotiate with precision. This step uses multiple Qumis tools together to turn the negotiation cycle from a manual, spreadsheet-driven process into a structured, citation-backed workflow.

    **Compare Documents** — Run a side-by-side comparison of all quotes against each other and against the expiring:

    ```
    Compare all uploaded carrier quotes against each other and against
    the expiring policy. For each material difference in coverage terms,
    limits, deductibles, exclusions, and endorsements, cite the specific
    language from each quote. Organize findings by coverage dimension,
    not by carrier. Flag any terms in a quote that are worse than the
    expiring.
    ```

    **QumisScore** — Use the proprietary quality indicator to quickly identify which quotes provide the strongest coverage and where the most significant gaps lie. The QumisScore cuts through the noise on complex multi-carrier comparisons by surfacing the findings that matter most.

    **Coverage Table** — Generate a comparison table showing limits, deductibles, and key terms across all quotes side by side. Download the table to Excel for further analysis or to share with your team and client.

    **Negotiate with precision** — For every term that doesn't meet your requirements, use Chat to draft a counter-request that cites the specific quote language and states exactly what needs to change:

    ```
    Based on the quote comparison, generate counter-negotiation points
    for [carrier name]. For each term that is worse than the expiring
    or that doesn't meet our coverage enhancement requests: (1) cite
    the carrier's quote language, (2) state what we need instead, and
    (3) provide the justification. Prioritize the asks by importance
    to the client. Format as a professional email to the underwriter.
    ```

    This step feeds directly into the [Quote Comparison](/playbooks/broking/quote-comparison) playbook, which covers the full process of analyzing quotes, building a client-ready recommendation, and presenting the comparison. Use both playbooks together for the strongest placement outcomes.

    → [Compare Documents](/platform/compare) · [Coverage Table](/platform/populate) · [Chats](/platform/chats)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Prompt templates by line of business

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Commercial Property">
    **Pre-marketing analysis prompt:**
    ```
    Review this commercial property policy including all endorsements.
    Identify: (1) valuation basis and whether it appears adequate,
    (2) coinsurance provisions and penalty risk, (3) sublimits for
    flood, wind, earthquake, and equipment breakdown relative to the
    schedule of values, (4) business interruption coverage including
    waiting period and extended period of indemnity, (5) any
    exclusions or limitations on ordinance or law coverage,
    (6) vacancy provisions, and (7) any endorsements that restrict
    coverage from the base form. Flag the top five issues to address
    in the marketing.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="General Liability">
    **Pre-marketing analysis prompt:**
    ```
    Review this commercial general liability policy including all
    endorsements. Identify: (1) additional insured endorsement scope
    and whether it includes ongoing and completed operations,
    (2) products-completed operations aggregate and whether it's
    dedicated or shared, (3) scope of the pollution exclusion and
    any exceptions, (4) professional services or technology
    exclusions, (5) contractual liability coverage and any
    limitations, (6) per-project or per-location aggregates if
    applicable, and (7) any endorsements that restrict coverage
    from the ISO base form. Flag the top five issues to address
    in the marketing.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Umbrella / Excess">
    **Pre-marketing analysis prompt:**
    ```
    Review this umbrella/excess liability program including all
    layers. Identify: (1) whether each layer is true follow-form
    or has its own coverage terms, (2) any exclusions in the
    umbrella/excess that are not in the underlying, creating a
    gap in the tower, (3) drop-down provisions and when they
    trigger, (4) defense cost treatment (inside or outside limits)
    at each layer, (5) self-insured retention requirements,
    (6) scheduled underlying policy requirements and whether
    they match the actual underlying program, and (7) any
    differences in named insured or additional insured scope
    between layers. Flag the top five issues and any gaps in
    the tower structure.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Professional Liability / E&O">
    **Pre-marketing analysis prompt:**
    ```
    Review this professional liability/E&O policy including all
    endorsements. Identify: (1) definition of "professional
    services" and whether it covers all services the insured
    actually provides, (2) prior acts date and any gap in
    continuity, (3) defense cost treatment and impact on
    available limits, (4) consent to settle provisions,
    (5) definition of "claim" and whether it includes regulatory
    proceedings, (6) exclusions for specific services, industries,
    or client types, and (7) extended reporting period options
    and cost. Flag the top five issues to address in the marketing.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Cyber Liability">
    **Pre-marketing analysis prompt:**
    ```
    Review this cyber liability policy including all endorsements.
    Identify: (1) first-party vs. third-party coverage scope and
    any gaps, (2) ransomware/extortion coverage and sublimits,
    (3) business interruption trigger and waiting period,
    (4) social engineering/funds transfer fraud coverage,
    (5) regulatory proceeding coverage including fines and
    penalties, (6) vendor/supply chain incident coverage scope,
    (7) war and infrastructure exclusions and how broadly they're
    drafted, and (8) retroactive date and prior acts coverage.
    Flag the top five issues to address in the marketing.
    ```
  </Tab>

## Structuring the submission package

Use this framework to organize every submission you send out. The Qumis-powered components are the differentiators that set your submission apart.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Standard submission components (table stakes)">
    These are the components every broker includes. They get you in the door but don't differentiate you.

    - ACORD applications (completed and signed)
    - Current dec pages and policy forms
    - Five-year loss history with loss runs valued within 60 days
    - Supplemental applications (by line of business)
    - Schedule of values (property), vehicle schedule (auto), employee count (WC)
    - Financial statements (if required by carrier)
    - Prior carrier information and expiring premium
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Qumis-powered submission enhancements">
    These are the components that elevate your submission and show underwriters you've done the analytical work.

    - **Submission cover narrative** generated from your coverage analysis, positioning the account and highlighting specific coverage needs
    - **Coverage enhancement request list** with citations to the expiring policy language that needs improvement, giving underwriters a clear target rather than vague asks
    - **Market context from Web Search** that grounds your coverage requests in current market conditions, industry trends, and emerging risk factors
    - **Program structure summary** from the Coverage Table that maps the full tower (if applicable) and identifies where each carrier sits, making it easy for excess markets to understand the program without reading every underlying policy

    <Tip>
      The combination of a clear narrative and cited coverage requests signals to underwriters that you've done the work. This often results in faster turnaround and more competitive initial quotes because underwriters can focus their time on pricing rather than trying to figure out what you actually want.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Measuring success

| Metric | What to track | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Coverage Tables generated | Number of accounts where you built a Coverage Table before marketing | 100% of accounts above $25K premium |
| Submission narratives included | Percentage of submissions sent with a Qumis-generated cover narrative | 80%+ |
| Quote response rate | Percentage of carriers who return a quote (vs. decline or NTU) | 10–15% improvement over baseline |
| Rounds of negotiation | Average number of back-and-forth rounds before binding | Decrease by 1–2 rounds |
| Time from quote receipt to comparison | Hours from last quote received to structured comparison completed | 50% reduction vs. manual process |
| Coverage enhancements achieved | Number of requested improvements that carriers agreed to | Track per placement |

<Note>
  The first two steps — gathering submission data with the Coverage Table and assessing risk with Policy Analysis — are the highest-leverage activities in this playbook. Even if you don't use every Qumis tool on every account, these two steps alone will change how you approach every placement because they force you to understand the expiring program before you market it.
</Note>
