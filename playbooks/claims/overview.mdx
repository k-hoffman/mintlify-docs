Claims are where insurance promises get tested. When a client reports a loss, the speed and accuracy of your coverage analysis determines whether they feel protected or abandoned. When a carrier denies a claim, the quality of your rebuttal determines whether the client recovers or absorbs the loss.

Most brokerages treat claims support as reactive. A loss comes in, the broker forwards it to the carrier, and the carrier drives the process. Qumis gives your team the tools to be proactive and authoritative at every stage: analyzing coverage before the carrier does, identifying every policy that might respond, and building legally grounded rebuttals when a denial doesn't hold up.

## Who these playbooks are for

These playbooks are designed for **claims advocates, account managers, producers, and anyone on the brokerage team who supports clients through the claims process**. They're also valuable for risk managers and in-house claims teams at the client level who use Qumis directly. They assume familiarity with the Claims Analysis report, the Chat feature within reports, and Legal Search.

<Warning>
  Qumis provides coverage analysis and legal research tools. It does not provide legal advice.
</Warning>

## How to use these playbooks

Each playbook follows the same structure:

- **The strategy** explains why this claims activity matters and how Qumis changes the workflow.
- **The Qumis workflow** walks you through the exact steps using the Claims Analysis report, Chat, and Legal Search.
- **Templates and prompts** give you ready-to-use language for analysis, communication, and documentation.
- **Measuring success** defines what good looks like for your claims advocacy practice.

These playbooks can be used independently based on the situation, but they often work in sequence: a loss is reported (Policy Identification), coverage is analyzed (Coverage Analysis), and if the carrier denies, you rebut (Rebutting a Denial).

## How claims connects to the full lifecycle

If you've been running the [Servicing Playbooks](/playbooks/servicing/overview), you already have the client's full program organized in the Document Vault and a history of coverage questions that may be directly relevant to the claim. If you used the [Broking Playbooks](/playbooks/broking/overview), the binding review you conducted documented the exact coverage terms in effect. This accumulated intelligence gives you a head start on every claims analysis.
