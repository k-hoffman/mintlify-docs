Renewals are the revenue engine of every brokerage. The quality of your renewal process determines whether accounts grow, stay flat, or walk. A strong renewal isn't just a re-placement of last year's program. It's an opportunity to demonstrate that you've been paying attention all year, that you understand the client's evolving risk profile, and that you have a point of view on how their program should change.

Qumis gives your team the analytical foundation to approach every renewal with the depth and precision that clients notice. From the first strategy conversation through the final presentation, every stage is powered by structured, citation-backed intelligence that replaces guesswork with evidence.

## Who these playbooks are for

These playbooks are designed for **producers, account managers, and placement specialists** who manage the end-to-end renewal cycle. They assume you have access to Qumis and are comfortable with the core features.

<Note>
  If you're new to Qumis, start with the [Get Started](/get-started/welcome) guides first. These renewal playbooks build on foundational skills and are most effective when combined with the [Broking Playbooks](/playbooks/broking/overview) and [Servicing Playbooks](/playbooks/servicing/overview).
</Note>

## How to use these playbooks

Each playbook follows the same structure:

- **The strategy** explains why this stage of the renewal matters and how Qumis changes the approach.
- **The Qumis workflow** walks you through the exact steps, features, and prompts.
- **Templates and prompts** give you ready-to-use language for analysis, communication, and documentation.
- **Measuring success** defines what good looks like so your team can track performance.

These three playbooks follow the natural sequence of a renewal. Use them in order for the most impact: strategy feeds marketing, and marketing feeds the presentation.

## How renewals connect to the full lifecycle

If you've been running the [Servicing Playbooks](/playbooks/servicing/overview) throughout the policy period, your renewal starts in a stronger position than any competitor's. The stewardship health checks, coverage Q&As, and contract reviews you've conducted all year become direct inputs to your renewal strategy. You're not starting from scratch at 120 days out. You're building on 12 months of documented insight.

<Tip>
  The brokerages that win on retention don't treat renewals as a separate event. They treat the entire policy period as renewal preparation. Every stewardship finding, every coverage question answered, and every contract reviewed during servicing is an input that makes the renewal strategy sharper and the presentation more credible.
</Tip>
