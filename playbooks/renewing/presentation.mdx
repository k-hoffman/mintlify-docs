## The strategy

The renewal presentation is the moment where all your work becomes visible to the client. The quality of the presentation determines whether the client sees you as a strategic advisor or a vendor who collected some quotes.

Most of the analytical work is already done by this point — the strategy identified what to fix, the marketing process addressed it, and the quotes are analyzed. The presentation challenge isn't generating more analysis. It's assembling what you already have into a clear narrative with a confident recommendation. Qumis helps you do that by structuring the story arc, generating the client-facing proposal, and giving you a reference sheet for the detailed coverage comparisons so your team can speak to the specifics during the meeting.

## Prerequisites

<Info>
  This playbook follows the [Renewal Marketing](/playbooks/renewing/marketing) playbook. You should have a finalized options matrix with all carrier quotes analyzed, renewal objectives scored, and an internal recommendation ready before building the presentation.
</Info>

Before starting, you should have:

- A completed final options matrix from the marketing phase
- All carrier quotes uploaded and analyzed in the Document Vault
- The renewal strategy document (for framing)
- The expiring program for comparison
- A scheduled meeting with the client's decision-makers

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate the renewal story arc">
    Before building the presentation, define the narrative. A strong renewal presentation tells a story: where the client started (the expiring program and its issues), what you did about it (the marketing process and objectives), what the market delivered (the options), and what you recommend (your pick and why).

    **Narrative framing prompt:**
    ```
    Based on the renewal strategy, the marketing results, and the
    final options matrix, generate a renewal presentation narrative
    that follows this arc:

    1. WHERE WE STARTED: Summarize the expiring program's key
       strengths and the issues identified in the renewal strategy.
       Reference specific stewardship findings, coverage gaps, and
       renewal objectives. This reminds the client why the renewal
       was approached the way it was.

    2. WHAT WE DID: Briefly describe the marketing approach. How
       many carriers were approached? What specific improvements
       were requested? What was the negotiation focus? This
       demonstrates the effort and strategy behind the results.

    3. WHAT THE MARKET DELIVERED: Summarize the quoting results at
       a high level. Were objectives met? Was the market receptive?
       Were there surprises (better terms than expected, unexpected
       restrictions, capacity challenges)?

    4. WHAT WE RECOMMEND: State the recommendation clearly. Which
       option and why? Frame it in terms of the client's stated
       priorities from the strategy kick-off meeting.

    Write as a presentation script. Each section should be three
    to five sentences. The tone is confident and advisory.
    ```

    → [Chat](/platform/chats)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use the Proposal Generator to create the quote presentation">
    Use the [Proposal Generator](/platform/coming-soon/proposal-generator) to build the client-facing renewal proposal. The Proposal Generator pulls from the quotes and analysis already in the Document Vault to produce a structured presentation that includes the executive summary, line-by-line options, premium comparisons, and your recommendation.

    Feed the story arc from Step 1 into the Proposal Generator to frame the narrative sections. The generator handles the formatting and structure — your job is to review the output and ensure the recommendation language reflects how your team wants to position each option.

    → [Proposal Generator](/platform/coming-soon/proposal-generator)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate a detailed options crib sheet for the team">
    The Proposal Generator creates the client-facing deliverable. This step creates the internal reference document that the presenting team uses to handle questions during the meeting — the detailed coverage comparisons, risk context, and trade-off analysis that may not all appear in the proposal but that the team needs at their fingertips.

    **Crib sheet prompt:**
    ```
    Using the final options matrix, generate an internal reference
    document for the presenting team. Structure as follows:

    FOR EACH LINE OF BUSINESS:

    1. OPTIONS TABLE: Each carrier option with premium, limits,
       deductibles, and key terms.

    2. COVERAGE COMPARISON: For the three to five most significant
       coverage differences between options, show the exact language
       from each carrier's quote side by side. Explain in plain
       language what each difference means for the client.

    3. RENEWAL OBJECTIVES SCORECARD: For each renewal objective
       from the strategy, show which options met it and which
       didn't (Met / Partially Met / Not Met).

    4. LINE-LEVEL RECOMMENDATION: For each line, state which
       option the team recommends and the primary reason.

    THEN, AT THE PROGRAM LEVEL:

    5. TOTAL PROGRAM VIEW: The recommended program across all
       lines, total premium, and the net coverage position vs.
       expiring (improved, lateral, or reduced, and why).

    6. COST-OF-SAVINGS ANALYSIS: If the recommended option is
       not the cheapest, show every coverage difference where the
       cheaper option provides less protection. Quantify the
       exposure shift where possible.

    7. RISK CONTEXT: For the one to two coverage differences that
       matter most, include a relevant case, claim scenario, or
       industry example that illustrates why the difference matters.
       Present in two to three sentences a non-insurance audience
       can understand.

    This document is for internal use during the presentation
    meeting. It should be comprehensive enough that the team can
    answer any client question about coverage differences, trade-
    offs, or risk context without needing to look anything up.
    ```

    → [Chat](/platform/chats)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Measuring success

| Metric | What to track | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Proposals built with Proposal Generator | Percentage of renewal presentations built using the Proposal Generator | Track adoption across accounts |
| Client satisfaction with process | Feedback on clarity of the presentation and confidence in the decision | Track via direct feedback or NPS |
| Crib sheets generated in Qumis | Percentage of renewal presentations where the team had a Qumis-generated internal reference document | 100% for remarketed lines |
| Renewal objectives referenced in proposal | Percentage of proposals that explicitly tied options back to the stated renewal objectives from the strategy phase | 100% |
