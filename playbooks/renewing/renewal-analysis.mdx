# Renewal Analysis

The renewal is the highest-stakes moment in the client relationship. This workflow shows how to use Qumis to produce a thorough renewal analysis that identifies every coverage change, flags gaps, and supports your professional recommendation — in a fraction of the time it takes manually.

---

## The renewal workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Compare expiring vs. renewal">
    Upload both the expiring policy and the renewal quote (or issued policy). Run a Comparison Report focused on the differences that matter:

    *"Compare these two policies. Identify every change in limits, deductibles, exclusions, conditions, and endorsements. For each change, explain the practical impact on coverage."*

    This produces the foundation of your renewal review — a structured analysis of everything that changed and what it means.

    → [Comparison Report](/platform/compare)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the Coverage Table">
    Run a Coverage Table on the renewal policy to produce a visual summary of the coverage program. This becomes the "at-a-glance" view for your client presentation.

    → [Coverage Table](/platform/evaluate)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run gap analysis">
    Use Coverage Verification to check whether the renewal policy adequately addresses the client's risk exposures:

    *"Given that this client is a [industry] company with [key exposures], identify any significant coverage gaps in the renewal policy."*

    If the client has contracts with insurance requirements, also run Contract Compliance to verify continued compliance.

    → [Coverage Verification](/platform/verify) | [Contract Compliance](/platform/verify)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Draft the renewal summary">
    Ask Qumis to synthesize the findings:

    *"Based on our comparison and gap analysis, draft a renewal summary that: (1) lists the top 5 most significant coverage changes, (2) identifies the 3 most important gaps, and (3) recommends actions to address each gap. Format for a client-facing coverage letter."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare recommendations">
    Add your professional judgment: Do you recommend binding the renewal as-is, negotiating specific terms, or remarketing? Use the Qumis analysis as supporting evidence for your recommendation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Renewal analysis checklist

Use this checklist to ensure your renewal review is comprehensive:

**Coverage changes:**
- [ ] Limits — any increases, decreases, or restructuring?
- [ ] Deductibles — any changes to deductible amounts or structure?
- [ ] Exclusions — new exclusions added? Existing exclusions modified?
- [ ] Endorsements — endorsements added, removed, or modified?
- [ ] Conditions — changes to notice, cancellation, or other conditions?
- [ ] Definitions — key definitions changed?

**Coverage adequacy:**
- [ ] Do limits remain appropriate for the client's exposure?
- [ ] Are there gaps relative to the client's risk profile?
- [ ] Are contractual insurance requirements still met?
- [ ] Have the client's operations changed in ways that affect coverage needs?

**Market context (you provide):**
- [ ] How does the renewal compare to market benchmarks?
- [ ] Are the changes consistent with market trends?
- [ ] Are there alternative market options worth exploring?

## Multi-quote renewal comparison

When you have multiple renewal options, extend the workflow:

1. **Generate Comparison Reports** for each pair of quotes (or compare all against the expiring policy)
2. **Extract key terms** using Populate to build a side-by-side comparison spreadsheet
3. **Run Coverage Verification** on the top 2-3 options to identify which provides the most comprehensive coverage
4. **Draft a recommendation memo** using Qumis to summarize the tradeoffs and support your recommendation

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    Start the renewal analysis as soon as the renewal quotes arrive. The faster you can produce a thorough comparison, the more time you have for negotiation and client discussion. Use the Qumis output to identify specific terms to negotiate with carriers.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    Run the renewal analysis before the renewal meeting — not the day of. Use the Coverage Table as a visual aid during the meeting. Focus your client conversation on the most significant changes and your recommendations, not a line-by-line walkthrough.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    If you manage claims that span multiple policy periods, the renewal comparison helps you understand how coverage changed between periods. This is critical for determining which policy year responds to a specific loss.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    Run the renewal analysis on every policy in your program, not just the largest lines. Small policy changes can create significant coverage gaps. Use Contract Compliance to verify that your renewed program still meets all contractual requirements.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
