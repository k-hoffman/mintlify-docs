## The strategy

Referral mining is the most common and highest-converting prospecting motion in commercial insurance. But most brokers approach it passively. They wait for clients to volunteer introductions or rely on COIs to think of them at the right moment. The problem isn't willingness — it's that clients and COIs don't have a concrete, remarkable thing to point to when someone asks "who's your broker?"

Qumis changes the equation. When you produce structured, citation-backed, attorney-grade coverage analysis and put it in front of your client, you create an artifact that travels. Their CFO sees it. Their attorney reviews it. Their risk manager shares it with a peer at another firm. The output itself becomes the referral trigger.

Your goal is to make every renewal touchpoint and QBR produce something so visually clear and analytically sharp that your client's network notices.

## The Qumis workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Build your renewal analysis in Document Vault">
    Upload your client's expiring policy, the proposed renewal, and any competing quotes into the Document Vault. Organize them in a folder named for the account and renewal date (e.g., `Acme Corp - 07/2026 Renewal`).

    This becomes your working set for the analysis. Having everything in one place means you can run comparisons across all documents without switching contexts.

    <Tip>
      Upload the expiring and renewal side by side. The more documents Qumis can reference in a single session, the richer your coverage comparison will be.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run a structured coverage comparison using Chats">
    Open a Chat session with the uploaded documents and run a prompt that produces a structured comparison. Focus on the areas your client cares about most.

    **Example prompt:**
    ```
    Compare the expiring policy against the proposed renewal. For each
    major coverage section, identify: (1) what changed, (2) what was
    removed, (3) what was added, and (4) any gaps or silent exposures
    that could affect the insured. Flag anything that narrows coverage
    or introduces new exclusions. Cite the specific policy language
    for each finding.
    ```

    The citation-backed output is what makes this deliverable different from a standard spreadsheet comparison. Your client can see exactly where each finding comes from in the actual policy language.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate a polished report">
    Take the key findings from your Chat session and generate a report. The report feature formats the analysis into a clean, professional document that's ready to share with your client and their stakeholders.

    Focus the report on three to five findings that have the highest business impact. This isn't about volume — it's about showing your client something they didn't know about their own program.

    <Tip>
      The QumisScore and Visual Analysis features help you highlight the most significant differences at a glance. Use them to lead the report. Executives scan before they read.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save your best prompts to the Prompt Library">
    Once you've dialed in a coverage comparison prompt that produces strong results for a particular line of business or program structure, save it to the Prompt Library. This turns a one-off analysis into a repeatable workflow you can run across your entire book.

    Name prompts descriptively so other team members can use them: `Coverage Comparison - Commercial Property - Renewal vs. Expiring` or `Gap Analysis - Umbrella/Excess Tower`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Deliver the analysis at the client touchpoint">
    Present the report at your next renewal meeting, QBR, or stewardship review. Walk through the findings with your client and their team. The goal is to create a moment where the client sees the depth of your analysis and thinks, "my peers need to see this."

    Leave behind a copy of the report. This is the artifact that travels.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Templates and talk tracks

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Asking for the referral">
    After delivering a strong coverage analysis, use this talk track to open the referral conversation naturally. Don't force it — let the work speak first.

    **At the meeting:**
    > "I'm glad this was useful. We've been doing this kind of deep-dive analysis across all our accounts now that we have the right tools. If you know anyone in your network — whether it's a colleague, your attorney, your CFO's contacts — who could benefit from this level of coverage visibility, I'd love an introduction. Sometimes the best way to show what we do is to just show them a report like this one."

    **In the follow-up email:**
    > "Attached is the coverage comparison report we walked through. If anyone on your team or in your network would benefit from seeing what this kind of analysis looks like on their own program, I'm happy to run a complimentary review. Just send them my way."
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="COI outreach">
    Use this template when reaching out to centers of influence (attorneys, CPAs, bankers) who interact with your target prospects.

    **Email to a COI:**

    **Subject:** Coverage intelligence tool your clients should see

    > Hi [Name],
    >
    > I wanted to share something we've been using with our clients that's been getting a strong reaction. We now produce structured, citation-backed coverage analysis on commercial programs using AI that was built by insurance attorneys. The output looks like [brief description of a recent finding, e.g., "a gap in a $50M excess tower where the follow-form language didn't actually follow"].
    >
    > Your clients who carry complex commercial programs would benefit from seeing this. I'm happy to run a complimentary analysis on any of their current policies. No strings, just a chance to show them what's possible.
    >
    > Worth a quick call to walk you through a sample?
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="QBR framework">
    Structure your QBR to maximize referral potential. Dedicate the final section to the Qumis-powered coverage analysis.

    **QBR agenda (referral-optimized):**
    1. Program overview and claims summary (5 min)
    2. Market conditions and renewal outlook (5 min)
    3. **Coverage intelligence review** powered by Qumis (15 min)
       - Walk through the top three to five findings
       - Show the Visual Analysis and QumisScore
       - Highlight any gaps or silent exposures
    4. Recommendations and next steps (5 min)
    5. Open discussion (5 min) — this is where the referral conversation happens naturally

    <Tip>
      Invite your client's CFO or general counsel to the QBR. They're often the ones with the strongest external networks, and the coverage analysis is most impressive to people who don't see this level of detail from their current broker.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Standardizing across your book

The power of this playbook scales when you run it across every account, not just your largest ones.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Build a prompt library by line of business">
    Create and save prompts in the Prompt Library for each major line you write: commercial property, general liability, auto, umbrella/excess, professional liability, D&O, cyber, and workers' comp. Each line has its own common gaps and comparison points. A standardized prompt means any producer on your team can generate a strong analysis without starting from scratch.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Set a cadence for coverage analysis delivery">
    Don't wait for renewals. Run a proactive coverage review at the six-month mark on your largest accounts. This creates two referral touchpoints per year instead of one. Use the Document Vault to store the mid-term review alongside the renewal analysis so you're building a longitudinal view of the client's program.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Track which reports generate referrals">
    Keep a simple log: account name, report delivered, referral received (yes/no), referral source (client, COI, stakeholder). Over time, you'll see which types of analysis and which client personas are your best referral sources. Double down on those.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Measuring success

| Metric | What to track | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Reports delivered per month | Number of Qumis-powered coverage analyses shared with clients | 1 per active account per quarter |
| Referral conversations initiated | Times you asked for a referral after delivering a report | 80% of report deliveries |
| Referrals received | Warm introductions from clients or COIs | 2–3 per quarter per producer |
| Referral-to-meeting conversion | Percentage of referrals that convert to a first meeting | 50%+ |

<Note>
  Referral mining is a long game. The first month is about building the habit of delivering Qumis-powered analysis at every touchpoint. Referrals will follow once clients have seen the output two or three times and start recognizing it as a differentiator worth mentioning.
</Note>
