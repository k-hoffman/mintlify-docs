## What Is Legal Search?

Legal Search is an AI-powered legal research tool designed specifically for insurance coverage questions. Instead of manually searching legal databases, you describe your coverage question and Qumis searches case law, coverage opinions, regulatory guidance, and legal precedent to find relevant authority.

The result is a research foundation for coverage decisions backed by case law and legal precedent. Legal Search is most powerful when combined with your policy analysis in Qumis—you research the legal landscape while simultaneously analyzing what your policy actually says.

## What Legal Search Covers

Legal Search queries include:

- **Case law**: Court decisions addressing coverage questions, exclusion interpretation, and coverage disputes
- **Coverage opinions**: Published coverage analysis from insurance and legal experts
- **Regulatory guidance**: Insurance commissioner opinions, bulletin releases, and regulatory interpretations
- **Legal precedent**: Established legal principles relevant to your coverage question, across all U.S. jurisdictions
- **Claim resources**: Published analyses of how courts have ruled on similar claim scenarios

Legal Search also respects jurisdiction boundaries, so you can research how your specific state's courts have addressed similar issues.

## Understanding Your Results

Each Legal Search result includes:

- **Summary**: A concise explanation of the legal issue and how the court or authority ruled
- **Relevance score**: How closely the result matches your question, on a scale that helps you prioritize what to read first
- **Citation information**: Complete case name, year, court, and jurisdiction so you can locate the full opinion if needed
- **Key holdings**: The specific legal principle or ruling relevant to your coverage question
- **Jurisdiction tag**: Shows which state or states the authority applies to, helping you assess applicability to your situation

Results are ranked by relevance, so the most applicable authorities appear first. You can adjust sorting to prioritize recent cases, specific jurisdictions, or specific coverage lines.

## Refining Your Search

Legal Search supports multiple filters to focus your research:

- **Jurisdiction filters**: Target specific states or multi-state precedent
- **Date ranges**: Find recent decisions or trace how law has evolved over time
- **Coverage line focus**: Filter results to specific coverage types (CGL, Professional Liability, Cyber, etc.)
- **Authority type**: Focus on case law, published opinions, or regulatory guidance
- **Search terms**: Refine your question based on results and search again with updated language

If your first search returns too broad results, refine your question or add filters. If results are too narrow, broaden your jurisdiction selection or remove date restrictions to include historical precedent.

## Combining with Policy Analysis

Legal Search is most powerful when used alongside Qumis Chats and policy analysis:

1. **Ask about your policy**: Use Chats to understand exactly what your policy says about the coverage question
2. **Research the law**: Use Legal Search to find case law and precedent that interprets similar language
3. **Synthesize**: Combine your policy language findings with legal precedent to build a defensible coverage position
4. **Document**: Use both your policy analysis and legal research findings in coverage determination memos, claim files, and client communications

For example, if you're analyzing an exclusion, you might:
- Chat with your policy to get the exact exclusion language
- Search Legal Search for how courts have interpreted similar exclusions
- Compare your policy language to how courts ruled on nearly identical language
- Document both sources in your coverage position

<Warning>
Legal Search provides research assistance, not legal advice. The results are starting points for your analysis, not definitive legal conclusions. Always verify findings against primary sources (the actual court opinions and authorities cited), and consult with legal counsel for coverage decisions that have significant claim or financial implications. Legal Search identifies relevant authority, but interpreting that authority requires legal expertise.
</Warning>

## Best Practices for Legal Research

- **Be specific**: Ask about specific coverage issues, exclusions, or trigger language rather than broad questions
- **Start jurisdictional**: If you're analyzing a specific claim or policy, begin with the jurisdiction where the claim arose or policy is issued
- **Compare holdings**: When you find relevant cases, compare their facts to your situation. Sometimes similar language is interpreted differently based on surrounding circumstances
- **Track authority**: Save or export your search results to document your research process and the authorities you relied on
- **Update regularly**: Revisit Legal Search results periodically as new cases are decided. Case law evolves, and a recent decision may change how an exclusion is interpreted
