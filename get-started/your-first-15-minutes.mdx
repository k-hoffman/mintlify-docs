This walkthrough takes you from zero to your first AI-powered insurance analysis. By the end, you'll have uploaded a document, asked a question about it, and generated a professional report — all in about 15 minutes.

<Info>
  **What you'll need:** One insurance document (policy, quote, binder, or endorsement) in PDF, Word, Excel, or image format. If you don't have one handy, any policy document will work for practice.
</Info>

---

## Step 1: Upload a document to Vault

Your documents are the foundation of everything in Qumis. Start by adding one to your secure Vault.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Vault">
    From the left sidebar, click **Vault**. You'll see two tabs: **My Vault** (private to you) and **Shared Vault** (visible to your team).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload your document">
    Drag your file into the **Drop files here** box, or click to browse. Qumis accepts PDF, Word, Excel, and image files (JPG, PNG, TIFF).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name it clearly">
    Click the pencil icon next to the file name and give it a descriptive name — something like "ABC Corp GL Policy 2025" rather than "policy.pdf." You'll thank yourself later.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can also email documents to **clerk@qumis.ai** and they'll appear in your Vault automatically. This is useful when you receive quotes or binders from underwriters and want to capture them immediately.
</Tip>

Your document is now securely stored and available across every Qumis tool — Chats, Reports, Comparisons, and more. You won't need to re-upload it.

## Step 2: Ask a question with Chats

Now let's talk to your document.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Chats">
    From the left sidebar, click **Chats**, then click **New Chat**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your document">
    In the **Search and Add Documents From Vault** bar, search for the document you just uploaded and select it. Qumis will process and index it automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask a question">
    Try something like:
    - "Summarize the key coverages and limits in this policy"
    - "What exclusions should I be aware of?"
    - "Does this policy cover independent contractors?"

    Press **Ask** and Qumis will analyze the document and respond with a structured answer, including citations pointing to the specific clauses it referenced.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow up">
    Ask a follow-up question to go deeper: "Show me the exact wording of that exclusion" or "Explain this in plain language I can share with a client." Every conversation is saved automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Turn citations on.** If you don't see source references in the response, toggle **Hide Citations** off (top-right of the chat). Citations let you verify every answer against the original document.
</Tip>

## Step 3: Generate your first report

Reports transform raw document analysis into client-ready deliverables.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a report type">
    From the left sidebar, navigate to the report type that fits your document:
    - **Single Policy Analysis** — if you uploaded one policy and want a coverage summary
    - **Comparison Report** — if you want to compare two or more documents
    - **Claims Analysis** — if you're evaluating whether a policy responds to a claim
    - **Contract Review** — if you're checking compliance with contract requirements

    For your first report, **Single Policy Analysis** is the easiest starting point.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your document">
    Upload or pull your document from Vault. Add any context in the **Additional Information** field to focus the analysis (e.g., "Focus on liability coverages and sublimits").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate">
    Click **Generate**. Qumis uses multiple AI agents to read, analyze, validate, and cross-reference your document. This takes about 5–10 minutes — you'll see a progress indicator.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and explore">
    When it's ready, review the executive summary and key findings. Use **Qumis Clerk** (the chat within the report) to ask follow-up questions about specific findings. Download as PDF or Excel for client presentations.
  </Step>
</Steps>
