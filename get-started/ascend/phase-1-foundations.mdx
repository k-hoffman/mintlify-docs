# Phase 1: Foundations (Weeks 1–4)

This is the preparation phase — no team members will use Qumis yet. Phase 1 ensures your organization understands why Qumis matters and wants to use it before anyone receives access. Skipping or rushing this phase is the most common cause of implementation failure.

---

## What happens in Phase 1

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 1: Define your North Star and select your pilot workflow">
    **Objective:** Establish clear, measurable business outcomes and identify the specific workflow where Qumis will deliver the fastest value.

    **Actions:**

    Convene a strategy session with senior leadership (60 minutes) to define your North Star metric — the specific business outcome you're trying to improve with Qumis. Focus on measurable outcomes like premium growth, claims cycle time reduction, quote-to-bind conversion, or compliance review speed.

    Then conduct a workflow prioritization session with 3–6 high-performing brokers, claims professionals, and account managers (90 minutes). Ask: "What are your most time-consuming document-based tasks?" Select ONE pilot workflow based on high impact and natural fit for Qumis capabilities.

    The ideal pilot workflow is recurring (weekly), high-value, and maps directly to a "sticky" Qumis feature. This combination builds the weekly habit and demonstrates value immediately.

    **Recommended starter workflows:**

    | Role | Pilot Workflow | Qumis Feature | Why It Works |
    |---|---|---|---|
    | Brokers / Producers | Monday Quote Comparison | Comparison Report | Frequent, high-value task that trains brokers on a core sticky feature |
    | Claims Professionals | Claim Coverage Assessment | Claims Analysis | Integrates into a core daily/weekly task with immediate defensible value |
    | Account Managers | Contract Review | Contract Review Report | High-frequency task crucial for risk management; leverages a powerful sticky feature |

    **Success Metrics:** Written North Star statement approved; single pilot workflow selected; baseline metrics documented.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 2: Establish governance and security framework">
    **Objective:** Create clear policies for responsible AI use that address your legal, compliance, and risk management requirements.

    **Actions:**

    Designate a Qumis Governance Working Group (QGWG) with representatives from leadership, legal, IT, and end-users. Draft a Responsible AI Use Policy that mandates use of Qumis's Private Document Vault and requires human oversight at specific checkpoints — for example, any coverage decision communicated to a client.

    Obtain formal approval of the policy from Legal/Compliance before pilot launch.

    **Success Metrics:** QGWG established; written policy approved by Legal/Compliance.

    <Info>
      Your Qumis Customer Success Manager can provide a Responsible AI Use Policy template as a starting point. Most organizations customize this template rather than drafting from scratch.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 3: Build awareness and desire through targeted communication">
    **Objective:** Help your team understand why Qumis is being implemented and create personal motivation to adopt it.

    **Actions:**

    Conduct executive messaging sessions — your CEO or senior leader should communicate the business imperative and opportunity. Frame the message around team empowerment: "Qumis handles document analysis. You handle client relationships and strategic judgment. We're investing in AI so we can invest more in you doing what only humans can do."

    Hold role-specific awareness sessions (30 minutes per role) with tailored messages about how Qumis applies to each team's specific work.

    Address fears directly:

    - *"AI will replace my job."* → "Qumis handles document analysis. You handle client relationships and strategic judgment."
    - *"AI makes mistakes and I'll be blamed."* → "You remain responsible for all decisions. Qumis provides analysis; you validate it. Our policy requires human review at specific checkpoints."

    **Success Metrics:** 100% of pilot participants attend awareness session; pulse survey shows >70% understand the purpose.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key decisions in Phase 1

**How many pilot participants?** Typically 4–8 users from a single team or role. Small enough to support individually, large enough to generate meaningful usage data. All participants should share the same pilot workflow.

**Who should be in the pilot?** Select high-performers with peer influence, availability to commit 2 hours/week for 8 weeks, workflows that fit Qumis, and the ability to give constructive feedback. These are your future Champions.

**Which workflow first?** Pick the one that's recurring (weekly), high-value (saves meaningful time), and maps to a sticky feature (not just chat). The workflow should be immediately relevant to the pilot group's daily work.

---

## Common Phase 1 mistakes

**Rushing to get people into the platform.** The preparation work in Phase 1 directly impacts retention. Organizations that skip governance, awareness, and proper pilot selection consistently see lower adoption rates.

**Selecting too many pilot users.** More isn't better. Each pilot participant needs dedicated manager support (10 min/week) and implementation manager attention. Start with a manageable group and expand in Wave 2.

**Choosing a pilot workflow that isn't recurring.** One-time tasks don't build habits. The pilot workflow must be something the team does every week.
