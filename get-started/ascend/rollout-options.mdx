# Rollout Options

The Ascend™ framework supports three rollout timelines. All three follow the same three-phase structure and drive the same three success factors — they differ in how much overlap and compression is applied.

---

## At a glance

| | Standard | Parallel Pathed | Shotgun Start |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Timeline** | 12 weeks | 10 weeks | 8 weeks |
| **Pilot Phase** | Full 4-week pilot | Compressed 4-week pilot | No pilot |
| **Risk Level** | Lowest | Low-Medium | Higher |
| **IM Time Intensity** | Steady 20 hrs/week | Higher Weeks 1–6 | Highest Weeks 3–6 |
| **Validation Approach** | Full iteration cycle | Full iteration cycle | Launch & adjust |
| **Best For** | First AI implementation | Speed + proven approach | Small teams, urgent needs |

---

## Standard Rollout (12 Weeks)

The standard rollout runs each phase sequentially with no overlap. This is the lowest-risk option and the recommended approach for organizations implementing AI tools for the first time.

**Phase 1: Foundations (Weeks 1–4)** — Full preparation: team setup, governance, pilot selection, awareness building, SSO setup, and training document preparation.

**Phase 2: Implementation (Weeks 5–8)** — Full pilot: hands-on training, weekly usage and check-ins, dashboard tracking, mid-pilot review, champion identification and training, go/no-go decision.

**Phase 3: Scale & Sustain (Weeks 9–12)** — Full scale: champion-led Wave 2 training, best practice documentation, SOP updates, ROI measurement, Wave 3 planning.

**Choose this when:** Your organization is implementing AI tools for the first time, you want maximum time to learn and iterate, or you have the calendar space for a 12-week program.

---

## Parallel Pathed (10 Weeks)

The Parallel Pathed option overlaps portions of the Foundations and Pilot phases, getting your team using Qumis sooner while preserving the three-phase framework and full validation cycle.

**Phase 1: Foundations (Weeks 1–2)** — Accelerated setup: compressed team formation, governance, and awareness. SSO setup and pilot selection happen in parallel.

**Phase 1+2: Foundations + Pilot (Weeks 3–4)** — Overlap period: remaining Foundations activities (training document prep) run alongside the beginning of pilot activities (pilot training in Week 3).

**Phase 2: Pilot (Weeks 5–6)** — Full validation: weekly usage, check-ins, champion identification, go/no-go decision.

**Phase 3: Scale & Sustain (Weeks 7–10)** — Champion-led Wave 2, SOP updates, ROI measurement, expansion planning.

**Choose this when:** You want to move faster while still having a validated pilot, your team has some experience with AI tools, or you need to compress the timeline by 2 weeks.

<Info>
  Parallel Pathed is Qumis's recommended approach for most organizations. It balances speed with the validation that helps maximize the success of the broader rollout.
</Info>

---

## Shotgun Start (8 Weeks)

The Shotgun Start is the fastest path to having all users in Qumis. It compresses the timeline to 8 weeks by eliminating the separate pilot phase — going directly from Foundations to organization-wide training.

**Phase 1: Foundations (Weeks 1–4)** — Full preparation: team setup, governance, policy development, user selection, awareness, SSO, and training documents. No compression here — the foundation work is essential regardless of rollout speed.

**Phase 3: Scale & Sustain (Weeks 5–8)** — All-hands training (5× user training sessions in Week 5–6), intensive support, adoption monitoring, training surveys, follow-up time study, SOP updates, best practice documentation, Wave 3 planning.

**Choose this when:** You have a small team (fewer than 20 users), need to move with extreme urgency, or are confident in your team's ability to adopt new tools without a validated pilot approach.

<Warning>
  The Shotgun Start skips the pilot validation cycle. This means you won't have the opportunity to iterate on your approach based on pilot data before training the broader team. Use only when the speed benefit clearly outweighs the risk of a less-refined rollout.
</Warning>

## How to choose

**Start with Standard** if any of these apply:
- This is your organization's first AI implementation
- You're unsure which pilot workflow to select
- Managers have limited availability for check-ins
- You want maximum confidence before scaling

**Choose Parallel Pathed** if:
- You have clear pilot workflow candidates
- Your Implementation Manager can handle higher intensity in Weeks 1–6
- You want the fastest path that still includes full validation
- You've seen AI implementations before (even if not Qumis)

**Only consider Shotgun Start** if:
- You have fewer than 20 total users
- There's genuine urgency driving the timeline
- Your team is comfortable adopting new tools quickly
- You can accept the risk of not iterating before broad rollout

Your Qumis Customer Success Manager will help you select the right option based on your organization's specific situation.
