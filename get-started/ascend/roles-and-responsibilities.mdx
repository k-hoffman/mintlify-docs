# Roles and Responsibilities

A successful Ascend™ implementation requires specific roles with clear responsibilities and defined time commitments. This page details what each role does, when they're active, and what success looks like.

---

## Implementation Manager

### The quarterback and daily driver

The Implementation Manager is the most critical role. Everything routes through you. Your structured, data-driven execution is the difference between success and failure.

**Time commitment:** 20 hours per week (50% dedicated role) for 12 weeks. Total investment: approximately 240 hours.

<Warning>
  This is not a "side project." If you cannot dedicate 50% of your time, escalate to the Executive Sponsor immediately. Either request removal of other responsibilities or delay implementation until the resource is available.
</Warning>

**The golden rule:** Intervene within 48 hours of any red flag. Early intervention prevents failure. Late intervention manages failure.

**Key responsibilities by phase:**

| Phase | Key Actions |
|---|---|
| **Phase 1** (Weeks 1–4) | Coordinate strategy sessions, manage pilot selection, establish governance framework, prepare training materials, set up tracking dashboards, conduct baseline time study |
| **Phase 2** (Weeks 5–8) | Facilitate or co-facilitate pilot training, run weekly pilot reviews, track usage data daily, intervene with disengaged users within 48 hours, identify Champions, coordinate go/no-go decision |
| **Phase 3** (Weeks 9–12) | Support Champion-led training, manage Wave 2 rollout, update SOPs and best practices, conduct follow-up time study, plan expansion |

**Weekly rituals:**
- Monday: Review usage dashboard, identify red flags, plan interventions
- Midweek: Check in with Implementation Manager weekly review (QGWG)
- Friday: Update tracking, prepare weekly status report for Executive Sponsor

→ The Implementation Toolkit includes a detailed [Implementation Manager Onboarding Guide](/get-started/ascend/toolkit-and-templates) for your first week.

## Executive Sponsor

### Strategic champion and obstacle remover

Your visible commitment is THE difference between success and failure. If you cannot commit to the actions below, delay implementation. Passive sponsorship guarantees failure.

**Time commitment:** 5–7 hours total over 12 weeks. Heaviest in Week 1 (2 hours for setup), then 15–30 minutes per week for check-ins, with additional time at key milestones (Weeks 2, 6, 8).

**Key responsibilities:**

| Action | When | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Strategy session — define North Star metric | Week 1 | 60 min |
| Executive messaging — communicate the "why" to the organization | Week 2 | 30 min |
| Weekly check-in with Implementation Manager | Weeks 2–12 | 15 min/week |
| Mid-pilot review — assess progress and address barriers | Week 6 | 30 min |
| Go/no-go decision — approve broader rollout | Week 8 | 30 min |
| Visible endorsement at key moments | As needed | 15 min each |

**What success looks like:** You have removed obstacles before the Implementation Manager has to ask twice. Your team knows you personally endorse this initiative. When challenges arise, you respond within 24 hours.

## Direct Managers

### The #1 success factor

**The data: 58% retention WITH your weekly support. 1.4% retention WITHOUT.**

You are not "helpful." You are not "important." You ARE the difference between success and failure. There is no other factor in the entire implementation that matters more than your 10-minute weekly check-in.

**Time commitment:** 10 minutes per pilot participant per week for 8 weeks. Example: 3 participants × 10 minutes = 30 minutes per week. Total investment: approximately 5 hours per participant over 8 weeks.

**Your non-negotiable commitment:** Same day, same time, every week, for 8 weeks. Calendar it. Protect it. Never skip it.

**The weekly 10-minute check-in:**
1. "Did you complete the pilot workflow this week?"
2. "What worked well?"
3. "What was frustrating or unclear?"
4. "What did you discover?"
5. "What will you try next week?"

After the check-in: log it in the tracking sheet (2 minutes). If anything concerns you, alert the Implementation Manager immediately.

**Red flags to watch for:**
- Participant didn't complete the workflow this week
- Participant says "I didn't have time" two weeks in a row
- Participant is only using chat, not the structured workflow
- Participant sounds frustrated or dismissive

**When you see a red flag:** Alert the Implementation Manager within 24 hours. Don't wait for the next check-in cycle.

## Pilot Participants

### Pioneers and future Champions

You were selected because you're a high-performer your colleagues trust, you have peer influence, you're available to commit 2 hours per week for 8 weeks, your workflow is a natural fit for Qumis, and you communicate well.

Your experience in this pilot will determine whether your peers adopt Qumis in Wave 2. You're not just testing software — you're testing this for the entire organization.

**Time commitment:** 2 hours per week for 8 weeks.
- Week 5: 90-minute kickoff workshop
- Weeks 5–8: 30–60 minutes of weekly workflow usage (time decreases as you get faster)
- Weeks 5–8: 10-minute weekly manager check-in

**What success looks like:**
- You complete the pilot workflow every week (consistency > volume)
- You ask follow-up questions and explore beyond the initial output
- You verify AI findings against source documents
- You share feedback — both what works and what doesn't
- You'd recommend Qumis to a colleague based on your experience

## Champions

### Peer trainers and credible voices

Champions are successful pilot participants who've been trained to lead Wave 2 adoption. Your authenticity is your superpower — you've lived the journey from skepticism to success, and your peers trust you more than they trust vendors or executives.

**Time commitment:** 4–5 hours (concentrated).
- Week 8: 2-hour train-the-trainer session
- Week 8.5: 30-minute dry-run call
- Weeks 9–10: 90 minutes per Wave 2 session (typically 2–3 sessions)

**What you'll do in training:**
- Learn to facilitate (not just present)
- Craft your personal "Qumis story" — from first reaction to current usage
- Practice leading the hands-on workshop
- Rehearse handling common objections

**Why your voice matters more than anyone else's:** You're one of them, you've lived the journey, you have peer credibility, and you speak their language.

## Qumis Governance Working Group (QGWG)

### AI oversight committee

The QGWG provides governance oversight for Qumis usage, including the Responsible AI Use Policy, human oversight checkpoints, and escalation procedures.

**Composition:** Representatives from leadership, legal, IT, and end-users.

**Time commitment:** 3–4 hours total over 12 weeks.
- Week 2: Formation meeting (30 min)
- Week 3: Policy review and approval (60 min)
- Week 6: Mid-pilot review (30 min)
- Week 8: Go/no-go decision participation (30 min)
- Quarterly: Ongoing governance review (30 min)

**Key deliverables:** Responsible AI Use Policy, human oversight checkpoints defined, escalation procedures documented.

## Role summary

| Role | Phase 1 | Phase 2 | Phase 3 | Total Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Implementation Manager** | Lead setup | Lead pilot | Manage scale | ~240 hours |
| **Executive Sponsor** | Set vision, remove barriers | Check in, mid-pilot review | Go/no-go, ongoing sponsorship | ~7 hours |
| **Direct Managers** | Attend manager training | Weekly 10-min check-ins | Continue support for new users | ~5 hrs/participant |
| **Pilot Participants** | Attend awareness session | 2 hrs/week for 8 weeks | Transition to Champion or regular user | ~16 hours |
| **Champions** | — | Identified in Weeks 6–7 | Lead Wave 2 training | ~5 hours |
| **QGWG** | Form, approve policy | Mid-pilot review | Quarterly reviews | ~4 hours |
