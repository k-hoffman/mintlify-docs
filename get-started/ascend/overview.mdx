Most AI implementations fail. Not because of the technology — because of change management. Ascend™ is Qumis's evidence-based implementation framework, built on data from 336 users across 26 cohorts, designed to de-risk your implementation and set your team up for lasting success.

---

## Why implementation matters

According to McKinsey, more than 70% of AI implementations fail to meet their objectives. The cause isn't the technology — it's how organizations introduce it. Users try a tool once, then revert to old methods. Managers don't support adoption. There's no structured learning pathway. Sporadic usage doesn't build habits. Generic training doesn't address real work.

The result is expensive shelfware.

Ascend™ addresses every one of these failure points with a structured, evidence-based approach that transforms trial into practice.

---

## The evidence

Qumis analyzed usage data from 336 users across 26 implementation cohorts and identified three factors that predict whether an implementation succeeds or fails:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Deep First Experiences" icon="bolt">
    Users with 15–20 interactions in their first week are **twice as likely** to still be using Qumis at the end of their first month compared to users with just 1–2 interactions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sticky Features" icon="chart-line">
    Users who adopt structured workflows — Comparisons, Claims Analysis, Coverage Tables, Contract Review — show **significantly higher retention** than users who only use basic chat.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Weekly Habits" icon="calendar-check">
    Consistent users (those engaging every week) achieve **60% retention** versus **1–2%** for users who engage in bursts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

These aren't theoretical principles. They're patterns observed in real implementation data that directly inform every step of the Ascend methodology.

## Three phases, twelve weeks

Ascend follows a three-phase structure that moves from preparation through structured testing to sustainable scale:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Phase 1: Foundations (Weeks 1–4)">
    **No Qumis usage yet.** This is the preparation phase — defining goals, assembling the implementation team, establishing governance, selecting the pilot group, and building organizational awareness. The work here sets up everything that follows.

    Key deliverables: Implementation team formed, Responsible AI Use Policy approved, success metrics defined, pilot group selected, awareness sessions complete.

    → [Phase 1 detail](/get-started/ascend/phase-1-foundations)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 2: Implementation (Weeks 5–8)">
    **The critical phase.** Pilot users get hands-on with Qumis through workflow-focused training (not feature tours). Week 5 is the most important week — where deep first experiences are built. Weeks 6–8 focus on building the weekly habit through structured accountability and manager support.

    Key deliverables: Pilot training complete, weekly usage established, mid-pilot review, go/no-go decision for broader rollout, Champions identified and trained.

    → [Phase 2 detail](/get-started/ascend/phase-2-implementation)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 3: Scale & Sustain (Weeks 9–12+)">
    **From pilot to organizational capability.** Trained Champions (successful pilot participants) lead Wave 2 training. New workflows are added. Qumis becomes embedded in daily operations. Monthly stewardship meetings ensure continued momentum.

    Key deliverables: Wave 2 users trained, new use cases supported, monthly stewardship meetings established, ROI documented, expansion plan created.

    → [Phase 3 detail](/get-started/ascend/phase-3-scale-and-sustain)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Rollout options

The standard implementation runs 12 weeks, but Ascend offers flexibility based on your organization's needs and urgency:

| Option | Duration | Pilot Phase | Risk Level | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | 12 weeks | Full 4-week pilot | Lowest | First AI implementation, organizations wanting full validation |
| **Parallel Pathed** | 10 weeks | Compressed 4-week pilot | Low-Medium | Speed + proven approach, overlapping Foundations and Pilot activities |
| **Shotgun Start** | 8 weeks | No pilot | Higher | Small teams, urgent needs, organizations comfortable moving fast |

## Implementation team

Ascend requires specific roles to succeed. Each role has a defined time commitment and clear responsibilities:

| Role | Description | Time Commitment |
|---|---|---|
| **Implementation Manager** | Quarterback and daily driver — coordinates everything | 20 hrs/week (50% FTE) for 12 weeks |
| **Executive Sponsor** | Decision authority and obstacle remover | 5–7 hours total |
| **Direct Managers** | Habit enablers — the #1 predictor of success | 10 min/week per participant |
| **Pilot Participants** | Test users who validate the approach | 2 hrs/week for 8 weeks |
| **Champions** | Peer trainers who lead Wave 2 adoption | 4–5 hours (concentrated) |
| **Qumis Governance Working Group** | AI oversight committee | 3–4 hours total |

→ [Roles and responsibilities detail](/get-started/ascend/roles-and-responsibilities)
