Phase 2 is where Qumis usage begins. Week 5 is the critical first session — the moment that determines whether users develop competence and confidence or write off the platform after a surface-level experience. This phase focuses on building all three success factors: Deep First Experiences, Sticky Features, and Weekly Habits.

---

## What happens in Phase 2

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 4: Deliver workflow-focused training (not feature tours)">
    **Objective:** Build deep competence in your selected pilot workflow through hands-on practice with real documents and iterative dialogue.

    **Actions:**

    Design a 90-minute hands-on workshop structured around completing the pilot workflow end-to-end. Do NOT provide a tour of features — structure the entire session around solving a real problem.

    Use real documents from your firm. Have each participant complete the full workflow on their own device. The goal is 15–20 meaningful interactions during this first session.

    Teach iterative dialogue explicitly. This is the most important training element. Teach participants to view initial output as a draft and ask follow-up questions:

    - *Query 1:* "Compare coverage between these two quotes."
    - *Query 2:* "Now focus only on the cyber liability provisions."
    - *Query 3:* "What specific exclusions in Quote A would allow the carrier to deny a ransomware claim?"
    - *Query 4:* "Draft a two-paragraph summary I can include in my client email recommending Quote B."

    Require validation exercises. Have each participant locate the specific text in the source document that supports a finding made by Qumis. This builds the critical habit of verification.

    **Success Metrics:** 100% of pilot participants complete hands-on workshop; post-training assessment shows >80% can demonstrate iterative dialogue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 5: Build the weekly habit through structured accountability">
    **Objective:** Create consistent week-over-week engagement during the critical first month. This directly addresses the most significant predictor of retention.

    **Actions:**

    Make the pilot workflow mandatory, not optional, for the 8-week pilot period. For example: all Monday quote reviews *must* use Compare Documents.

    Implement weekly manager check-ins. Direct managers must spend 10 minutes each week with each pilot participant to review what they completed and discuss challenges. This creates the external accountability that drives the 58% retention rate (vs. 1.4% without manager support).

    Create usage dashboards to track consistency and identify users who need intervention.

    Intervene quickly. If a user misses a week or shows declining engagement, the Implementation Manager reaches out within 48 hours. Early intervention prevents failure — late intervention manages failure.

    **Success Metrics:** >80% of pilot participants engage every week for the first 4 weeks; 100% of managers complete weekly check-ins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 6: Measure, learn, and adapt">
    **Objective:** Systematically capture feedback, identify patterns, and refine your approach before broader rollout.

    **Actions:**

    Conduct weekly pilot reviews with the QGWG (30 minutes) to review usage data and share success stories.

    Collect structured feedback at Week 8. Ask: "How much time does Qumis save you per workflow?" and "What's your biggest frustration?"

    Capture and document best practices — the most effective prompts, common workflow patterns, and tips that experienced users have discovered. Create a shared prompt library.

    Make a go/no-go decision on broader rollout at Week 8. Evaluate: Did we achieve >50% Month 1 retention? Is there measurable business impact?

    **Success Metrics:** >75% response rate on surveys; documented best practices shared; business impact data shows positive trend.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The critical first session (Week 5)

Week 5 is the most important week in the entire implementation. This is where Success Factor 1 (Deep First Experiences) is either achieved or missed. Here's what a successful first session looks like:

**Before the session:** Participants have attended the awareness session, understand why Qumis is being implemented, and have their pilot workflow documents ready.

**During the session (90 minutes):**
- Minutes 1–10: Brief context-setting (not a feature tour)
- Minutes 10–60: Hands-on workflow completion with real documents
- Minutes 60–80: Iterative dialogue practice — refining, narrowing, exploring
- Minutes 80–90: Validation exercise — checking AI citations against source documents

**After the session:** Each participant has completed the full pilot workflow, asked follow-up questions, verified citations, and achieved 15–20 meaningful interactions.

<Tip>
  **Critical:** Use real documents from your firm, not generic examples. The experience must feel immediately relevant to participants' actual work. Generic examples reduce engagement and undermine the depth of the first experience.
</Tip>

## Manager check-in template

Direct managers should cover these questions in their weekly 10-minute check-in:

1. **"Did you complete the pilot workflow this week?"** — Tracks consistency (Success Factor 3)
2. **"What worked well?"** — Reinforces positive experiences
3. **"What was frustrating or unclear?"** — Identifies barriers before they cause dropout
4. **"What did you discover that surprised you?"** — Encourages deeper exploration
5. **"What would you like to try next week?"** — Builds forward momentum

<Info>
  **The data behind manager check-ins:** Users who receive weekly manager support achieve 58% retention. Users without manager support achieve 1.4% retention. This single intervention — 10 minutes per participant per week — is the largest driver of implementation success.
</Info>

## Identifying Champions

During Weeks 6–8, the Implementation Manager should identify 2–3 pilot participants who show strong engagement, peer influence, and communication skills. These will become your Champions — the peer trainers who lead Wave 2 adoption in Phase 3.

Champion criteria: consistent weekly usage, adoption of sticky features (not just chat), enthusiasm for the platform, ability to articulate value to peers, and willingness to teach others.

Champions receive train-the-trainer preparation in Week 8 (approximately 2 hours) plus a 30-minute dry-run call before leading their first Wave 2 session.

## Common Phase 2 mistakes

**Running a feature tour instead of a workflow workshop.** The first session must be hands-on with real work. Feature tours produce shallow engagement that doesn't convert to habits.

**Making the pilot workflow optional.** During the pilot period, the workflow must be mandatory. "Try it when you have time" ensures no one has time.

**Skipping manager check-ins.** This is the single highest-leverage intervention. If managers can't commit 10 minutes per participant per week, delay the implementation until they can.

**Waiting too long to intervene with disengaged users.** The Implementation Manager's golden rule: intervene within 48 hours of any red flag. A user who misses a week needs immediate outreach — not a note in next week's report.
