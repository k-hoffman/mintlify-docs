Phase 3 transforms Qumis from a pilot project to an embedded capability. The pilot group has validated the approach, Champions have been trained, and the organization has data on what works. Now it's time to scale — and to build the systems that make adoption self-sustaining.

---

## What happens in Phase 3

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 7: Scale to additional workflows and teams">
    **Objective:** Expand Qumis usage systematically based on pilot learnings.

    **Actions:**

    Prioritize the next workflows based on impact and readiness. Use the pilot data to identify which capabilities delivered the most value and which roles are most ready for adoption.

    Launch Wave 2 with Champion-led training. Your Champions — successful pilot participants who've been trained as peer facilitators — lead the training sessions for new users. Research shows peer-led training achieves twice the adoption rate of vendor-led training because Champions have credibility, speak the team's language, and can share authentic stories of their own journey.

    Wave 2 training follows the same structure as pilot training: 90-minute hands-on workshops with real documents, iterative dialogue, and validation exercises. Champions co-facilitate with the Implementation Manager for their first session.

    Introduce new Qumis capabilities to existing users. Pilot participants who mastered their initial workflow are ready to explore additional features — for example, a broker who started with Compare Documents reports might now adopt Coverage Tables for proposals or use Chats for pre-meeting prep.

    **Success Metrics:** Wave 2 users trained and actively using the platform; existing users expanding to additional workflows.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 8: Embed Qumis in organizational operations">
    **Objective:** Make Qumis usage the default — not the exception — for document-intensive work.

    **Actions:**

    Establish monthly stewardship meetings with the Executive Sponsor, Implementation Manager, and QGWG to review usage trends, discuss new use cases, and plan future expansion.

    Set up Qumis Office Hours — regular drop-in sessions where users can get help, share tips, and learn from each other. These informal touchpoints sustain momentum after the structured pilot period ends.

    Update standard operating procedures. If your team's renewal workflow now includes a Compare Documents report, update the written process to reflect that. Making Qumis part of the documented workflow removes ambiguity about whether it's expected.

    Update best practices and the shared prompt library based on ongoing usage. As users discover effective patterns, capture and share them.

    Document ROI. Conduct a follow-up time study comparing baseline metrics from Week 2 to current performance. Quantify the efficiency gains for leadership and budget discussions.

    Plan Wave 3 and beyond. Use adoption data to identify the next teams, roles, and workflows for expansion.

    **Success Metrics:** Monthly stewardship meetings established; SOPs updated; ROI documented; Wave 3 expansion plan created.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Champion-led training

Champions are the key to scalable adoption. Here's how the train-the-trainer process works:

**Champion Training Session (2 hours).** Champions learn to facilitate — not just present. They practice leading the hands-on workshop, craft their personal "Qumis story" (from skepticism to success), and rehearse handling common objections.

**Dry-Run Call (30 minutes).** Each Champion runs through their session plan with the Implementation Manager, getting feedback and building confidence.

**Wave 2 Sessions.** Champions lead training with the Implementation Manager available for support. After their first solo session, most Champions are self-sufficient.

**Why Champions work:**

- They're *one of them* — they understand the work, concerns, and culture
- They've *lived the journey* — from skepticism to success, including the struggles
- They have *peer credibility* — the team trusts them more than vendors or executives
- They speak *their language* — no corporate jargon, no overselling, just real talk

## Monthly stewardship meetings

After the structured implementation period, monthly stewardship meetings keep adoption on track. These 30-minute sessions typically cover:

1. **Usage trends.** Are users maintaining weekly consistency? Are new capabilities being adopted?
2. **Success stories.** What wins have users achieved? Share these to maintain organizational momentum.
3. **Challenges and feedback.** What's frustrating users? Where do they need support?
4. **New use cases.** What additional workflows could benefit from Qumis?
5. **Expansion planning.** Which teams or roles are next?

## Measuring success

By the end of Phase 3, you should be able to measure:

| Metric | How to Measure |
|---|---|
| **User retention** | % of trained users still active after 30 days |
| **Weekly consistency** | % of active users engaging every week |
| **Feature depth** | % of users who've adopted structured workflows beyond chat |
| **Time savings** | Hours saved per workflow, compared to baseline time study |
| **Business impact** | Improvement in your North Star metric (premium growth, cycle time, etc.) |
| **Internal capability** | Number of trained Champions who can lead future cohorts independently |

## What happens after Week 12

Ascend doesn't end at Week 12 — it transitions from intensive implementation support to ongoing partnership:

**Qumis Office Hours** provide regular touchpoints for user support and best practice sharing.

**Quarterly QGWG Reviews** ensure the governance framework stays current as usage expands and new use cases emerge.

**Ongoing Qumis partnership** continues with value measurement, use case development, and support for future expansion waves.

The goal is self-sufficiency: your organization can onboard new users, expand to new workflows, and sustain adoption without being dependent on Qumis for every step.
