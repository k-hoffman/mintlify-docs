The Ascend™ Implementation Toolkit provides everything you need to run a successful Qumis implementation. Your Qumis Customer Success Manager will share access to the full toolkit as part of your implementation kickoff. This page describes what's included and how to use it.

---

## Toolkit structure

The toolkit is organized into numbered folders that map to the implementation workflow:

| Folder | Contents | When You Need It |
|---|---|---|
| **00. Start Here** | Implementation Guide, Quick Start Checklist | Day 1 — your starting point |
| **01. Role Quick Reference Cards** | One-page guides for every role | Week 1 — distribute to each team member |
| **02. Appendices** | Detailed reference material for the Implementation Guide | As needed throughout |
| **03. Week-by-Week Materials** | Phase-specific templates and checklists for Weeks 1–12 | Each week — follow sequentially |
| **04. Communication Templates** | Email templates, Slack/Teams messages, scripts | Before each communication milestone |
| **05. Training Materials** | Kickoff training, manager training, champion training | Weeks 2, 5, and 8 |
| **06. Tracking Tools** | Usage dashboards, check-in logs, survey templates | Ongoing from Week 2 |
| **07. Support Resources** | Troubleshooting guides, FAQ documents | As needed |
| **08. Visual Assets** | Branded graphics and presentation materials | For internal communications |
| **09. Governance & Compliance** | Responsible AI Use Policy template, QGWG charter | Weeks 2–3 |

## Start here: Quick Start Checklist

The Quick Start Checklist is your first-day guide, organized by role. It tells you exactly what to read and do first:

**Executive Sponsor:** Read your Quick Reference Card, skim Section 1 of the Implementation Guide, review Week 1 critical actions.

**Implementation Manager:** Read the full Implementation Guide, complete the Implementation Manager Onboarding Guide, review all Week 1 materials.

**Direct Manager:** Read your Quick Reference Card, review the weekly check-in template, calendar your check-in times.

**Pilot Participant:** Read your Quick Reference Card, prepare your pilot workflow documents, calendar the kickoff training.

**Champion:** Read your Quick Reference Card (after being identified in Week 7), prepare for train-the-trainer session.

---

## Role Quick Reference Cards

Each role has a one-page Quick Reference Card (QRC) designed to be read in under 10 minutes. QRCs include the role's purpose, time commitment, key actions by phase, the critical data they need to know, and a decision framework for common situations.

Available QRCs:

- **Executive Sponsor QRC** — Strategic champion and obstacle remover
- **Implementation Manager QRC** — Quarterback and daily driver
- **Direct Manager QRC** — The #1 success factor (58% vs. 1.4% retention)
- **Pilot Participant QRC** — Pioneer and future champion
- **Champion QRC** — Peer trainer and credible voice

<Tip>
  **Distribute QRCs in Week 1.** Every team member should have their QRC before the implementation begins. These cards are designed to be practical — not just informational. They include decision trees, red flag checklists, and specific scripts for common situations.
</Tip>

## Communication templates

The toolkit includes ready-to-customize templates for every communication milestone:

### Email templates

- **Executive announcement** — CEO/leader message introducing Qumis to the organization
- **Pilot participant invitation** — Personalized outreach to selected pilot members
- **Manager briefing** — What managers need to know about their check-in role
- **Wave 2 announcement** — Building excitement for the next cohort
- **Success story sharing** — Template for sharing wins across the organization

### Slack/Teams messages

- **Weekly reminders** — Nudges for pilot participants to complete their workflow
- **Quick wins** — Short posts celebrating user achievements
- **Tips and tricks** — Weekly Qumis tips for the team channel

### Scripts

- **Executive sponsor talking points** — For the all-hands awareness session
- **Manager check-in guide** — The five questions and how to handle common responses
- **Objection handling** — Responses to common resistance ("I don't have time," "I don't trust AI," "My workflow is fine")

## Training materials

### Kickoff Training (Week 5)

The 90-minute hands-on workshop that creates deep first experiences. Includes facilitator guide, participant workbook, and real-workflow exercise templates for each role.

### Manager Training (Week 2)

A 60-minute session that prepares direct managers for their check-in role. Covers the data behind manager support (58% vs. 1.4%), the five check-in questions, red flag identification, and escalation procedures.

### Champion Training (Week 8)

The train-the-trainer session that equips Champions to lead Wave 2 adoption. Includes facilitation techniques, personal story framework, objection handling practice, and a dry-run checklist.

---

## Tracking tools

### Usage dashboard

A pre-built dashboard template for tracking the metrics that matter: weekly active users, feature adoption (structured workflows vs. chat), consistency of engagement, and intervention status.

### Manager check-in log

A simple tracking sheet for direct managers to log their weekly check-ins — what was discussed, any red flags identified, and follow-up actions.

### Survey templates

Pre-built surveys for key measurement points: post-training assessment (Week 5), mid-pilot feedback (Week 6), end-of-pilot evaluation (Week 8), and follow-up satisfaction (Week 12).

### Time study template

A structured template for measuring time savings. Used at baseline (Week 2–3) and follow-up (Week 8–9) to quantify the efficiency gains from Qumis adoption.

---

## Governance documents

### Responsible AI Use Policy template

A customizable template that covers: approved use cases for Qumis, document handling and privacy requirements, human oversight checkpoints (e.g., any coverage decision communicated to a client requires human review), escalation procedures for unexpected AI output, and compliance with your organization's existing data governance policies.

### QGWG charter

A template for establishing your Qumis Governance Working Group, including purpose, membership, meeting cadence, decision authority, and reporting requirements.
