# Three Success Factors

Qumis analyzed usage data from 336 users across 26 implementation cohorts to identify what separates successful implementations from failed ones. Three factors emerged as the strongest predictors of sustained adoption. Every element of the Ascend™ framework is designed to drive these three factors.

---

## Success Factor 1: Deep First Experiences

### The data

Users with 15–20 interactions in their first week on Qumis are twice as likely to still be using the platform at the end of their first month than their teammates with just 1–2 interactions.

### What this means

A superficial first experience creates superficial commitment. Users who deeply engage — asking follow-up questions, refining outputs, exploring implications — develop both competence and confidence. They see what Qumis can actually do, not just a surface-level demo.

This is why Ascend mandates 90-minute hands-on workshops instead of 15-minute overviews. The goal is 15–20 meaningful interactions in the first session, using real documents from your firm — not generic examples.

### How Ascend drives this factor

**Workflow-focused training, not feature tours.** Training is structured around completing the pilot workflow end-to-end. Participants solve a real problem in their first session, not watch a demo.

**Real documents, not generic examples.** Participants use their firm's actual policies, quotes, and contracts. The experience is immediately relevant to their work.

**Iterative dialogue.** Facilitators teach participants to treat the first AI output as a draft and ask follow-up questions — narrowing scope, requesting specifics, exploring scenarios. This naturally generates the depth of engagement that predicts retention.

**Validation exercises.** Participants locate the specific text in the source document that supports the AI's analysis. This builds the verification habit and deepens understanding of how the platform works.

---

## Success Factor 2: Sticky Features

### The data

Users who adopt structured workflows — Comparisons, Claims Analysis, Coverage Tables, Contract Review, and Single Policy Analysis reports — show significantly higher retention. Users who only use basic chat show substantially lower retention.

### What this means

Qumis's chat interface is accessible and easy to start with, but users who only use chat miss the structured workflows that provide deeper analysis and insights. They experience Qumis as a marginally better search tool rather than a transformative capability.

The structured report types — Compare, Evaluate, Summarize, and the various report formats — are where Qumis delivers the most differentiated value. Adoption of these features is what separates users who get lasting value from those who drift away.

### How Ascend drives this factor

**Onboarding around specific workflows.** Training is designed around a specific workflow — like "Automate Your Quote Comparisons" — not around a feature tour. Users learn the capability that maps to their highest-value task.

**First experience is a complete workflow.** Users solve a real problem in their first session using a structured feature, not just chat.

**Feature progression tracking.** Implementation managers identify users who are stuck on chat and intervene with targeted coaching to help them discover structured workflows.

**Recommended pilot workflows by role:**

| Role | Recommended Pilot Workflow | Sticky Feature |
|---|---|---|
| Brokers / Producers | Monday Quote Comparison | Comparison Report |
| Claims Professionals | Claim Coverage Assessment | Claims Analysis |
| Account Managers / Service Teams | Contract Review | Contract Review Report |
| Risk Managers | Contract Compliance Check | Verify |

## Success Factor 3: Weekly Habits

### The data

Consistent users — those engaging every week — achieve 60% retention versus 1–2% for burst users (those who use the platform intensively for a short period and then stop).

### What this means

Your team must use Qumis as part of their regular workflow, not as an occasional tool. A user who analyzes one policy every Monday is more likely to get sustained value than a user who analyzes 20 policies once and never returns.

Habit formation requires the same task, the same day, every week. This repetition is what transforms Qumis from something users try to something users rely on.

### How Ascend drives this factor

**Recurring task identification.** For each role, Ascend identifies a recurring task in the weekly workflow that naturally fits Qumis (e.g., Monday quote reviews for brokers).

**Mandatory pilot workflow.** During the 8-week pilot period, the pilot workflow is mandatory — not optional. All Monday quote reviews *must* use Compare Documents.

**Manager check-ins.** Direct managers spend 10 minutes each week with each pilot participant to review what they completed and discuss challenges. This creates external accountability.

**Consistency tracking, not volume tracking.** Success is measured by weekly engagement — "Did they use it this week?" — not by total interactions. This focus on consistency is what builds lasting habits.

---

## How the three factors work together

These success factors build on each other sequentially:

| Factor | Timeframe | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Deep First Experiences** | Week 5 (first session) | Depth of initial engagement (15–20 interactions) |
| **Sticky Features** | Ongoing (all sessions) | Which features users adopt (structured workflows vs. chat) |
| **Weekly Habits** | Weeks 5–12 (habit formation) | Consistency of engagement (weekly vs. burst) |

The first session must be deep (Factor 1). All sessions must use the right features (Factor 2). The pattern must be weekly (Factor 3).

Ascend orchestrates all three by structuring training around deep engagement with sticky features, then building the accountability systems that make weekly usage the norm.
