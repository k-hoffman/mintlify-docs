# How Qumis Reads Documents

Qumis doesn't work like a chatbot that happens to read PDFs. It uses a multi-agent AI system specifically trained on insurance document analysis. This page explains how that system works and why it produces better results than general-purpose AI tools.

---

## Multi-agent architecture

When you generate a report or ask a question, Qumis doesn't send your document to a single AI and wait for an answer. Instead, multiple specialized AI agents work together, each handling a different part of the analysis:

**Reader agents** parse your document, identifying its structure — insuring agreements, conditions, exclusions, endorsements, definitions, schedules. They understand how insurance documents are organized and where to find specific types of provisions.

**Analysis agents** interpret the policy language in context. They understand that "bodily injury" has a specific defined meaning, that exclusions may have exceptions, and that endorsements can modify or override base policy provisions.

**Validation agents** cross-check findings against the source document, verifying that citations are accurate and that the analysis is supported by the actual policy language. This reduces errors and catches inconsistencies.

**Synthesis agents** combine the analysis into a coherent, structured output — whether that's a comparison report, a coverage opinion, or an answer to a specific question.

This multi-agent approach is why Qumis produces output that reads like an experienced coverage professional's work product rather than a generic summary.

---

## What Qumis understands about insurance documents

Qumis's AI has been specifically trained on insurance document analysis. This training gives it capabilities that general-purpose AI tools lack:

**Policy structure.** Qumis recognizes the standard architecture of insurance policies — declarations, insuring agreements, conditions, exclusions, definitions, endorsements — and understands how these sections interact. It knows that an endorsement modifying an exclusion changes the scope of coverage, even when the endorsement doesn't repeat the full exclusion language.

**Defined terms.** When a policy defines "occurrence" or "professional services" or "insured," Qumis applies that specific definition throughout its analysis — not the common dictionary meaning, but the policy-specific meaning that controls coverage.

**Coverage mechanics.** Qumis understands how coverage grants, conditions, and exclusions work together. It can trace the logical path from an insuring agreement through applicable conditions and exclusions to determine whether a particular scenario would trigger coverage.

**Document comparison patterns.** When comparing policies, Qumis doesn't just diff the text. It identifies material differences — changes in limits, new exclusions, modified conditions, added or removed endorsements — and explains what those differences mean for coverage.

## How documents flow through the system

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload and parsing">
    When you upload a document, Qumis processes it into a structured representation. It identifies sections, headers, page breaks, tables, and text formatting. This parsing step ensures the AI can navigate the document accurately and cite specific locations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Document classification">
    Qumis identifies what type of document it's working with — a commercial general liability policy, a professional liability quote, a certificate of insurance, a contract with insurance requirements. This classification helps it apply the right analytical framework.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Structural mapping">
    The AI maps the document's internal structure: which sections are coverage grants, which are exclusions, where the definitions are, how endorsements modify the base form. This map is what allows Qumis to trace relationships across the document.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Analysis execution">
    Based on what you're asking — a question, a comparison, a summary, an evaluation — the appropriate agents analyze the document using the structural map. They work through the policy language systematically, the same way a coverage attorney would.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Citation and verification">
    Every finding is linked back to specific policy language. The validation agents verify these citations, ensuring that the analysis is grounded in what the document actually says — not what the AI thinks it might say.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Document types Qumis handles

Qumis is designed to work with the full range of insurance documents you encounter in your daily work:

| Document Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Policies** | CGL, professional liability, property, umbrella/excess, cyber, D&O, EPL, crime, auto |
| **Quotes and proposals** | Carrier quotes, binders, specimen policies, indication letters |
| **Endorsements** | Manuscript endorsements, standard ISO forms, carrier-specific modifications |
| **Certificates** | Certificates of insurance, evidence of coverage |
| **Contracts** | Leases, vendor agreements, loan covenants, construction contracts — any document with insurance requirements |
| **Claims documentation** | Loss reports, claim summaries, demand letters, reservation of rights letters |

<Tip>
  **File formats:** Qumis accepts PDF, DOCX, and common image formats. For best results, upload text-based PDFs rather than scanned images. If you have a scanned document, Qumis can still process it, but text-based files produce faster and more accurate results.
</Tip>

## Why this matters for your work

Understanding how Qumis reads documents helps you use it more effectively:

**Provide context.** When you tell Qumis what you're looking for — "focus on the cyber coverage provisions" or "the client is a manufacturing company with product liability exposure" — the analysis agents can prioritize the most relevant parts of the document.

**Trust the citations.** Because every finding is linked to specific policy language, you can verify any point the AI makes by checking the cited provision. This is your quality control mechanism.

**Ask follow-up questions.** The AI retains its understanding of the document throughout your conversation. If the initial analysis raises questions, ask them — the AI can go deeper on any point without re-reading the document.
