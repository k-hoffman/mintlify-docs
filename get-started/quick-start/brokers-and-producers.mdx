## Your highest-value workflow: Competitive Quote Comparison

The single most impactful thing you can do with Qumis on day one is compare quotes side-by-side. This is the workflow that brokers tell us saves the most time and wins the most business.

### How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload competing quotes to Vault">
    Add the quotes, proposals, or specimen policies you want to compare. Name them clearly — e.g., "Carrier A GL Quote 2025" and "Carrier B GL Quote 2025."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate a Comparison Report">
    Navigate to **Comparison Report** from the sidebar. Add all the documents you want compared. In the **Additional Information** field, add context like: "Focus on coverage differences, sublimits, and exclusions. This is for a manufacturing client with high product liability exposure."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the analysis">
    Qumis produces a detailed side-by-side breakdown covering:
    - **Coverage differences** highlighted across all documents
    - **QumisScore** benchmarking so you can see relative strength at a glance
    - **Strategic leverage insights** — specific language you can use in negotiations
    - **Gaps and exclusions** that may affect your client's risk profile
  </Step>
  <Step title="Chat for deeper analysis">
    Use **Qumis Clerk** (the chat within the report) to ask follow-up questions: "Which policy has the broadest additional insured coverage?" or "Summarize the key differences I should present to the client."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download and present">
    Export the report as PDF for client presentations, or use specific findings in your proposal narrative. The output is formatted for professional delivery — no reformatting needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Other workflows to try this week

### Prospect policy review

When you receive a prospect's current policy, use **Single Policy Analysis** to quickly identify coverage strengths, weaknesses, gaps, and improvement opportunities. This gives you a data-backed narrative for why switching to your program matters.

→ [Single Policy Analysis](/platform/summarize)

### Pre-meeting preparation with Ask

Before a client meeting, pull the relevant policy into **Chats** and ask:
- "What are the three biggest coverage gaps in this policy?"
- "List all sublimits and deductibles with their amounts"
- "Are there any sunset or notice provisions I should flag?"

Use the answers to prepare talking points — you'll walk in better prepared than if you'd spent an hour reading the policy manually.

→ [Ask (Chats)](/platform/chats)

### Coverage Table for proposals

Use the **Coverage Table** report to generate a clean, side-by-side summary of limits, terms, and conditions across multiple options. This is the format clients and CFOs expect to see in a formal proposal.

→ [Coverage Table](/platform/evaluate)

## Tips from experienced users

**Start with your best client.** Don't practice on a random document — use a real renewal or competitive situation. You'll get more value from the analysis and build muscle memory on a case you care about.

**Use Vault as your working library.** Upload the policies, quotes, and forms you work with most frequently. Once they're in Vault, you can pull them into any tool without re-uploading.

**Add context to every report.** The "Additional Information" field makes a real difference. Tell Qumis about the client's industry, key exposures, and what you're looking for. More context means more relevant analysis.

**Save your best prompts.** When you find a question that consistently produces useful answers (like "Identify all coverage grants and their corresponding exclusions"), save it to the **Prompt Library** so you can reuse it across clients.
