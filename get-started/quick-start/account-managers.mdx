## Your highest-value workflow: Renewal Coverage Gap Analysis

The most impactful workflow for account managers is comparing the expiring program to its renewal or market alternatives — and surfacing what changed, what's missing, and what the client needs to know.

### How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload the expiring and renewal policies">
    Add both the expiring policy and the renewal quote (or multiple renewal options) to your **Vault**. Name them clearly — e.g., "Client X GL Expiring 2024" and "Client X GL Renewal 2025."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate a Comparison Report">
    Navigate to **Comparison Report**. Add both documents. In **Additional Information**, provide context: "This is a renewal comparison for a healthcare client. Flag any coverage reductions, new exclusions, or changes to sublimits. The client is sensitive to cyber liability and professional liability coverage."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review coverage changes">
    The report highlights every material difference between the two documents:
    - **Coverage reductions** — limits that decreased, new exclusions added
    - **Coverage improvements** — expanded definitions, broader grants
    - **Unchanged provisions** — the baseline that carried over
    - **QumisScore** — a relative benchmark of each policy's coverage strength
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare your client narrative">
    Use **Qumis Clerk** to ask: "Summarize the three most important changes this client needs to know about" or "Draft talking points for the renewal meeting highlighting coverage gaps." Use the answers to prepare a clear, proactive recommendation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export for the client">
    Download the report as PDF for the renewal meeting, or use the **Coverage Table** report to produce a clean side-by-side summary the client's CFO or risk manager will appreciate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Other workflows to try this week

### Mid-term policy review with Ask

Between renewals, pull the client's current policy into **Chats** and ask proactive questions:
- "Are there any coverage gaps for a company expanding into e-commerce?"
- "Does this policy cover claims arising from AI-generated content?"
- "What happens if the client acquires a subsidiary mid-term?"

This turns routine check-ins into advisory conversations that demonstrate your value.

→ [Ask (Chats)](/platform/chats)

### Contract compliance with Verify

When a client's lease, contract, or vendor agreement requires specific insurance, use **Contract Review** to verify whether their current program meets the requirements. Qumis flags gaps and explains what needs to change — before the landlord or GC does.

→ [Contract Review](/platform/contract-review)

### Portfolio-level coverage summary

For book-of-business reviews, use **Single Policy Analysis** across multiple policies to build a coverage summary for each client. This helps you identify cross-selling opportunities and coverage gaps across the portfolio.

→ [Single Policy Analysis](/platform/summarize)

## For service teams

If your primary role is client service — processing endorsements, handling COIs, or fielding client inquiries — here are the Qumis workflows that save the most time:

**Quick policy lookups.** Instead of reading through a 100-page policy to answer a client's question, pull it into **Chats** and ask: "Does this policy cover water damage from a burst pipe?" You'll get a cited answer in seconds.

**COI and contract verification.** Use **Contract Review** to quickly check whether a COI or policy meets the requirements spelled out in a client's contract. Qumis flags gaps so you can respond to the client or broker immediately.

**Endorsement analysis.** When an endorsement comes in, ask Qumis: "What did this endorsement change from the original policy?" to quickly understand the impact without reading the full document.

## Tips from experienced users

**Front-load renewal prep.** Upload the expiring policy to Vault as soon as you start the renewal cycle — not the week before the meeting. That way it's ready whenever you need it.

**Build a Vault per client.** Keep each client's current policies, endorsements, and key contracts in Vault with consistent naming (e.g., "ClientName - GL 2025"). Your whole team can access them from Shared Vault.

**Use reports to show, not tell.** Instead of explaining coverage gaps verbally, hand the client a Comparison Report. The professional formatting and cited analysis carries more weight than a verbal summary.
