# Quick Start for Risk Managers

This guide covers the Qumis workflows that matter most to risk managers: verifying that insurance programs meet contractual requirements, analyzing exposure across a portfolio, and building a data-driven view of your organization's coverage posture.

<Info>
  **Prerequisites:** Complete [Your First 15 Minutes](/get-started/your-first-15-minutes) first if you haven't already. That guide covers the basics of uploading documents, using Chats, and generating reports.
</Info>

## Your highest-value workflow: Contract Compliance Verification

The most immediate value for risk managers is checking whether your insurance program meets the requirements spelled out in contracts, leases, and vendor agreements. This is typically a manual, clause-by-clause exercise — Qumis reduces it to minutes.

### How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload the contract and the policy">
    Add the contract (lease, vendor agreement, or loan covenant) that specifies insurance requirements to your **Vault**, along with the relevant insurance policy or COI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate a Contract Review report">
    Navigate to **Contract Review** from the sidebar. Add both documents. In **Additional Information**, specify what you're checking: "Verify that our current GL and umbrella policies meet the insurance requirements in Section 14 of this lease agreement. Flag any gaps or deficiencies."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the compliance analysis">
    Qumis produces a structured compliance table:
    - **Each requirement** from the contract, matched against the corresponding policy provision
    - **Compliance status** for each requirement (met, partially met, not met, or not found)
    - **Specific gaps** with explanations — e.g., "Contract requires $5M umbrella; current policy limit is $3M"
    - **Recommendations** for addressing deficiencies
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take action on gaps">
    Use the gap analysis to request endorsements, increase limits, or discuss alternatives with your broker. The report serves as documentation of your compliance review process.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Other workflows to try this week

### Exposure benchmarking with Compare

Use **Compare** to benchmark your current insurance program against market alternatives, prior-year programs, or industry standards. Upload your program and a benchmark document, and Qumis will surface the material differences in coverage, limits, and terms.

This is especially useful during renewal when evaluating whether proposed changes strengthen or weaken your coverage position.

→ [Compare](/analyze/compare/comparing-documents)

### Portfolio coverage assessment with Ask

For a high-level view of your coverage landscape, pull individual policies into **Chats** and ask targeted questions:
- "Does this policy cover cyber events, including ransomware?"
- "What are the aggregate limits and how do they apply across locations?"
- "Are there any sunset clauses or reporting requirements I need to calendar?"

Build a picture of your total program by running these checks across your key policies.

→ [Ask (Chats)](/analyze/ask/chats)

### Coverage Table for board presentations

Use the **Coverage Table** report to produce a clean, structured summary of your insurance program — limits, deductibles, and key terms across all lines. This format is what boards and CFOs expect to see, and it saves hours of manual spreadsheet work.

→ [Coverage Table](/report/types/coverage-table)

## Building a risk management library in Vault

Risk managers often work across many policies, contracts, and compliance documents. Here's how to organize your Vault for efficiency:

**Use Shared Vault for the active insurance program.** Upload all current policies, endorsements, and key contracts so your team — and your broker — can access the same source documents.

**Name files by line and period.** A naming convention like "GL - Carrier - 2025-2026" or "Umbrella - Carrier - 2025-2026" makes it easy to find the right document when you need it.

**Update at every renewal.** When policies renew, upload the new versions immediately. Outdated documents in Vault lead to outdated analysis.

## Tips from experienced users

**Start with your highest-stakes contract.** Pick the lease, vendor agreement, or loan covenant with the most stringent insurance requirements and run a Contract Review. The gaps it finds will demonstrate the tool's value immediately.

**Build a renewal prep checklist.** Before each renewal, generate a Comparison Report (expiring vs. renewal), a Coverage Table (for your broker and board), and a Contract Review against your top 3 contracts. This three-report workflow covers the risk manager's core renewal responsibilities.

**Use Evaluate for scenario planning.** When your organization is considering a new activity, acquisition, or market — run a Claims Analysis against a hypothetical loss scenario to test whether your current program would respond. This proactive approach identifies gaps before they become claims.
