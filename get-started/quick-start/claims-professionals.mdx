## Your highest-value workflow: Coverage Determination

The core claims workflow in Qumis is evaluating whether a policy responds to a specific loss. This is the workflow that claims teams tell us most directly reduces their reliance on outside counsel for routine determinations.

### How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload the policy and claim documentation">
    Add the relevant policy (and any endorsements) to your **Vault**, along with the claim file — the first notice of loss, adjuster's report, or any supporting documentation that describes the claim.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate a Claims Analysis report">
    Navigate to **Claims Analysis** from the sidebar. Add the policy and claim documents. In **Additional Information**, describe the claim: "Water damage claim from burst pipe in a commercial tenant space. Client is alleging landlord negligence. Need to determine whether the CGL policy responds, and whether the property exclusion applies."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the coverage opinion">
    Qumis produces a structured coverage analysis:
    - **Coverage grants** that potentially apply, with policy language citations
    - **Exclusions** that may bar coverage, with specific clause references
    - **Conditions** and duties that affect coverage (notice, cooperation, etc.)
    - **Legal reasoning** — how the policy language interacts with the facts of the claim
    - **Conclusion** — a supported determination with confidence assessment
  </Step>
  <Step title="Research supporting precedent">
    Use **Legal Search** to find case law that supports or challenges the determination. Search for the specific coverage issue — e.g., "CGL property exclusion tenant water damage" — and review relevant opinions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Document your analysis">
    Download the report as PDF. The structured format with citations and legal reasoning serves as documentation of your analysis — useful for internal review, reserving decisions, and potential litigation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Other workflows to try this week

### Prior vs. current policy comparison

When a claim spans multiple policy periods, use **Compare** to see exactly what changed between the policy in effect at the time of loss and the current or prior policy. This is critical for occurrence-based claims that may trigger coverage under different policy periods.

→ [Compare](/platform/compare)

### Rapid coverage questions with Ask

For quick coverage checks before committing to a full analysis, pull the policy into **Chats** and ask:
- "Does this policy cover loss of income due to a cyber event?"
- "What are the notice requirements under this policy?"
- "Is there a pollution exclusion, and does it have a hostile fire exception?"

Use Chats as a triage step — if the answer is clear, you may not need a full Claims Analysis report.

→ [Ask (Chats)](/platform/chats)

### Coverage Table for complex claims

For claims involving multiple policies or coverage parts, generate a **Coverage Table** to see all applicable limits, deductibles, and conditions in one view. This is especially useful for property claims with multiple sublimits or umbrella/excess tower analysis.

→ [Coverage Table](/platform/evaluate)

## A note on AI and claims decisions

Qumis is a tool for analysis, not a replacement for professional judgment. Every coverage determination involves facts, policy language, applicable law, and professional experience — and the final decision belongs to you.

What Qumis does is accelerate the analysis phase. It reads policy language, identifies relevant provisions, applies legal reasoning, and produces a documented analysis in minutes rather than hours. You bring the judgment, the context, and the accountability.

Use the citations in every Qumis output to verify the analysis against the original documents. The goal is to give you a thorough first draft that you refine with your expertise — not to bypass the human review that makes coverage determinations defensible.

## Tips from experienced users

**Describe the claim clearly.** The more specific your claim description in the "Additional Information" field, the more targeted the analysis. Include the type of loss, the parties involved, the alleged cause, and the specific coverage question you need answered.

**Pair Claims Analysis with Legal Search.** Generate the coverage opinion first, then use Legal Search to find case law supporting (or challenging) the determination. This two-step process mirrors how experienced claims professionals and coverage counsel work.

**Save claims-specific prompts.** Build a library of prompts for common coverage questions — "Identify all exclusions that could apply to a [type] claim," "What conditions precedent must be satisfied for coverage," "Does the policy's definition of [term] encompass [scenario]."
