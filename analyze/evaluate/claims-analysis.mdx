Claims analysis in Qumis helps you determine whether a policy provides coverage for a specific claim or loss scenario. By leveraging AI to compare policy language with claim facts, you can make faster, more defensible coverage determinations.

## Understanding Claims Analysis

Claims analysis is the process of evaluating whether a specific claim falls within the scope of coverage under one or more insurance policies. Qumis automates this by:

1. **Extracting policy language** - Identifying insuring agreements, exclusions, conditions, definitions, and endorsements from policy documents
2. **Analyzing claim facts** - Processing the specific circumstances of the claim or loss event
3. **Comparing provisions** - Determining how policy language applies to your claim scenario
4. **Building supporting arguments** - Identifying coverage-supporting and coverage-limiting provisions with citations

The output is a structured coverage position with supporting reasoning, citations, and confidence indicators.

## When to Use Claims Analysis

Use claims analysis when you need to:

- **Evaluate new claims** - Determine coverage position for incoming claim notifications
- **Resolve coverage disputes** - Analyze ambiguous policy language or novel claim scenarios
- **Draft reservation of rights** - Support your position with cited policy language and reasoning
- **Review claim denials** - Verify the legal and contractual basis for a denial decision
- **Manage claims counsel** - Provide outside counsel with your preliminary analysis and supporting materials

## What Qumis Analyzes

Qumis evaluates the following elements of your insurance contracts:

- **Insuring agreements** - Does the claim fall within the primary coverage grant?
- **Exclusions** - Are there specific exclusions that eliminate coverage?
- **Conditions** - Are policy conditions met (notice, cooperation, proof of loss)?
- **Definitions** - Do defined terms in the policy apply to your claim?
- **Endorsements** - Do endorsements expand, restrict, or modify coverage?
- **Limits and deductibles** - What is the maximum and minimum company exposure?

## How Claims Analysis Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Policy Documents">
    Provide the policy documents you want analyzed. You can include the base policy, all endorsements, and related documents.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe the Claim Scenario">
    Provide facts about the claim: What happened? When? Who was involved? What is the nature of the loss? Qumis uses these facts to evaluate coverage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Analysis Scope">
    Choose whether to analyze the full policy or focus on specific coverage parts or endorsements. You can also indicate priority areas (e.g., liability vs. coverage defense).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate Analysis">
    Qumis analyzes policy language against claim facts and produces a structured coverage position with citations and supporting reasoning.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and Refine">
    Review Qumis's analysis. You can ask follow-up questions, request deeper analysis of specific provisions, or generate a formatted report for sharing with counsel or other stakeholders.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key Outputs

A claims analysis provides:

- **Coverage position** - A clear determination (coverage likely, coverage unlikely, or coverage uncertain) with a confidence level
- **Supporting arguments** - Provisions and reasoning that support coverage
- **Limiting arguments** - Exclusions, conditions, or other provisions that could limit or deny coverage
- **Cited policy language** - Direct quotes from the policy with page references
- **Analysis by section** - Structured assessment of each relevant policy section (insuring agreement, exclusions, conditions, etc.)

## Important Limitations

While Qumis claims analysis is powerful, be aware of these limitations:

<Warning>
Qumis analysis should support—not replace—human legal judgment. Complex multi-party claims, novel coverage questions, and high-exposure determinations should always involve qualified coverage counsel. Use Qumis to organize information and support your analysis, not to make final determinations in isolation.
</Warning>

Qumis may have difficulty with:

- **Complex multi-party claims** - Claims involving multiple policies, multiple insureds, or multiple loss events
- **Novel coverage questions** - Emerging risks or claim types without established case law
- **Heavily negotiated policies** - Non-standard policy language or extensive customizations
- **Regulatory or statutory considerations** - Coverage variations based on state law or regulatory requirements
