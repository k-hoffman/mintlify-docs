# Building Defensible Determinations

This advanced workflow combines Qumis's Evaluate capability with Legal Search to build coverage positions that are thorough, well-documented, and defensible. It's designed for claims professionals who need to produce coverage analysis that can withstand scrutiny — from coverage counsel, regulators, or courts.

---

## The three-step framework

<Steps>
  <Step title="Analyze the policy language">
    Start with the policy. Upload the relevant policy (including all endorsements) and ask Qumis to analyze the specific provisions that apply to your claim scenario.

    **Key prompts:**
    - "Analyze whether the insuring agreement in Section [X] responds to [specific facts]."
    - "Identify every exclusion that could potentially apply to a claim involving [scenario]."
    - "For each applicable exclusion, are there any exceptions that might restore coverage?"
    - "What conditions precedent must be satisfied for coverage to apply?"

    Document your findings from this step — they form the foundation of your determination.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Research the legal landscape">
    Use Legal Search to find how courts have interpreted similar policy language in similar fact patterns. This adds legal authority to your policy analysis.

    **Key prompts:**
    - "Find case law interpreting [specific policy language] in the context of [claim type]."
    - "How have courts in [jurisdiction] applied the [specific exclusion] to claims involving [scenario]?"
    - "Are there any split of authority issues on the interpretation of [specific provision]?"

    → [Legal Search](/research/legal-search) | [Coverage Determination](/research/coverage-determination)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build the documented determination">
    Combine your policy analysis and legal research into a structured coverage determination. Ask Qumis to help you organize the analysis.

    **Key prompts:**
    - "Based on our analysis of the policy language and the relevant case law, draft a coverage determination for [claim scenario]. Structure it as: facts, policy analysis, legal authority, conclusion."
    - "What counterarguments could the opposing side raise, and how would we address each one?"
    - "Draft the coverage analysis section of a reservation of rights letter based on our findings."
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What makes a determination defensible

**Policy text first.** Start with what the policy actually says. Every conclusion in your determination should trace back to specific policy language. Qumis provides citations to help you build this chain.

**Legal authority.** Where available, support your interpretation of policy language with case law. Courts interpreting identical or substantially similar language in similar fact patterns strengthen your position.

**Counterargument awareness.** A defensible determination anticipates and addresses the arguments the other side will make. Ask Qumis to identify potential counterarguments.

**Clear reasoning chain.** The determination should walk logically from facts → policy language → interpretation → conclusion. Each step should be documented.

## Common determination scenarios

### Scenario 1: Coverage exists

When your analysis concludes that coverage exists, document: the specific insuring agreement that responds, the facts that trigger coverage, any conditions that have been (or must be) satisfied, and the applicable limits and deductibles.

### Scenario 2: Coverage is excluded

When an exclusion applies, document: the specific exclusion and its relevant language, why the claim facts fall within the exclusion, whether any exception to the exclusion might apply, and (if applicable) case law supporting the exclusion's applicability.

### Scenario 3: Coverage is uncertain

When reasonable professionals could disagree, document: the policy language creating the ambiguity, the competing interpretations, the legal authority supporting each interpretation, and your recommendation with reasoning.

<Info>
  **A note on professional judgment:** Qumis provides analysis to support your determination, not to replace your professional judgment. Coverage determinations involve evaluating facts, interpreting language, considering jurisdiction-specific law, and applying professional experience. Qumis accelerates the analytical work — the determination itself requires human expertise.
</Info>

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    While building formal coverage determinations is typically a claims function, brokers benefit from understanding this process when advising clients on coverage adequacy. Use the framework to identify potential gaps in a client's program — "If a claim like [scenario] occurred, would the policy respond?"
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    Use this framework when clients ask about coverage for specific scenarios. You can run a policy analysis and legal search to provide an informed response — while clearly noting that formal coverage determinations should involve the client's legal counsel.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    This is your primary advanced workflow. Build it into your routine for any coverage question that involves ambiguous language, significant exposure, or the potential for litigation. The documentation you produce at each step creates a defensible record.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    Use this framework when evaluating whether your insurance program would respond to specific loss scenarios. The three-step process — analyze the policy, research the legal landscape, document the determination — applies equally to proactive risk assessment.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
