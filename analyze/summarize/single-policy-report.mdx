Single policy analysis provides a comprehensive, structured summary of an individual insurance policy. Instead of navigating complex policy documents page-by-page, you get an organized summary that extracts what matters: coverage details, limits, deductibles, exclusions, and notable provisions.

## Understanding Single Policy Analysis

Single policy analysis uses AI to read an entire insurance policy and extract:

- **Policy basics** - Named insured, policy period, policy number, form numbers
- **Coverage parts** - Each coverage section included in the policy
- **Limits and deductibles** - Maximum coverage amounts and out-of-pocket costs
- **Insuring agreements** - What the policy covers (simplified)
- **Key exclusions** - What the policy does NOT cover
- **Notable endorsements** - Modifications that expand, restrict, or clarify coverage
- **Important conditions** - Requirements for coverage (notice deadlines, cooperation, proof of loss, etc.)
- **Definitions** - How the policy defines critical terms
- **Special provisions** - Unusual terms, limitations, or requirements unique to the policy

The result is an organized, easy-to-understand summary of the policy—not a replacement for the full policy document, but a roadmap to understanding it.

## When to Use Single Policy Analysis

Use single policy analysis when you need to:

- **Review a new policy** - Quickly understand what you have
- **Prepare for renewal** - Get a fresh understanding of current coverage before discussing changes
- **Onboard a new client** - Create a summary document for client meetings
- **Evaluate coverage adequacy** - Understand what the policy covers before determining if it's appropriate
- **Brief a colleague or counsel** - Create a summary for someone who needs to understand the policy quickly
- **Support a claim investigation** - Get a complete overview of coverage before facts emerge
- **Prepare for a coverage dispute** - Understand the policy language thoroughly before taking positions

## What Gets Extracted

A single policy summary includes:

### Policy Identification
- Named insured
- Policy number and form numbers
- Policy period (effective and expiration dates)
- Carrier and underwriter
- Broker/producer information

### Coverage Overview
- Each coverage part included (General Liability, Property, etc.)
- Coverage territory
- Occupancy or business classification

### Coverage Details by Section
For each coverage part:
- Insuring agreement (what's covered, simplified)
- Limits of liability (per occurrence, aggregate, etc.)
- Deductibles or retentions
- Premium or rate information
- Any sub-limits (specific coverages with lower limits)

### Exclusions
- Major exclusions that eliminate coverage
- Commonly encountered exclusions (e.g., pollution, war, criminal acts)
- Any unusual exclusions specific to this policy

### Endorsements
- Every endorsement included, with brief description
- Whether endorsements expand coverage, restrict coverage, or clarify terms
- Special conditions created by endorsements

### Important Conditions
- Notice and reporting requirements
- Cooperation requirements
- Proof of loss requirements
- Premium payment terms
- Cancellation/non-renewal conditions
- Other policy conditions affecting coverage

### Definitions
- Key defined terms and how the policy defines them
- Terms that might be interpreted differently than you'd expect

### Notable Provisions
- Unique or unusual terms
- Provisions that differ from standard forms
- Anything unexpected or important

## How Results Are Organized

Results are organized in sections that mirror the policy's structure:

1. **Executive summary** - One-page overview of coverage and key points
2. **Policy basics** - Named insured, period, form numbers
3. **Coverage summaries** - Each coverage part summarized clearly
4. **Limits and deductibles** - Easy-to-reference financial terms
5. **Key exclusions** - What's NOT covered
6. **Endorsements** - Every endorsement listed and explained
7. **Important conditions** - Requirements for coverage to apply
8. **Definitions** - How critical terms are defined
9. **Special provisions** - Anything unusual or notable

This organization makes it easy to find what you're looking for without reading the entire policy document.

## Single vs. Multi-Policy Analysis

Single policy analysis covers ONE policy. If you need to:

- **Compare two policies** - Use comparison analysis instead
- **Review your complete program** - Use coverage table analysis
- **Analyze coverage for a claim** - Use claims analysis

Single policy analysis is most useful for getting a complete understanding of one specific policy.

## Next Steps

Once you've analyzed a single policy:

- **Generate a report** - Create a formatted policy analysis report for sharing, filing, or reference
- **Compare with renewal** - If getting a renewal, compare the new proposal with this analysis
- **Evaluate adequacy** - Use the summary to determine if coverage limits and terms are appropriate
- **Research coverage questions** - If you have specific questions about coverage, drill deeper into policy language
