# Policy Analysis

Summarize is the capability that turns a complex insurance policy into a clear, structured analysis. Whether you're preparing for a client meeting, reviewing a new submission, or evaluating coverage adequacy, policy analysis gives you a comprehensive understanding of what a policy covers — and what it doesn't.

---

## What Qumis extracts

When you run a policy analysis, Qumis reads the entire document — including all endorsements, schedules, and defined terms — and produces a structured summary covering:

**Coverage overview.** What the policy covers, who is insured, and the key insuring agreements.

**Limits and deductibles.** Per-occurrence limits, aggregates, sublimits by coverage type, and deductible structure.

**Key exclusions.** The most significant exclusions and their practical impact. Qumis flags exclusions that are broader or narrower than standard market forms.

**Conditions and requirements.** Notice provisions, cooperation clauses, cancellation terms, and other conditions that could affect coverage.

**Endorsements.** How endorsements modify the base policy — which coverages they add, restrict, or eliminate.

**Defined terms.** Key definitions that control the scope of coverage, especially where they differ from standard definitions.

---

## When to use policy analysis

**New submissions.** Quickly understand what a prospect's current program covers so you can identify improvement opportunities.

**Pre-meeting preparation.** Get a structured overview of a client's policy before a review meeting. Know the key provisions, limitations, and gaps before the conversation starts.

**Coverage adequacy reviews.** Systematically evaluate whether a policy provides appropriate coverage for the insured's risk profile.

**Onboarding a new account.** When taking over an account from another broker, policy analysis gives you a rapid understanding of the existing program.

---

## Getting the most from policy analysis

**Specify what matters.** If you're reviewing a property policy for a manufacturing client, tell Qumis: "Focus on business income coverage, equipment breakdown, and environmental liability provisions." This produces more relevant analysis than a generic summary.

**Ask about specific scenarios.** After the initial analysis, follow up with scenario-based questions: "Based on this policy, would a supply chain disruption that shuts down production for 60 days be covered?"

**Request the client-ready version.** Ask Qumis to reformat key findings for a client letter or presentation: "Summarize the three most important coverage features and the two most significant gaps in language suitable for a CFO audience."

## Policy analysis vs. Single Policy Report

**Policy analysis in Chat** (Summarize capability) — Conversational. Ask questions, get summaries, dig into specific provisions. Good for exploration and understanding.

**Single Policy Analysis Report** (in the Report tab) — Structured, formatted document. Good for producing a polished analysis you can file, share, or present. → [Single Policy Analysis Report](/report/types/single-policy-analysis)

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    **Prospect analysis:** Upload a prospect's current policy and ask Qumis to identify the top 5 coverage gaps. Use these as talking points in your pitch meeting.

    **Pre-meeting prep:** Before any client meeting, run a quick policy analysis to refresh your understanding of their coverage. Focus on areas the client is likely to ask about.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    **Renewal preparation:** Analyze the current policy before the renewal process begins. Identify provisions that need renegotiation and coverage gaps to address.

    **Client advisory letters:** Use the analysis as a foundation for coverage letters that document what the policy covers and recommend improvements.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    **Rapid triage:** When a new claim comes in, run a policy analysis focused on the relevant coverage provisions. This gives you a starting point for your coverage determination.

    **Coverage mapping:** Ask Qumis to map specific claim facts against the policy provisions — insuring agreement, exclusions, conditions — to build your analysis.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    **Program review:** Analyze each policy in your portfolio individually, then use the findings to assess overall program adequacy.

    **Board reporting:** Request executive-level summaries of each major policy for board presentations. Specify "two paragraphs maximum, focusing on what's covered, key limits, and the most significant gaps."
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
