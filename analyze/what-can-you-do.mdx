# What Do You Need to Do?

Qumis organizes its platform around eight capabilities. Six of them live here in the Analyze tab — Ask, Compare, Summarize, Evaluate, Vault, and Prompts. The other two — [Populate](/report/populate/data-extraction) and [Verify](/report/verify/contract-compliance) — live in the [Report tab](/report/reports-overview) because they produce structured output (spreadsheets and compliance tables). Start with what you're trying to accomplish, and we'll point you to the right tool.

---

## Choose your task

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Ask a question about a document" icon="message-question" href="/analyze/ask/chats">
    **Ask** — Get answers from your documents

    Upload a policy, quote, endorsement, or claim file and ask plain-English questions. Get structured answers with citations pointing to the exact clauses Qumis referenced. Best for: quick coverage checks, pre-meeting prep, targeted policy questions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Compare two or more documents" icon="files" href="/analyze/compare/comparing-documents">
    **Compare** — Understand what changed, and what it means

    Side-by-side analysis of quotes, renewal vs. expiring policies, or any two documents. Qumis highlights every material difference in coverage, limits, exclusions, and terms. Best for: competitive quoting, renewal analysis, coverage benchmarking.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Summarize a single policy" icon="list-check" href="/analyze/summarize/policy-analysis">
    **Summarize** — See what's covered, and what's not

    Comprehensive analysis of one policy — coverage grants, exclusions, sublimits, conditions, and key provisions. Best for: prospect policy review, coverage gap identification, onboarding to a new account.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Evaluate coverage for a claim" icon="scale-balanced" href="/analyze/evaluate/claims-analysis">
    **Evaluate** — Make defensible coverage calls fast

    Assess whether a policy responds to a specific claim or scenario with structured legal reasoning, cited policy language, and a supported determination. Best for: coverage determination, claims analysis, scenario planning.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Store and organize documents" icon="lock" href="/analyze/vault/document-vault">
    **Vault** — Upload once, reuse forever

    Your secure digital filing cabinet. Upload documents once and access them from any Qumis tool — Chats, Reports, Comparisons. Private or shared with your team. Best for: organizing client files, team collaboration, streamlining workflows.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Save reusable questions" icon="sparkles" href="/analyze/prompts/prompt-library">
    **Prompts** — Save your smartest questions

    Build a library of your best questions and analysis templates. Reuse them across clients and documents to maintain consistency. Best for: standardizing analysis, building team playbooks, capturing institutional knowledge.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Not sure where to start?

Here's a quick decision guide:

| If you want to... | Use |
|---|---|
| Ask a specific question about a policy | **[Ask](/analyze/ask/chats)** |
| See what changed between two documents | **[Compare](/analyze/compare/comparing-documents)** |
| Get an overview of a policy you haven't read | **[Summarize](/analyze/summarize/policy-analysis)** |
| Determine if a policy covers a specific claim | **[Evaluate](/analyze/evaluate/claims-analysis)** |
| Store documents for reuse across tools | **[Vault](/analyze/vault/document-vault)** |
| Save and reuse your best questions | **[Prompts](/analyze/prompts/prompt-library)** |
| Generate a client-ready report or spreadsheet | **[Go to Reports →](/report/choosing-the-right-report)** |

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    Start with **Compare** to analyze competing quotes, then use **Ask** for pre-meeting prep. **Summarize** is great for quickly reviewing a prospect's incumbent policy before your first meeting.

    → [Broker & Producer Quick Start](/get-started/quick-start/brokers-and-producers)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    Start with **Compare** for renewal analysis (expiring vs. renewal), then use **Ask** for proactive mid-term coverage checks. **Summarize** helps you prepare for book reviews.

    → [Account Manager Quick Start](/get-started/quick-start/account-managers)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    Start with **Evaluate** for coverage determinations, then use **Ask** for quick coverage triage. **Compare** is essential when a claim spans multiple policy periods.

    → [Claims Professional Quick Start](/get-started/quick-start/claims-professionals)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    Start with **Compare** to benchmark your program, then use **Ask** for targeted exposure questions. **Evaluate** is useful for scenario planning against hypothetical losses.

    → [Risk Manager Quick Start](/get-started/quick-start/risk-managers)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
