# Comparing Documents

Compare is one of Qumis's most powerful capabilities — and the feature that produces the highest retention among new users. This page explains when to compare, what Qumis looks for, and how to get the most from your comparisons.

---

## When to compare

Document comparison is valuable whenever you need to understand differences between two insurance-related documents. The most common scenarios:

**Quote vs. quote.** A client has received competing quotes. You need to identify which provides better coverage for their specific risks, where the meaningful differences lie, and which tradeoffs each quote involves.

**Renewal vs. expiring.** A renewal has arrived. You need to identify every change from the current policy — additions, removals, modifications to limits, deductibles, exclusions, and conditions.

**Policy vs. contract requirements.** A client's contract specifies insurance requirements. You need to verify that the actual policy meets every requirement and flag any gaps.

**Current vs. proposed endorsement.** A carrier has offered an endorsement modification. You need to understand exactly what changes and whether it broadens or restricts coverage.

---

## What Qumis compares

When you upload two documents and run a comparison, Qumis doesn't just diff the text. It performs a structured coverage analysis that identifies:

**Limits and deductibles.** Changes in per-occurrence limits, aggregate limits, sublimits, and deductible structures.

**Coverage grants.** New coverages added, existing coverages removed, or modifications to the scope of insuring agreements.

**Exclusions.** New exclusions, removed exclusions, or modifications to exclusion language — including changes to exceptions within exclusions.

**Conditions.** Changes to notice requirements, cooperation clauses, other insurance provisions, and subrogation.

**Definitions.** Changes to defined terms that affect the scope of coverage.

**Endorsements.** Added or removed endorsements and modifications to existing endorsement forms.

For each difference, Qumis explains the practical impact — not just what changed, but what it means for coverage.

## How to structure an effective comparison

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload both documents">
    Upload the two documents you want to compare. Qumis works best with text-based PDFs. Label them clearly so the output references the right document.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Specify your focus">
    Tell Qumis what matters most. "Compare these two CGL quotes with a focus on products-completed operations coverage, additional insured provisions, and professional liability coverage" produces more targeted analysis than "Compare these quotes."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the structured output">
    Qumis produces a comparison organized by category — limits, coverage grants, exclusions, conditions, etc. Each finding includes citations to both documents so you can verify.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask follow-up questions">
    Drill into the findings that matter: "Explain the practical impact of the different cyber exclusion language in Quote A versus Quote B for a technology company client."
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Comparison vs. Comparison Report

Qumis offers two ways to compare documents:

**Compare in Chat** (the Ask capability) — Conversational, iterative. Good for exploratory analysis where you want to ask follow-up questions and dig deeper into specific findings.

**Comparison Report** (in the Report tab) — Structured, formatted output. Good for producing a polished analysis you can share with clients or colleagues. → [Comparison Report](/report/types/comparison-report)

Both use the same underlying analysis engine. The difference is in the output format and the ability to iterate.

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    **Competitive quoting workflow:** Upload competing quotes and ask Qumis to compare them with a focus on the coverages that matter most to your client. Request a summary paragraph you can include in your recommendation.

    **Monday Quote Comparison:** Make this your weekly habit — every Monday, run a comparison on the quotes that came in over the past week. This is the recommended pilot workflow for the Ascend™ implementation framework.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    **Renewal analysis:** Compare the expiring policy against the renewal quote to identify every change. Flag items that need discussion with the client — especially reduced limits, new exclusions, or increased deductibles.

    **Contract compliance:** Compare the client's policy against their lease or vendor agreement to verify that insurance requirements are met.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    **Prior vs. current policy:** When a claim spans multiple policy periods, compare the prior and current policies to determine which provisions apply and where coverage may differ.

    **Coverage opinion support:** Compare the policy language against the claim facts to build your coverage analysis.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    **Contract compliance verification:** Compare your insurance program against contractual requirements to identify gaps. This is the recommended pilot workflow for risk managers in the Ascend™ framework.

    **Year-over-year tracking:** Compare this year's renewal against last year's policy to track how your coverage program is evolving.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
