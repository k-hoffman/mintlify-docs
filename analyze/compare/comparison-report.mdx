Comparison analysis helps you quickly identify differences between insurance documents. Whether you're evaluating competitive quotes, reviewing renewal changes, or analyzing policy versions, Qumis provides structured, side-by-side comparison to highlight what's changed and what matters most.

## Understanding Comparison Analysis

A comparison analysis examines two or more insurance documents and identifies:

- **Coverage term differences** - Changes to insuring agreements and coverage grants
- **Limit and deductible changes** - Shifts in financial terms
- **Exclusion differences** - New, removed, or modified exclusions
- **Condition changes** - Updates to policy conditions and requirements
- **Endorsement additions or removals** - Changes in endorsement status
- **Premium and rating changes** - Pricing impacts
- **Definition updates** - Changes in how terms are defined

The output is organized section-by-section with clear change indicators showing what's been added, removed, or modified.

## Types of Comparisons

### Quote vs. Quote
Compare competitive quotes to evaluate:
- Coverage breadth across carriers
- Limit and deductible differences
- Premium pricing and rating approaches
- Endorsement inclusions
- Exclusion positioning

### Renewal vs. Expiring
Compare your renewal proposal to the expiring policy:
- Coverage enhancements or reductions
- Rate changes and reasons
- Endorsement changes
- Condition modifications
- Limit adjustments

### Policy vs. Contract Requirements
Compare your insurance policy to a third-party requirement (client contracts, lease agreements, regulatory requirements):
- Coverage gaps relative to requirements
- Term differences
- Insured/additional insured changes
- Limits and deductibles vs. requirements

### Coverage vs. Benchmark
Compare your policy to industry benchmarks or best practices:
- Market-standard terms and conditions
- Appropriate limit levels
- Common exclusion positions
- Endorsement recommendations

## When to Use Comparison Analysis

Use comparison analysis when you need to:

- **Evaluate competitive quotes** - Determine which quote best meets your needs and client budget
- **Review renewal changes** - Understand what's different in your renewal and whether changes are favorable
- **Identify coverage gaps** - Compare your policy to contract requirements or benchmarks
- **Track historical changes** - Understand how your coverage has evolved over time
- **Support negotiation** - Show carriers or brokers exactly what needs to change
- **Perform compliance review** - Verify policies meet contractual or regulatory requirements

## How Comparison Analysis Works

Comparison analysis examines:

### Coverage Terms
- Insuring agreement language and scope
- Named coverages and endorsements
- Coverage enhancements or restrictions
- Any changes in what's covered

### Financial Terms
- Limits of liability
- Deductibles and self-insured retentions
- Aggregate limits
- Premium and rating changes

### Exclusions
- Specific exclusions
- Condition exclusions (failure to meet conditions = no coverage)
- Geographic or temporal limitations
- Industry-specific exclusions

### Conditions
- Notice requirements and timelines
- Cooperation and subrogation terms
- Proof of loss requirements
- Premium payment terms
- Cancellation and non-renewal conditions

### Endorsements
- Which endorsements are included
- Modification or deletion of standard terms
- Special conditions or limitations added
- Premium impacts of endorsements

## Output Organization

Comparison results are organized section-by-section:

- **Coverage summary** - Quick overview of coverage terms for each document
- **Detailed comparison by section** - Side-by-side language for each policy section
- **Change indicators** - Visual markers showing Added, Removed, Modified, or Unchanged
- **Coverage gaps** - Provisions in one document but not another
- **Summary of changes** - One-page overview of key differences
- **Material changes highlight** - Focus on changes that impact coverage or cost
