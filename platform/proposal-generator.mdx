The Proposal Generator turns your uploaded policy documents into professional, client-ready proposals. Choose a template, customize the sections to fit your account, and Qumis produces a fully formatted document you can edit, brand, and export as PDF — all in minutes instead of hours.

---

## What this tool does

The Proposal Generator is built for brokers and producers who need to deliver polished proposals quickly. With this tool, you can:

- Generate multi-section proposals from uploaded policies, quotes, and supporting documents.
- Apply branded templates with your organization's themes, colors, and logo.
- Include coverage comparison tables, executive summaries, marketing logs, and recommendations — all extracted from your source documents.
- Edit the generated proposal in a rich document editor with formatting controls similar to Microsoft Word or Google Docs.
- Switch individual sections between portrait and landscape orientation for wide tables.
- Export to PDF or print directly from the editor.

Templates are reusable, shareable, and lockable — so your team produces consistent proposals every time.

## Before you start

<Note>
  **Prerequisites:**

  - Have your policy documents ready (quotes, binders, applications, expiring policies). Qumis supports up to 20 attachments or 1,000 pages in the aggregate.
  - Know what sections you want in the proposal (e.g., executive summary, coverage comparison, recommended program, binding instructions).
  - If you plan to use an existing template, confirm it's available under **My Templates** or **Organization Templates**.
  - Have 10–15 minutes for generation and fine-tuning.
</Note>

    - **Lock Template Sections** — Toggle on to prevent users from editing, adding, or removing sections when they generate a proposal with this template. This ensures consistency across your team's output.

    - Remove sections that aren't relevant to this account.
    - Edit the prompts within each section to tailor the output to the specific considerations of this proposal.

    This is where you customize a reusable template for the unique needs of an individual account — adding client-specific context, adjusting focus areas, or refining instructions before generation.

    When you're satisfied with the sections, click the **Proposal Generator** button at the bottom to start generation.

    Proposals may take up to **10 minutes** to generate depending on the number of documents and complexity of the template.

    Key editing capabilities include:

    - Text formatting (bold, italic, underline, font family, size, and color)
    - Paragraph alignment and list styles
    - Title and heading styles (Title 1, Title 2, Title 3)

    - Markdown mode for advanced editing

## Writing effective section prompts

The prompts you write for each template section directly control the quality of Qumis's output. Whether you're building a new template or refining an existing one, these principles will help you get the best results.

**Be more specific than you think is necessary.** If you want Qumis to create a table, specify the columns and their order. If you want a narrative summary, describe exactly what information to include. Vague prompts produce generic output; precise prompts produce proposals that need minimal editing.

**Use "verbatim" for exact wording.** If there's text you want Qumis to display word-for-word, instruct it clearly — for example, "Show this text verbatim: [your text here]." This is useful for legal disclaimers, standard disclosures, or boilerplate language your firm requires in every proposal.

**Lock text with double brackets to prevent editing.** If you want verbatim text to be non-editable once the proposal is generated, add an empty line above and below the text you want to lock. Type `[[` on the empty line above the locked text and `]]` on the empty line below it. Anything between the double brackets cannot be edited by users generating proposals with this template.

**For coverage comparison tables, list extraction items explicitly.** Tell Qumis to create a table using the source documents, then list each coverage item you want extracted — each item becomes a separate row in the generated table.

<Tip>
  Copy and paste checklists you've saved in the [Prompt Library](/platform/prompt-library) to accelerate template development — especially for coverage comparison sections where you already have line-of-business checklists defined.
</Tip>

## Example section prompts

These prompts can be copied directly into your template sections. Adjust them to match your firm's proposal structure and terminology.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Executive Summary">
    ```
    The executive summary must contain the following information.
    Make each item a separate paragraph and use all of the uploaded
    documents to generate the content.

    1. Agreed Strategy: provide a narrative description of the
       marketing strategy, including how the account was marketed
       to insurance carriers.
    2. Marketing Results: summarize the number of markets approached,
       the number that provided quotes, and any common reasons
       carriers declined. If there were common themes in the
       reason carriers declined to quote, provide a brief
       explanation.
    3. Change in Premium: calculate the percentage change in premium
       relative to the expiring term. Present the results in a table
       with three columns in this order: Name of Carrier, Percent
       Difference from Expiring Premium, Reason for Change. Reasons
       may include exposure changes, rate changes, or changes to
       the limits.
    4. Recommended Course of Action: review the quotes obtained and
       provide a recommendation on which quote the insured should
       select. Balance the breadth of each quote's coverage against
       the needs of the business, the exclusions and limitations,
       the cost, the limits offered, the size and financial stability
       of each carrier, and the quality of claims handling. Explain
       your reasoning to the client.

    If the source documents don't provide enough information to
    complete any item, insert a clearly visible note so the user
    knows they need to manually enter this information. Make these
    notes noticeable so the user doesn't accidentally miss them.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Named Insured Schedule">
    ```
    Extract the full list of named insureds from the source
    documents. Present them in a table with two columns: First
    Named Insured and Additional Named Insureds.

    If the documents list subsidiaries, DBA names, or affiliated
    entities, include those as additional named insureds. If no
    additional named insureds are found, note that the field
    requires manual entry.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Marketing Summary">
    ```
    Create a marketing log table showing each carrier that was
    approached for this account. The table must have the following
    columns in this order: Line of Coverage, Carrier Name, Status,
    and Quoted/Indicated Premium.

    For the Status column, use one of the following values:
    Quoted, Declined, Indication, or No Response.

    Populate the table from the source documents. Sort the table
    by line of coverage, then by status with Quoted carriers first,
    followed by Indication, Declined, and No Response.

    If the source documents don't contain enough information to
    complete the table, insert a clearly visible note listing
    which fields need to be filled in manually.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Quote Comparison">
    ```
    Create a detailed coverage comparison table using the source
    documents. The table should have one column for each quoted
    carrier, plus a column for the expiring policy. Present the
    recommended option in the second column for easy reference.
    Extract and compare the following items as separate rows:

    - Issuing Company
    - AM Best Rating
    - Admitted / Non-Admitted
    - Policy Period
    - Premium
    - Commission
    - General Aggregate Limit
    - Per Occurrence Limit
    - Deductible / Retention
    - Defense Cost Treatment
    - Retroactive Date
    - Extended Reporting Period
    - Key Exclusions
    - Notable Endorsements

    Highlight material differences between options in bold.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Recommended Program">
    ```
    Based on the quotes and coverage analysis in the source
    documents, present the recommended insurance program.
    Include:

    1. The recommended carrier and the rationale for selection.
    2. A summary of the key coverage terms: limits, deductibles,
       and premium.
    3. Any coverage enhancements that were negotiated or are
       available.
    4. Any residual coverage gaps the insured should be aware of.

    Write this as a professional narrative suitable for a
    client-facing proposal. Do not use bullet points — use
    complete paragraphs.
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Binding Instructions">
    ```
    Show the following text verbatim:

    [[
    To bind this coverage, please confirm the following:
    1. Review and approve the recommended program outlined
       in this proposal.
    2. Sign and return the attached applications and
       warranties.
    3. Provide any outstanding documentation requested by
       the carrier.
    4. Confirm the effective date and any special billing
       arrangements.

    Please contact your account team with any questions or
    to request modifications before binding.
    ]]
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

  These prompts are starting points. The most effective templates are refined over time based on your firm's proposal standards, client expectations, and the feedback you receive from generated output. Save your best versions to the [Prompt Library](/platform/prompt-library) for reuse.

## Best practices

- **Invest time in your templates.** A well-crafted template with detailed section prompts will produce better proposals every time it's used. Front-load the work once, then reuse it across accounts.
- **Be explicit in section prompts.** Specify exact column names for tables, the order of information, and whether content should be narrative or tabular. The more precise your instructions, the less editing you'll need after generation.
- **Use the Prompt Library.** Copy checklists from the Prompt Library directly into template sections — especially for coverage comparison tables where you've already defined what to extract.
- **Lock templates for team consistency.** When sharing templates with your organization, consider locking the sections to ensure everyone produces proposals with the same structure and quality standards.
- **Review every generated proposal.** AI-generated content should always be reviewed for accuracy. Check that figures, carrier names, and coverage details match the source documents before sending to a client.
- **Use landscape for comparison tables.** Switch sections with wide tables to landscape orientation so columns remain readable — especially when comparing more than two quote options.
- **Save before exporting.** Always click Save before Export PDF to ensure your latest edits are captured in the download.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My proposal is taking longer than 10 minutes to generate">
    Complex proposals with many documents may take slightly longer. If generation exceeds 15 minutes, refresh the page and check the Studio history tab — your proposal may have completed while the page was loading.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The coverage comparison table has too many narrow columns">
    Coverage comparison tables support up to 5 quote options in landscape orientation. If you've uploaded more than 5 documents and the table is too wide, consider splitting the comparison across two proposals or removing less relevant quote options.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I can't edit certain text in the proposal">
    Text enclosed in `[[` and `]]` in the template is locked and cannot be edited after generation. If you need to change locked content, edit the template itself and regenerate the proposal.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I don't see any templates to choose from">
    If no templates appear, you'll need to create one. Click **+ Create New Template** and follow the four-step wizard. You can also ask your team lead or account Owner to share an Organization Template with your team.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I can't create a new theme">
    Only account **Owners** can create themes. Contact your account Owner or reach out to your Qumis Customer Success Manager for assistance with branding setup.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where are my previously generated proposals?">
    All saved proposals appear on the **Studio** page. Click **Studio** in the left sidebar and look under the **Documents** section. You can filter by "Proposal" to see only proposal documents.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I reuse a template with different documents?">
    Yes. Templates are fully reusable. Each time you start a new Proposal Generator workflow, you select a template and upload fresh documents. The template sections and prompts remain the same, but Qumis generates new content from the new documents.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I update an existing template?">
    Navigate to the template selection screen, click the three-dot menu on the template card, and select **Edit**. You can modify the name, description, sections, prompts, theme, and permissions at any time. Changes apply to future proposals, not previously generated ones.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

The **Proposal Generator** is the final step in turning your analysis into a client deliverable. Building insurance proposals traditionally means hours of copying data between spreadsheets, formatting tables, and assembling documents. The Proposal Generator automates the heavy lifting — reading your source documents, populating sections with extracted data, and formatting everything according to your branded template. What once took an afternoon now takes minutes, freeing you to focus on strategy, client relationships, and winning business.
