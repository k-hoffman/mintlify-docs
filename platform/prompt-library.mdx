## What Is the Prompt Library?

The Prompt Library is a collection of saved prompts—questions, instructions, and analysis templates that you and your team have created and tested. Instead of writing the same question repeatedly, you save it once and apply it to any document in your Vault.

Think of prompts as templates for analysis. A well-written prompt captures institutional knowledge and best practices, making it easy for anyone on your team to conduct consistent, thorough analysis without starting from scratch.

## Why Save Prompts?

Saved prompts deliver three key benefits:

- **Consistency**: Everyone on your team asks the same question the same way, producing consistent results and reducing variation in analysis
- **Institutional knowledge**: Your team's best practices and most effective questions are captured in reusable form. New team members can use them on day one without reinventing the wheel
- **Speed**: Instead of writing out detailed questions, you select a prompt from your library and apply it. Analysis that once took 30 minutes now takes 30 seconds

Over time, your Prompt Library becomes a repository of your team's expertise and analytical approaches.

## Creating and Saving a Prompt

<Steps>
  <Step title="Write Your Prompt">
    In the Chats interface, write a detailed question or instruction. Include context, specificity, and any requirements for the answer. For example: "List all exclusions under the Commercial General Liability coverage section. For each exclusion, explain how it might apply to a manufacturing client's operations."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test Your Prompt">
    Apply your prompt to one or more documents and review the results. Does Qumis return the information you expected? Is the answer complete and accurate? Refine your prompt wording if needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save to Library">
    Once you're satisfied with the results, save the prompt to your Prompt Library. Give it a clear name and add a description of what it does and when to use it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Categorize and Tag">
    Assign your prompt to a category (analysis, comparison, extraction, research) and add tags so your team can find it easily.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Using Saved Prompts

To use a prompt from your library:

1. Open Chats and select the document(s) you want to analyze
2. Click "Select from Prompt Library" instead of typing a new question
3. Browse or search for the prompt you want
4. Review the prompt and customize it if needed (add specific dates, client names, or coverage lines)
5. Run the prompt on your selected documents

Using a saved prompt ensures you're applying the same analytical approach every time, while still allowing customization for specific situations.

## Sharing Prompts Across Your Team

The Prompt Library supports two types of prompts:

- **Personal prompts**: Saved to your account only. Use these for specialized questions unique to your role
- **Team prompts**: Shared across your entire organization. These become your standardized analytical approaches

When you create a prompt you want to share, mark it as shareable. Organization admins can review and publish team prompts so everyone has access to them. This ensures your entire team benefits from your best practices.

## Prompt Categories

Organize your prompts by how you use them:

- **Analysis prompts**: Extract information, identify gaps, summarize coverage. Example: "What are all the conditions and limitations that apply to this coverage?"
- **Comparison prompts**: Compare terms, limits, and conditions across policies. Example: "How do the deductibles and limits differ between these two Excess Liability policies?"
- **Extraction prompts**: Pull specific data for reports or spreadsheets. Example: "Extract the policy number, limits, deductibles, and premium for each policy and format as a table."
- **Research prompts**: Guide legal research or investigation. Example: "What case law or coverage opinions exist regarding the interpretation of this exclusion?"

## Writing Effective Reusable Prompts

The best prompts are specific and detailed, but flexible enough to apply to different documents and situations:

- **Be specific**: Name the sections you want analyzed ("Under the Property Coverage section...") rather than relying on Qumis to guess
- **Include examples**: If you want a certain format or type of answer, provide an example
- **Explain your goal**: Tell Qumis what you'll do with the answer ("For renewal discussions" or "To identify coverage gaps")
- **Use variables**: If you need to adapt a prompt for different situations, use brackets for custom information, like "[CLIENT NAME]" or "[COVERAGE LINE]"

<Tip>
  Start by saving the questions you find yourself asking repeatedly. These become your team's institutional knowledge. A question you ask three times a month is worth saving. Once saved, you can apply it to any policy in seconds instead of typing it out each time. New team members can use it on day one.
</Tip>

## Example Prompts

Here are common prompts that insurance professionals find valuable:

**For Brokers & Producers:** "Summarize the key coverage terms, limits, deductibles, and exclusions for a client who is unfamiliar with this policy."

**For Account Managers:** "What are the major changes between the 2025 and 2026 policies? How do these changes affect our renewal strategy?"

**For Claims Professionals:** "Based on this policy language, is [SPECIFIC CLAIM SCENARIO] covered? Identify all relevant inclusions, exclusions, and conditions."

**For Risk Managers:** "Does this coverage meet the contractual insurance requirements specified in our vendor contract? If not, what gaps exist?"

## Managing Your Library

Over time, your Prompt Library will grow. Keep it organized:

- Review and update prompts periodically as your processes evolve
- Delete prompts that are no longer useful
- Rename prompts for clarity if team feedback suggests they're confusing
- Document when and why you use each prompt so new team members understand the context

A well-maintained library is more valuable than a large library. Focus on prompts your team actually uses.

## Tips for Your Role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    Build a library of prompts for competitive analysis: "Compare the coverage terms, limits, and exclusions across these quotes and highlight where each carrier offers a competitive advantage." Save prompts for client-ready summaries that you can apply to any policy before a presentation.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    Create prompts for renewal analysis: "Identify all changes between the expiring and renewal policies, categorized as coverage enhancements, coverage reductions, and pricing changes." Save prompts for coverage gap identification that you can run at every renewal.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    Build prompts for coverage determination: "Analyze whether [CLAIM SCENARIO] is covered under this policy. Identify the relevant insuring agreement, applicable exclusions, and any conditions that affect coverage." Save jurisdiction-specific prompts for common claim types you encounter.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    Create prompts for compliance verification: "Does this insurance program meet the requirements specified in the attached contract? Identify any gaps in required coverage, limits, or endorsements." Save prompts for periodic program reviews and audit preparation.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
