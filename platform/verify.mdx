Verify automates the critical work of checking whether insurance meets both external requirements and internal expectations. Upload contracts and policies, and Qumis systematically checks every requirement against actual coverage — flagging gaps, confirming compliance, and producing a documented audit trail. Verify handles two complementary workflows: contract compliance checks that validate requirements imposed by leases, vendor agreements, and loan covenants, and coverage verification that confirms policies actually cover what clients expect them to cover.

---

## When to verify

Contract compliance verification applies when you need to confirm an insurance program satisfies external requirements. Common scenarios include commercial leases (landlords require tenants to carry specific coverages), vendor and subcontractor agreements (ensure their certificates meet your contractual requirements before onboarding), loan covenants (lenders specify minimum insurance requirements), and construction contracts (complex, detailed insurance requirements across multiple lines). Coverage verification addresses a different question: whether policies actually deliver the coverage clients expect. Verify coverage when you need to confirm client expectations against reality, conduct coverage adequacy reviews to address actual risk exposures, validate program design after placing new coverage, or check coverage before submitting a post-loss claim.

---

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define your verification objective">
    Choose between contract compliance (checking requirements from an external document like a lease or loan agreement) or coverage verification (validating that policies address specific coverage questions or exposure scenarios).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload the source documents">
    For contract compliance, upload the contract that specifies insurance requirements and the relevant policies or certificates. For coverage verification, upload the policies and endorsements that make up the coverage program.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the verification">
    Qumis analyzes each requirement or question. For contract compliance, it checks whether actual coverage meets stated requirements and marks results as met, not met, or partially met. For coverage verification, it identifies the relevant coverage grant, applicable exclusions, conditions, and provides a clear finding with citations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the documented findings">
    The output is a structured report showing each requirement or question, the verification result, and specific policy provisions that support or contradict the finding. You can verify the analysis and share results with clients or colleagues.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Qumis checks

**Contract compliance verification** validates that insurance programs satisfy external contractual requirements. Qumis checks minimum limits against per-occurrence and aggregate requirements, confirms required coverage types are included (CGL, auto, umbrella, workers' comp, professional, cyber), verifies additional insured status and naming, validates waiver of subrogation endorsements, checks primary and non-contributory provisions, confirms cancellation notice requirements, and ensures deductibles and self-insured retentions stay within contractual limits.

**Coverage verification** confirms policies actually cover what clients expect. Qumis runs systematic checks against standard verification checklists by coverage line: Commercial General Liability (products-completed operations, additional insured grants, contractual liability, exclusion scope), Professional Liability (prior acts, defense cost treatment, discovery periods), Cyber Liability (first-party vs. third-party coverage, ransomware, business interruption, social engineering fraud), Property (covered perils, business income, flood/earthquake sublimits), and Directors & Officers (side A/B/C allocation, insured vs. insured exclusion, prior acts). Each finding includes specific policy citations so you can verify the analysis.

---

## What you get

**Contract Compliance Report** shows each contractual requirement, verification result (met/not met/partially met), and the specific policy provisions that satisfy or fail to satisfy the requirement. This creates a documented audit trail demonstrating your compliance diligence.

**Coverage Verification Report** answers specific coverage questions with findings that identify the relevant coverage grant, applicable exclusions, conditions, and clear determination with policy citations. Reports work by individual question or by checklist, organized by coverage line.

Both outputs are structured for easy sharing with clients, colleagues, and internal compliance teams. Qumis handles batch verification across multiple contracts and certificates, producing compliance summaries across your entire portfolio.

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    **New business:** Use contract compliance verification to show prospects exactly where their current program falls short of contractual requirements — and how your proposed program closes gaps.

    **Pre-placement validation:** After designing a coverage program, run coverage verification to confirm proposed policies actually deliver intended coverage. Catch gaps before binding.

    **Client education:** Use verification results to walk clients through exactly what their policies cover, where gaps exist, and how you've addressed their contractual obligations. This builds trust and demonstrates value.

    **Renewal diligence:** At renewal, verify every client contract against the new policy to confirm continued compliance and coverage adequacy.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    **Proactive compliance monitoring:** Run contract compliance verification at policy inception and every renewal to catch gaps before they become problems. Flag any compliance issues before they impact the client.

    **Client advisory:** When a client signs a new contract, verify insurance requirements immediately and flag gaps that need to be addressed before the contract takes effect.

    **Annual coverage reviews:** Use the coverage verification checklist to systematically review each policy in a client's program and document findings for coverage review letters.

    **Gap identification:** Run targeted coverage verifications when clients face new risks — entering new markets, signing new contracts, launching new products. Identify whether existing coverage extends to new exposures.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    **Pre-claim verification:** When an insured reports a potential claim, run coverage verification to confirm the policy responds to these specific facts. Set realistic expectations and identify coverage issues early.

    **Additional insured validation:** When a claim involves additional insured status, use contract compliance verification to confirm whether proper endorsements are in place.

    **Multi-policy analysis:** For claims potentially triggering multiple policies, verify coverage across the entire program to identify the most favorable path and ensure complete response.

    **Contractual liability review:** Use verification to identify whether the policy's contractual liability provisions align with the contract's indemnification requirements.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    **Enterprise compliance:** Run systematic contract compliance checks across every contract in your portfolio. This is the primary verification workflow for risk managers and the recommended pilot workflow in implementation frameworks.

    **Vendor insurance management:** Verify every vendor's insurance certificates against your standard contractual requirements. Flag non-compliant vendors for follow-up before they begin work.

    **Exposure mapping:** Map your organization's top risk exposures against your insurance program. Verify that each significant exposure has corresponding coverage and adequate limits.

    **Program benchmarking:** Verify your coverage against industry standards and best practices for your sector. Identify areas where you may be underinsured relative to peers.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
