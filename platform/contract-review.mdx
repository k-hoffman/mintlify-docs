Contract Review automates the time-consuming process of checking contracts against insurance requirements. Upload a vendor agreement, lease, or service contract, and Qumis systematically identifies every insurance-related provision — additional insured requirements, waiver of subrogation clauses, minimum limits, indemnification language — then checks whether your current coverage meets those obligations. The result is a structured compliance report you can share with clients, legal teams, or underwriters.

---

## What Contract Review does

The Contract Review Report uses AI-powered analysis to examine contracts against your insurance requirements and risk management standards. Qumis analyzes vendor agreements, service contracts, leases, and other documents to identify insurance-related provisions, compliance gaps, and risk transfer issues.

The report examines:
- Insurance requirements embedded in the contract
- Additional insured endorsement requirements
- Waiver of subrogation provisions
- Indemnification language and scope
- Minimum coverage limits and deductibles
- Certificate of insurance requirements
- Compliance status against your current insurance program

## When to Use Contract Review

Use the Contract Review Report in these scenarios:

- **New Contract Review**: Assess a newly drafted or received contract before execution
- **Renewal with Insurance Provisions**: Review updated contracts during renewal cycles that contain insurance-related language
- **Vendor Onboarding**: Evaluate insurance requirements as part of new vendor assessment processes
- **Lease Review**: Analyze commercial lease agreements for insurance and indemnification requirements

## How to Generate a Contract Review Report

<Steps>
<Step title="Upload the Contract">
Upload the contract document (PDF, Word, or image) you want to review. Ensure the document is readable and contains the relevant contract language.
</Step>

<Step title="Optional: Upload Insurance Program Details">
If available, upload your current insurance certificate or program document. This helps Qumis compare the contract requirements against your actual coverage.

<Step title="Configure Review Scope">
Specify which aspects of the contract to prioritize:
- Insurance and indemnity requirements
- Additional insured needs
- Waiver of subrogation provisions

- Certificate requirements
</Step>

<Step title="Generate the Report">
Click Generate. Qumis analyzes the contract and creates a structured review within seconds.
</Step>

<Step title="Review Findings">
Examine the results, which include compliant items, identified gaps, and recommended actions.
</Step>
</Steps>

## What the Report Covers

### Insurance Requirements Identified
A list of all insurance-related provisions found in the contract, including coverage types, limits, and conditions.

### Additional Insured Requirements
Specific endorsements or additional insured designations the contract requires from your insurance carriers.

### Waiver of Subrogation
Analysis of waiver of subrogation language, scope, and any conditions or limitations.

### Indemnification Provisions
Detailed review of indemnity clauses, including who indemnifies whom, scope of indemnification, and applicability to bodily injury or property damage.

### Minimum Limits Requirements
A summary of minimum coverage limits specified in the contract by coverage type.

### Certificate Requirements
Documentation and certificate of insurance requirements, including delivery timelines and update frequency.

### Compliance Status
Assessment of whether your current insurance program meets the contract's requirements.

## Understanding Your Results

The report presents findings across three categories:

**Compliant Items**: Contract provisions that align with your current insurance program.

**Gaps Requiring Attention**: Insurance requirements in the contract that your current program does not meet, along with specific recommendations.

**Recommendations**: Suggested actions, such as obtaining additional endorsements, increasing coverage limits, or amending contract language.

## Exporting and Sharing

Export the Contract Review Report as a PDF for sharing with:
- Internal stakeholders for approval
- Insurance brokers for quotation requests
- Legal teams for contract negotiation
- Clients or vendors as part of contract approval workflows
