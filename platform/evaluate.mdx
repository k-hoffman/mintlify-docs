Evaluate is Qumis's core capability for making defensible coverage determinations. Upload a claim and policy, and get a structured analysis that maps claim facts to policy provisions, identifies applicable coverages, flags exclusions and conditions, and produces formatted reports backed by cited policy language.

---

## When to evaluate

Use Evaluate when you need to determine coverage for a specific claim or loss scenario. This applies to new claims that require coverage position decisions, disputed coverage questions where ambiguity exists, and scenarios where you need to document your reasoning for audit, litigation, or counsel engagement. Evaluate is essential when building defensible determinations—positions that can withstand scrutiny from coverage counsel, regulators, or courts.

---

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select policies and describe the claim">
    Upload the relevant policy documents (base policy, endorsements, related coverage documents) and provide claim facts: What happened? When? Where? Who was involved? What is the nature of the loss? Be specific—Qumis uses these details to evaluate coverage comprehensively.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure analysis scope">
    Choose whether to analyze the full policy or focus on specific coverage parts, endorsements, or priority areas. Indicate the depth level you need (summary, standard, or comprehensive).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate analysis">
    Qumis analyzes policy language against claim facts and produces a structured coverage position with citations, supporting reasoning, and confidence levels. The analysis covers insuring agreements, exclusions, conditions precedent, definitions, endorsements, limits, and deductibles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and refine">
    Review the analysis and ask follow-up questions. Request deeper analysis of specific provisions, generate formatted reports for sharing with counsel or stakeholders, and update the analysis as claim facts evolve.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## What Qumis analyzes

Qumis evaluates all key elements of your insurance contracts. It extracts and compares insuring agreements (the primary coverage grants), exclusions (specific carve-outs that eliminate coverage), conditions (requirements like notice, cooperation, proof of loss), definitions (policy-specific terms), endorsements (modifications and enhancements), and limits with deductibles. The result is a complete picture of how your policy responds to the specific claim scenario.

---

## What you get

### Claims Analysis Report
A professional formatted document suitable for filing, sharing with counsel, or client presentation. The report includes an executive summary of the coverage position and key findings, coverage-by-coverage analysis with section-by-section evaluation, exclusion and conditions analysis, coverage position statement with confidence level, cited policy language with page references, and recommendations for next steps.

### Coverage Table
A structured matrix showing your complete insurance program at a glance: coverage lines, carriers, policy numbers, limits, deductibles, retentions, territory, key endorsements, and expiration dates. Coverage tables make it easy to identify gaps, verify adequacy against contractual requirements, and support renewal planning and claims coordination.

### Building Defensible Determinations
For coverage positions that require rigorous documentation, Qumis helps you build evidence-based determinations. Start with policy language analysis, research the legal landscape with case law citations, and document a reasoned determination that anticipates counterarguments. The three-step framework—analyze the policy, research legal authority, document the determination—creates a defensible record that can withstand scrutiny.
