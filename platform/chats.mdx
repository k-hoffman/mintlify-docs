## What Are Chats?

Chats is a conversational AI interface that lets you ask natural language questions about your uploaded insurance documents. Instead of manually reviewing pages of policy language, you can have a real-time conversation with Qumis about your coverage, exclusions, conditions, and more. Every answer is backed by specific citations to your documents, so you can trust the sources behind each response.

Chats work with any document in your Document Vault—policies, endorsements, declarations, certificates, and contracts. Ask follow-up questions, refine your searches, and build a complete understanding of your coverage in minutes instead of hours.

## Start Your First Chat

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload or Select Documents">
    Upload your insurance documents to the Vault, or select documents you've already uploaded. You can chat with a single policy or ask questions across multiple documents simultaneously.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Type Your Question">
    Ask your question in natural language. Be specific about what you want to know: coverage details, exclusions, conditions, trigger language, or how terms interact across different sections.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Your Answer">
    Qumis returns a cited answer with direct references to the source language in your documents. Each citation shows exactly where the information appears, so you can verify the response instantly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask Follow-Ups">
    Continue the conversation. Ask for clarification, explore related topics, or dig deeper into specific sections. Your chat history maintains context across multiple questions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Asking Effective Questions

The quality of your answer depends on how you frame your question. Here are examples of strong questions:

- "What are the exclusions listed under the Cyber Liability section?"
- "Does this policy cover business interruption from a network outage?"
- "What conditions must be met before the Excess Liability coverage triggers?"
- "How do the definitions in the Commercial General Liability section compare to the umbrella policy?"

<Tip>
The more specific your question, the more precise the answer. Instead of "What does this policy cover?" try "What are the exclusions under the CGL coverage section?" Qumis performs best when you reference specific sections, coverage types, or conditions you're interested in.
</Tip>

## Understanding Your Responses

Each answer Qumis provides includes:

- **Direct citations**: Exact language from your documents, showing where the answer comes from
- **Source references**: Which document(s) the information appears in, plus page numbers when available
- **Confidence indicators**: Qumis signals when it's confident in the answer versus when additional verification may be helpful
- **Follow-up suggestions**: Related questions you might want to ask to build a fuller picture

If a response includes a low confidence indicator or seems incomplete, refine your question or ask a follow-up. You can also manually check the cited sections in your Document Vault.

## Managing Chat History

Qumis saves your chats automatically. To find a past conversation:

- Browse your recent chats in the interface
- Use the search function to find chats by document name or topic
- Return to any previous chat thread and continue asking questions from where you left off
- Start a new chat when you want to focus on a different document or topic

## Multi-Document Chats

One of Qumis's most powerful features is the ability to ask questions across multiple documents at once. For example:

- "Does the Excess Liability policy respond where the Primary GL is silent?"
- "How does the Umbrella coverage language address gaps in the underlying limits?"
- "Which endorsements modify the CGL exclusions?"

When chatting with multiple documents, Qumis identifies relevant information from each document and synthesizes a complete answer. This is especially valuable when analyzing complex coverage programs where multiple policies interact.

<Info>
Your chats are saved within your Qumis account. You can revisit any conversation, export chat transcripts, and use them as documentation for coverage decisions and client communications.
</Info>

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    Ask about renewal changes and new endorsements to identify coverage gaps for your client advisory. Use Chats to prepare renewal strategies and stewardship meeting agendas by researching coverage adequacy before the meeting.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    Interrogate policy language for coverage positions on specific claims. Use Chats to quickly locate relevant exclusions, conditions, and trigger language, and ask follow-up questions about how different sections interact.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    Verify that contractual insurance requirements are met by your current coverage. Use Chats to check coverage adequacy against your exposure profiles and understand the impact of endorsements on your total program.
  </Tab>
