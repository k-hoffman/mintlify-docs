Qumis organizes its platform around eight capabilities. Each one maps to a specific part of your insurance workflow. Start with what you're trying to accomplish, and we'll point you to the right tool.

---

## Choose your task

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Ask a question about a document" icon="message-question" href="/platform/chats">
    **Chats** — Get answers from your documents

    Upload a policy, quote, endorsement, or claim file and ask plain-English questions. Get structured answers with citations pointing to the exact clauses Qumis referenced. Best for: quick coverage checks, pre-meeting prep, targeted policy questions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Compare two or more documents" icon="code-compare" href="/platform/compare">
    **Compare Documents** — Understand what changed, and what it means

    Side-by-side analysis of quotes, renewal vs. expiring policies, or any two documents. Qumis highlights every material difference in coverage, limits, exclusions, and terms. Best for: competitive quoting, renewal analysis, coverage benchmarking.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Analyze a single policy" icon="list-tree" href="/platform/summarize">
    **Policy Analysis** — See what's covered, and what's not

    Comprehensive analysis of one policy — coverage grants, exclusions, sublimits, conditions, and key provisions. Best for: prospect policy review, coverage gap identification, onboarding to a new account.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Claim Analysis" icon="scale-balanced" href="/platform/evaluate">
    **Claim Analysis** — Make defensible coverage calls fast

    Assess whether a policy responds to a specific claim or scenario with structured legal reasoning, cited policy language, and a supported determination. Best for: coverage determination, claims analysis, scenario planning.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Build coverage tables" icon="table" href="/platform/populate">
    **Coverage Table** — Turn policies into structured data

    Pull specific fields from documents into spreadsheets or fill your client templates automatically. Best for: renewal summaries, schedule of insurance updates, data extraction for reporting.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Review contracts" icon="file-contract" href="/platform/contract-review">
    **Contract Review** — Check compliance in seconds

    Review contracts against insurance requirements, identifying gaps in additional insured provisions, waiver of subrogation, minimum limits, and indemnification language. Best for: contract compliance, vendor onboarding, lease review.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Store and organize documents" icon="lock" href="/platform/document-vault">
    **Document Vault** — Upload once, reuse forever

    Your secure digital filing cabinet. Upload documents once and access them from any Qumis tool. Private or shared with your team. Best for: organizing client files, team collaboration, streamlining workflows.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Save reusable questions" icon="sparkles" href="/platform/prompt-library">
    **Prompt Library** — Save your smartest questions

    Build a library of your best questions and analysis templates. Reuse them across clients and documents to maintain consistency. Best for: standardizing analysis, building team playbooks, capturing institutional knowledge.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Not sure where to start?

Here's a quick decision guide:

| If you want to... | Use |
|---|---|
| Ask a specific question about a policy | **[Chats](/platform/chats)** |
| See what changed between two documents | **[Compare Documents](/platform/compare)** |
| Get an overview of a policy you haven't read | **[Policy Analysis](/platform/summarize)** |
| Determine if a policy covers a specific claim | **[Claim Analysis](/platform/evaluate)** |
| Pull data into a spreadsheet or template | **[Coverage Table](/platform/populate)** |
| Check if coverage meets requirements | **[Contract Review](/platform/contract-review)** |
| Store documents for reuse across tools | **[Document Vault](/platform/document-vault)** |
| Save and reuse your best questions | **[Prompt Library](/platform/prompt-library)** |

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    Start with **Compare Documents** to analyze competing quotes, then use **Chats** for pre-meeting prep. **Policy Analysis** is great for quickly reviewing a prospect's incumbent policy before your first meeting. Use **Coverage Table** to extract data into your submission spreadsheets.

    → [Broker & Producer Quick Start](/get-started/quick-start/brokers-and-producers)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    Start with **Compare Documents** for renewal analysis (expiring vs. renewal), then use **Chats** for proactive mid-term coverage checks. **Coverage Table** helps you fill renewal summaries, and **Contract Review** streamlines contract compliance reviews.

    → [Account Manager Quick Start](/get-started/quick-start/account-managers)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    Start with **Claim Analysis** for coverage determinations, then use **Chats** for quick coverage triage. **Compare Documents** is essential when a claim spans multiple policy periods. **Contract Review** supports formal coverage verification.

    → [Claims Professional Quick Start](/get-started/quick-start/claims-professionals)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    Start with **Contract Review** to check contract compliance across your program, then use **Compare Documents** to benchmark coverage. **Claim Analysis** is useful for scenario planning against hypothetical losses, and **Coverage Table** extracts program data for board reporting.

    → [Risk Manager Quick Start](/get-started/quick-start/risk-managers)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
