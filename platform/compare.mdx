# Compare Documents

Compare is one of Qumis's most powerful capabilities. Whether you're evaluating competitive quotes, reviewing renewals, or verifying contract compliance, Qumis performs structured coverage analysis that identifies exactly what changed and what it means for coverage. This page explains when to compare, what Qumis analyzes, and how to get the most from your comparisons.

---

## When to compare

Document comparison is valuable whenever you need to understand differences between two insurance-related documents. The most common scenarios:

**Quote vs. quote.** A client has received competing quotes. You need to identify which provides better coverage for their specific risks, where the meaningful differences lie, and which tradeoffs each quote involves.

**Renewal vs. expiring.** A renewal has arrived. You need to identify every change from the current policy — additions, removals, modifications to limits, deductibles, exclusions, and conditions.

**Policy vs. contract requirements.** A client's contract specifies insurance requirements. You need to verify that the actual policy meets every requirement and flag any gaps.

**Current vs. proposed endorsement.** A carrier has offered an endorsement modification. You need to understand exactly what changes and whether it broadens or restricts coverage.

**Program consistency.** You need to compare policies across locations, subsidiaries, or carriers to identify inconsistencies and ensure appropriate coverage.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload both documents">
    Upload the two documents you want to compare. Qumis works best with text-based PDFs. Label them clearly so the output references the right document.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Specify your focus (optional)">
    Tell Qumis what matters most. "Compare these two CGL quotes with a focus on products-completed operations coverage, additional insured provisions, and professional liability coverage" produces more targeted analysis than "Compare these quotes."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the structured output">
    In Chat, Qumis produces a comparison organized by category — limits, coverage grants, exclusions, conditions, etc. Each finding includes citations to both documents so you can verify. For formal documentation, generate a Comparison Report with executive summary, change summary table, detailed side-by-side comparison, and gap analysis.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask follow-up questions">
    Drill into the findings that matter: "Explain the practical impact of the different cyber exclusion language in Quote A versus Quote B for a technology company client."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate a formal report (if needed)">
    When you need a polished document to share with clients or colleagues, export a formatted Comparison Report. Configure the scope (full document, specific sections, financial terms only), sensitivity level (material changes only or all differences), and output format (PDF, Word, HTML, Excel).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export and share">
    Share your report with clients, carriers, counsel, or include in your file. Adjust change sensitivity if you need more or less detail.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Qumis analyzes

Qumis performs a structured coverage analysis that identifies:

**Limits and deductibles.** Changes in per-occurrence limits, aggregate limits, sublimits, and deductible structures.

**Coverage grants.** New coverages added, existing coverages removed, or modifications to the scope of insuring agreements.

**Exclusions.** New exclusions, removed exclusions, or modifications to exclusion language — including changes to exceptions within exclusions.

**Conditions.** Changes to notice requirements, cooperation clauses, other insurance provisions, and subrogation.

**Definitions.** Changes to defined terms that affect the scope of coverage.

**Endorsements.** Added or removed endorsements and modifications to existing endorsement forms.

For each difference, Qumis explains the practical impact — not just what changed, but what it means for coverage.

---

## What you get

You can compare documents using Chat (quick, conversational) or generate a formal Comparison Report. Both use the same underlying analysis engine.

**In Chat** — Conversational, iterative analysis where you can ask follow-up questions and drill into specific findings.

**Comparison Report** — Structured, formatted output that includes an executive summary highlighting key differences and material changes; a one-page change summary table showing what changed across documents; side-by-side policy language for each section with change indicators (Added, Removed, Modified, Unchanged); coverage gap analysis showing provisions that exist in one policy but not the other; premium and pricing comparison if applicable; and recommendations based on differences identified. The report is formatted for professional presentation and exportable in multiple formats.

## Tips for your role

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brokers & Producers">
    **Competitive quoting workflow:** Upload competing quotes and ask Qumis to compare them with a focus on the coverages that matter most to your client. Generate a polished comparison report you can include in your recommendation to demonstrate market knowledge and justify your selection.

    **Monday Quote Comparison:** Make this your weekly habit — every Monday, run a comparison on the quotes that came in over the past week. This is the recommended pilot workflow for the Ascend™ implementation framework.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    **Renewal analysis:** Compare the expiring policy against the renewal quote to identify every change. Flag items that need discussion with the client — especially reduced limits, new exclusions, or increased deductibles. Generate a renewal comparison report to show clients what's changing year-over-year as a stewardship touchpoint.

    **Contract compliance:** Compare the client's policy against their lease or vendor agreement to verify that insurance requirements are met.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Claims Professionals">
    **Prior vs. current policy:** When a claim spans multiple policy periods, compare the prior and current policies to determine which provisions apply and where coverage may differ.

    **Coverage opinion support:** Compare the policy language against the claim facts and generate a historical policy comparison report for your file.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    **Contract compliance verification:** Compare your insurance program against contractual requirements to identify gaps. This is the recommended pilot workflow for risk managers in the Ascend™ framework.

    **Program consistency review:** Compare policies across locations or subsidiaries to identify coverage inconsistencies and verify compliance with your program requirements.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
