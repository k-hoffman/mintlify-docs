## What Is the Document Vault?

The Document Vault is your centralized storage for all insurance documents. Upload your policies, endorsements, declarations, and contracts once, and access them across every Qumis capability—Chats, Reports, Compare, and Legal Search. Your documents become the foundation for all analysis and research within Qumis.

Instead of managing documents across multiple systems or email folders, keep everything in one secure, searchable location. Once documents are in your Vault, they're immediately ready for analysis without additional uploading or processing.

## Supported Document Types

The Vault accepts any insurance document you need to analyze:

- **Policies**: Commercial General Liability, Property, Cyber, Professional Liability, Umbrella, and all other coverage forms
- **Endorsements**: Modifications, additions, and exclusions to your base policies
- **Declarations pages**: Summary of coverage, limits, and premium information
- **Schedules**: Detailed lists of insured locations, equipment, or values
- **Certificates of insurance**: Proof of coverage for clients or third parties
- **Contracts**: Insurance-related agreements, SLAs, and vendor contracts
- **Binders**: Temporary coverage documentation before formal policy issuance
- **Amendments**: Any other policy-related documents you need to reference

PDF is the primary supported format. Document quality should be clear and legible for Qumis to process accurately.

## Uploading Documents

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Vault">
    Open the Document Vault from the main Qumis interface.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Your Upload Method">
    Choose between drag-and-drop (single or multiple files) or the upload button to select files from your device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for Processing">
    Qumis automatically processes documents as they upload. Processing includes parsing the document structure, indexing content, and preparing the document for analysis. You'll see a progress indicator.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Upload Complete">
    Once processing finishes, your document appears in your Vault and is ready for use with Chats, Reports, Compare, and Legal Search.
  </Step>
</Steps>

For teams uploading many documents at once, use bulk upload to import multiple files in a single action. This is useful when migrating documents from another system or organizing an entire coverage program into Qumis.

## Organizing Your Documents

Keep your Vault organized so you can find documents quickly:

- **Folders**: Create folders for different clients, coverage lines, claim files, or whatever structure makes sense for your workflow
- **Tags**: Add tags like "CGL," "Renewal 2026," "Claims," or "Umbrella" to make documents discoverable
- **Search**: Use the search bar to find documents by name, tag, client name, or coverage type
- **Favorites**: Mark documents you reference frequently as favorites for quick access

A well-organized Vault saves time when you're answering questions, preparing analyses, or comparing coverage across multiple policies.

## How Documents Connect to Other Features

Once a document is in your Vault, it's available to every Qumis capability:

- **Chats**: Ask questions about your documents and get cited answers in real time
- **Reports**: Generate analysis reports that pull insights and data from your Vault documents
- **Compare**: Compare terms, limits, and conditions across multiple policies at once
- **Legal Search**: Reference Vault documents alongside legal research to build complete coverage positions

This integration means you never upload the same document twice. The Vault is the single source of truth for all your analysis.

## Document Processing

When you upload a document, Qumis immediately begins processing:

1. **Parsing**: The document structure is analyzed to identify sections, pages, and content hierarchy
2. **Indexing**: Document content is indexed for fast searching and retrieval across all Qumis features
3. **Preparation**: The document is formatted and prepared for use with AI-powered analysis tools
4. **Ready status**: Once processing completes, the document is fully available for Chats, Reports, Compare, and Legal Search

Most documents process within seconds to minutes, depending on size and complexity. Large or complex policies may take longer. You can begin working with a document as soon as the status shows "Ready."

<Info>
Documents uploaded to the Vault are encrypted at rest and in transit. Your documents are never used to train AI models. Each organization's data is completely isolated and never shared or accessed by other users. For more information, see our data privacy and security documentation.
</Info>

## Storage and Retention

Your Vault has ample storage for your entire insurance document library. Documents remain in your Vault indefinitely unless you delete them. You can archive older documents if you want to keep them but reduce clutter in your active Vault.

There are no limits on the number of documents you can store, so keep your complete history within Qumis. This becomes valuable context for multi-year trend analysis, renewal comparisons, and claims history research.

## Best Practices

- **Consistent naming**: Use clear file names that identify the client, coverage line, and policy year (e.g., "Acme Corp - CGL - 2026")
- **Complete programs**: Upload your entire coverage program so you can analyze how policies interact
- **Regular updates**: Upload renewals and endorsements as they arrive so your Vault stays current
- **Organization**: Use folders and tags consistently across your organization so any team member can find documents quickly

  <Tab title="Risk Managers">
    Upload your entire insurance program — every policy, endorsement, and certificate — into the Vault organized by coverage line.
