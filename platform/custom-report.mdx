## What Custom Reports Are

The Custom Report builder allows you to create tailored analysis and documentation without being limited to predefined report templates. Define your own analysis scope, sections, and output format to match your specific needs. Whether you need specialized analysis, unique client deliverables, or ad hoc research compilation, custom reports adapt to your exact requirements.

Custom reports leverage Qumis' AI analysis capabilities with your own prompts and instructions, enabling you to extract and structure information exactly as you need it.

## When to Use Custom Reports

Use custom reports when:

- **Standard Report Types Don't Fit**: Your analysis needs fall outside the scope of predefined reports
- **Specialized Analysis Requirements**: You need domain-specific analysis that standard reports don't provide
- **Unique Client Deliverables**: You want to create branded or customized output formats for client delivery
- **Ad Hoc Research Compilation**: You need to gather and structure information from multiple documents around a specific question or topic

## How to Build a Custom Report

<Steps>
<Step title="Start a New Custom Report">
Open the Custom Report builder from your dashboard and select the documents you want to analyze.
</Step>

<Step title="Define Your Analysis Prompt">
Write clear instructions describing what you want Qumis to analyze. Be specific about:
- What information to extract or analyze
- How to structure the output
- Any special considerations or focus areas
- The tone and format of the results
</Step>

<Step title="Select Documents">
Choose which documents to include in the analysis. You can upload multiple documents, and Qumis will analyze them as a collection.
</Step>

<Step title="Configure Output Sections">
Define how you want the results organized:
- Section headings
- Subsection structure
- Summary format
- Any custom data fields or tables
</Step>

<Step title="Generate the Report">
Run the analysis. Qumis processes your documents against your custom prompt and returns structured results.
</Step>

<Step title="Refine and Iterate">
Review the output. If needed, adjust your prompt or configuration and regenerate to optimize the results.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Customization Options

### Custom Prompts
Write your own analysis instructions that tell Qumis exactly what to look for and how to present findings. Your prompt becomes the core logic driving the report.

### Section Structure
Organize output into custom sections that match your internal processes or client requirements. Create hierarchical structures with headings, subheadings, and detailed sections.

### Output Formatting
Control how information is presented:
- Narrative summaries
- Bulleted lists
- Tables and matrices
- Executive summaries with detailed appendices
- Risk ratings or scoring systems

### Multiple Document Support
Include multiple documents in a single custom report. Qumis synthesizes analysis across all documents and presents findings in your chosen structure.

<Tip>
Once you've built a custom report that works well, save the prompt to your Prompt Library so you can reuse it. This creates consistent analysis outputs and reduces setup time for similar requests in the future.
</Tip>

## Tips for Effective Custom Reports

**Be Specific in Your Prompt**: The more detailed your instructions, the better the results. Include context about your audience and desired output format.

**Iterate on Results**: Review the first version and refine your prompt if the output needs adjustments. Small prompt refinements often significantly improve results.

**Save Successful Configurations**: Keep a library of prompts and settings that produce high-quality outputs for reuse with similar analysis requests.

**Define Success Criteria**: When setting up a custom report, clarify what "good" output looks like to guide your iterations.

## Limitations

**Complex Multi-Step Analysis**: Very complex analyses that require sequential reasoning across many document sections may benefit from breaking into multiple simpler reports rather than one complex custom report.

**Extremely Long Documents**: Analysis quality may decrease on documents exceeding 500+ pages. Consider splitting into multiple reports if needed.

**Highly Domain-Specific Terminology**: If your analysis requires industry-specific terms not commonly found in the Qumis knowledge base, include context and definitions in your prompt.
