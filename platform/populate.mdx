# Populate Spreadsheets and Templates

Populate turns insurance policies into structured data. Upload documents and tell Qumis what to extract — whether you need clean spreadsheet data for portfolio reviews or want to automatically fill your existing client templates. The result is consistent, accurate data you can use immediately in client presentations, renewals, compliance tracking, and management reporting.

---

## When to populate

Use Populate whenever you need to move data from documents into structured formats. Extract key information from policies for portfolio reviews and annual coverage summaries. Automate renewal management by pulling current policy details to pre-populate marketing submissions and renewal comparisons. Verify compliance across your vendor and tenant base by batch-extracting certificate data. Build management reports and executive dashboards by extracting premium, limits, and coverage data across your book of business. Rather than reading through policies manually or retyping data into spreadsheets, Populate handles the extraction automatically while maintaining accuracy and consistency.

---

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload your documents">
    Upload one or more insurance documents — policies, quotes, certificates, or schedules. Populate can process multiple documents in batch or individually, depending on your workflow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define what to extract">
    You can either use a standard extraction template for common scenarios (policy summary, certificate verification, quote comparison, claims data) or define custom extraction fields. Tell Qumis exactly what you need: policy numbers and effective dates, coverage types and limits, deductibles and self-insured retentions, named insureds and additional insureds, carrier information, endorsements, or any specialized fields your business requires.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map to your template (optional)">
    If you're using a client template, define how fields map from policy documents to your template structure. Train Qumis on where to find each piece of information, then save the configuration for reuse. This eliminates future manual data entry while keeping your team's standard formats intact.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and export">
    Qumis extracts the data and presents it in your chosen format — raw spreadsheet or populated template. Review the results, make any manual corrections needed, and export as Excel, PDF, or your preferred format for use in your workflows.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## What Qumis extracts

Populate can extract any data field from insurance documents. Standard extractions include named insureds and additional insureds, policy numbers and effective dates, coverage types, limits by coverage type, aggregate limits, deductibles and self-insured retentions, carrier information, retroactive dates, endorsements and special provisions, and premium amounts. For specialized needs, define custom extractions — for example, whether defense costs are inside or outside limits, specific sublimit information, or coverage-specific conditions. Qumis structures the output to match your specified fields and maintains consistency across batch processing.

---

## What you get

Two output options depending on your needs. Raw data extraction delivers clean, structured spreadsheet data organized by the fields you specified — ideal for analysis, reporting, and cross-document comparison. Populated client templates automatically fill your existing spreadsheet formats and deliverable structures with the extracted data, maintaining your firm's standard formats while eliminating manual data entry. You can review results before exporting, make corrections as needed, and save successful template configurations for future reuse across your team.
